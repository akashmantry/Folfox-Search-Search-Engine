<p><br />
<b>1863 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCLXIII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">starting on Thursday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> D) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>&#160;and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">common year starting on Tuesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> F) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1863rd year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 863rd year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd&#160;millennium</a>, the 63rd year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th&#160;century</a>, and the 4th year of the <a href="/wiki/1860s" title="1860s">1860s</a> decade. As of the start of 1863, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93March">January–March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1863&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>January – <a href="/wiki/John_Tyndall" title="John Tyndall">John Tyndall</a> first explains the workings of the <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_effect" title="Greenhouse effect">greenhouse effect</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Abraham_Lincoln" title="Abraham Lincoln">Abraham Lincoln</a> signs the <a href="/wiki/Emancipation_Proclamation" title="Emancipation Proclamation">Emancipation Proclamation</a> during the third year of the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, making the abolition of <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a> in the Confederate states an official war goal. It proclaims the freedom of 3.1 million of the nation's four million slaves, and immediately frees 50,000 of them, with the rest freed as Union armies advance.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>The first claim under the <a href="/wiki/Homestead_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Homestead Act">Homestead Act</a> is made, for a farm in <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">Nebraska</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_3" title="January 3">January 3</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/Harper%27s_Weekly" title="Harper's Weekly">Harper's Weekly</a></i> publishes <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Nast" title="Thomas Nast">Thomas Nast</a>'s first drawing of the modern <a href="/wiki/Santa_Claus" title="Santa Claus">Santa Claus</a> (although Santa existed previously).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_4" title="January 4">January 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/New_Apostolic_Church" title="New Apostolic Church">New Apostolic Church</a>, a Christian and <a href="/wiki/Chiliastic" class="mw-redirect" title="Chiliastic">chiliastic</a> church, established in <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>, Germany.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – In the <a href="/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland" title="Cantons of Switzerland">Swiss canton</a> <a href="/wiki/Ticino" title="Ticino">Ticino</a>, the village of <a href="/wiki/Bedretto" title="Bedretto">Bedretto</a> is partly destroyed, and 29 killed, by an <a href="/wiki/Avalanche" title="Avalanche">avalanche</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Resoconto_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Resoconto-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_8" title="January 8">January 8</a>
<ul>
<li>Ground is broken in <a href="/wiki/Sacramento,_California" title="Sacramento, California">Sacramento, California</a>, on the construction of the <a href="/wiki/First_Transcontinental_Railroad" title="First Transcontinental Railroad">First Transcontinental Railroad</a> in the United States.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire County Cricket Club</a> is founded at the <a href="/wiki/Adelphi_Hotel_(Sheffield)" title="Adelphi Hotel (Sheffield)">Adelphi Hotel</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sheffield" title="Sheffield">Sheffield</a>, England.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_10" title="January 10">January 10</a> – The first section of the <a href="/wiki/London_Underground" title="London Underground">London Underground</a> Railway (<a href="/wiki/Paddington_tube_station_(Circle_and_Hammersmith_%26_City_lines)" title="Paddington tube station (Circle and Hammersmith &amp; City lines)">Paddington</a> to <a href="/wiki/Farringdon_station" title="Farringdon station">Farringdon Street</a>) opens officially.<sup id="cite_ref-The_People.27s_Chronology_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_People.27s_Chronology-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_11" title="January 11">January 11</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Arkansas_Post" title="Battle of Arkansas Post">Battle of Arkansas Post</a>: General <a href="/wiki/John_McClernand" class="mw-redirect" title="John McClernand">John McClernand</a> and Admiral <a href="/wiki/David_Dixon_Porter" title="David Dixon Porter">David Dixon Porter</a> capture the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_River" title="Arkansas River">Arkansas River</a> for the Union.</li>
<li>In the Swiss Canton Ticino, the roof of the church of Sant'Antonio in <a href="/wiki/Locarno" title="Locarno">Locarno</a> collapses under the weight of snow, killing 47.<sup id="cite_ref-Resoconto_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Resoconto-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_15" title="January 15">January 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_intervention_in_Mexico" title="French intervention in Mexico">French intervention in Mexico</a> – French forces bombard <a href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" class="mw-redirect" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">Veracruz</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_21" title="January 21">January 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Adam_Opel" title="Adam Opel">Adam Opel</a> founds <a href="/wiki/Opel" title="Opel">Opel AG</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_22" title="January 22">January 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/January_Uprising" title="January Uprising">January Uprising</a> breaks out in Poland, <a href="/wiki/Lithuania" title="Lithuania">Lithuania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a>. The aim of the national movement is to liberate the <a href="/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Lithuanian%E2%80%93Ruthenian_Commonwealth" title="Polish–Lithuanian–Ruthenian Commonwealth">Polish–Lithuanian–Ruthenian Commonwealth</a> from Russian occupation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_29" title="January 29">January 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Indian_Wars" title="American Indian Wars">American Indian Wars</a>: The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army" title="United States Army">United States Army</a> led by General <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Edward_Connor" title="Patrick Edward Connor">Patrick Edward Connor</a> massacres Chief <a href="/wiki/Bear_Hunter" title="Bear Hunter">Bear Hunter</a> and forces of the <a href="/wiki/Shoshone" title="Shoshone">Shoshone</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bear_River_Massacre" title="Bear River Massacre">Bear River Massacre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Idaho_Territory" title="Idaho Territory">Idaho Territory</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_31" title="January 31">January 31</a> – <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne" title="Jules Verne">Jules Verne</a>'s first adventure novel, <i><a href="/wiki/Five_Weeks_in_a_Balloon" title="Five Weeks in a Balloon">Five Weeks in a Balloon</a></i> (<i>Cinq semaines en ballon</i>), is published in Paris.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – Radicals in Lithuania, Belarus, Latvia, northern Ukraine and western Russia join the <a href="/wiki/January_Uprising" title="January Uprising">January Uprising</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2" title="February 2">February 2</a> – January Uprising: Polish peasants are massacred by Russian hussars at Čysta Būda, near <a href="/wiki/Marijampol%C4%97" title="Marijampolė">Marijampolė</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_7" title="February 7">February 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/HMS_Orpheus_(1860)" title="HMS Orpheus (1860)">HMS&#160;<i>Orpheus</i></a> sinks attempting to enter <a href="/wiki/Manukau_Harbour" title="Manukau Harbour">Manukau Harbour</a> in New Zealand, with the loss of 189 lives.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_10" title="February 10">February 10</a>
<ul>
<li>The world-famous midgets <a href="/wiki/General_Tom_Thumb" title="General Tom Thumb">General Tom Thumb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lavinia_Warren" title="Lavinia Warren">Lavinia Warren</a> get married in New York City; <a href="/wiki/P._T._Barnum" title="P. T. Barnum">P. T. Barnum</a> takes an entrance fee.</li>
<li>Alanson Crane patents a <a href="/wiki/Fire_extinguisher" title="Fire extinguisher">fire extinguisher</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_16" title="February 16">February 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kansas_State_Agricultural_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Kansas State Agricultural College">Kansas State Agricultural College</a> is established as the first <a href="/wiki/Land-grant_university" title="Land-grant university">land grant college</a> created under the 1862 <a href="/wiki/Morrill_Land-Grant_Colleges_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="Morrill Land-Grant Colleges Act">Morrill Act</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a> – First meeting of the "Committee of the Five" in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, Switzerland, regarded as the foundation of the <a href="/wiki/International_Committee_of_the_Red_Cross" title="International Committee of the Red Cross">International Committee of the Red Cross</a>, following the lead of humanitarian businessman <a href="/wiki/Henry_Dunant" title="Henry Dunant">Henry Dunant</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_24" title="February 24">February 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Territory" title="Arizona Territory">Arizona</a> is organized as a <a href="/wiki/Political_divisions_of_the_United_States" title="Political divisions of the United States">United States territory</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_26" title="February 26">February 26</a> – Abraham Lincoln signs the <a href="/wiki/National_Banking_Act" class="mw-redirect" title="National Banking Act">National Banking Act</a> into law.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_2" title="March 2">March 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Clapham_Junction_railway_station" title="Clapham Junction railway station">Clapham Junction railway station</a> opens in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Idaho_Territory" title="Idaho Territory">Idaho Territory</a> is organized by the <a href="/wiki/Congress_of_the_United_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Congress of the United States">U.S. Congress</a>.</li>
<li>The U.S. <a href="/wiki/Enrollment_Act" title="Enrollment Act">National Conscription Act</a> is signed, leading to the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_draft_riots" title="New York City draft riots">New York City draft riots</a> in July.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_10" title="March 10">March 10</a> – Albert Edward, Prince of Wales (later <a href="/wiki/Edward_VII" title="Edward VII">Edward VII</a>) marries <a href="/wiki/Alexandra_of_Denmark" title="Alexandra of Denmark">Princess Alexandra of Denmark</a> (later Queen Alexandra).<sup id="cite_ref-Pocket_On_This_Day_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_On_This_Day-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_14" title="March 14">March 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> issues <a href="/wiki/Letters_Patent" class="mw-redirect" title="Letters Patent">Letters Patent</a> granting <a href="/wiki/Goulburn,_New_South_Wales" title="Goulburn, New South Wales">Goulburn, New South Wales</a>, city status, making it Australia's first inland city.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_19" title="March 19">March 19</a> – The <a href="/wiki/SS_Georgiana" title="SS Georgiana">SS&#160;<i>Georgiana</i></a> is destroyed on her maiden voyage while attempting to run the <a href="/wiki/Blockade" title="Blockade">blockade</a> into <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>. The wreck is discovered exactly 102 years later by <a href="/wiki/E._Lee_Spence" title="E. Lee Spence">E. Lee Spence</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_30" title="March 30">March 30</a> – 17-year-old Prince Wilhelm George of <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_Denmark" title="Monarchy of Denmark">Denmark</a> is elected by the <a href="/wiki/Hellenic_Parliament" title="Hellenic Parliament">Hellenic Parliament</a> as <a href="/wiki/George_I_of_Greece" title="George I of Greece">George, King of the Hellenes</a>; he will reign in Greece for fifty years. He arrives in <a href="/wiki/Athens" title="Athens">Athens</a> on <a href="/wiki/October_30" title="October 30">October 30</a> to take the throne.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1863&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_14" title="April 14">April 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Hu%E1%BA%BF_(1863)" title="Treaty of Huế (1863)">Treaty of Huế</a> is signed between <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a> and the <a href="/wiki/French_colonial_empire" title="French colonial empire">French Empire</a>.</li>

<ul>
<li><span lang="ar" xml:lang="ar"><i>Ayyam-i butun</i></span>: <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27u%27ll%C3%A1h" title="Bahá'u'lláh">Bahá'u'lláh</a> begins a 12-day stay in the <a href="/wiki/Garden_of_Ridv%C3%A1n,_Baghdad" title="Garden of Ridván, Baghdad">Najibiyyih gardens, Baghdad</a> (now known as the Garden of Ridván) during which he declares his station as "<a href="/wiki/He_whom_God_shall_make_manifest" title="He whom God shall make manifest">He whom God shall make manifest</a>". This date is celebrated in the <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%A1%27%C3%AD_Faith" title="Bahá'í Faith">Bahá'í Faith</a> as the festival of <a href="/wiki/Ridv%C3%A1n" title="Ridván">Ridván</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_Uprising" title="January Uprising">January Uprising</a>: The Polish peasant army, now led by Zygmunt Sierakowski, achieves its first victory over the Russian army, near <a href="/wiki/Raguva" title="Raguva">Raguva</a>.</li>

</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_30" title="April 30">April 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Camar%C3%B3n" title="Battle of Camarón">Battle of Camarón</a> in Mexico: 65 soldiers of the <a href="/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion" title="French Foreign Legion">French Foreign Legion</a> fight 2,000 Mexicans.</li>
</ul>

</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_14" title="May 14">May 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Jackson,_Mississippi" title="Battle of Jackson, Mississippi">Battle of Jackson, Mississippi</a>: Union General <a href="/wiki/Ulysses_S._Grant" title="Ulysses S. Grant">Ulysses S. Grant</a> defeats Confederate General <a href="/wiki/Joseph_E._Johnston" title="Joseph E. Johnston">Joseph E. Johnston</a>, opening the way for the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Vicksburg" title="Siege of Vicksburg">Siege of Vicksburg</a>.</li>

<li>Opening of <a href="/wiki/Salon_des_Refus%C3%A9s" title="Salon des Refusés">Salon des Refusés</a> in Paris draws attention to paintings by <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde" title="Avant-garde">avant-garde</a> artists, notably <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_Manet" title="Édouard Manet">Manet</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Le_D%C3%A9jeuner_sur_l%E2%80%99herbe" title="Le Déjeuner sur l’herbe">Le Déjeuner sur l’herbe</a></i>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_18" title="May 18">May 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Vicksburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Vicksburg">Siege of Vicksburg</a> begins (ends <a href="/wiki/July_4" title="July 4">July 4</a>, when 30,189 Confederate men surrender).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_21" title="May 21">May 21</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Port_Hudson" title="Siege of Port Hudson">Siege of Port Hudson</a>, Louisiana by Union forces begins.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/General_Conference_of_Seventh-day_Adventists" title="General Conference of Seventh-day Adventists">General Conference of Seventh-day Adventists</a> is formed in <a href="/wiki/Battle_Creek,_Michigan" title="Battle Creek, Michigan">Battle Creek, Michigan</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/May_28" title="May 28">May 28</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – The <a href="/wiki/54th_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="54th Massachusetts">54th Massachusetts</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/African-American" class="mw-redirect" title="African-American">African-American</a> regiment, leaves <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a> to fight for the Union.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_31" title="May 31">May 31</a> – The first <a href="/wiki/Prix_de_l%27Arc_de_Triomphe" title="Prix de l'Arc de Triomphe">Prix de l'Arc de Triomphe</a> horse race is held.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_7" title="June 7">June 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/French_intervention_in_Mexico" title="French intervention in Mexico">French intervention in Mexico</a> – French forces enter Mexico City.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_9" title="June 9">June 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Brandy_Station" title="Battle of Brandy Station">Battle of Brandy Station</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> ends inconclusively.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_12" title="June 12">June 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/The_Arts_Club" title="The Arts Club">The Arts Club</a> is founded by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dickens" title="Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frederic_Leighton" title="Frederic Leighton">Frederic Leighton</a> and others in Hanover Square, London.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/June_14" title="June 14">June 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Winchester" title="Second Battle of Winchester">Second Battle of Winchester</a>: A <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">Union</a> garrison is defeated by the <a href="/wiki/Army_of_Northern_Virginia" title="Army of Northern Virginia">Army of Northern Virginia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Shenandoah_Valley" title="Shenandoah Valley">Shenandoah Valley</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Winchester,_Virginia" title="Winchester, Virginia">Winchester, Virginia</a>.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1863&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: July–September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

</div>
<ul>
<li>July – The tiny <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate States of America</a> hand-propelled <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarine</a> <i><a href="/wiki/H._L._Hunley_(submarine)" title="H. L. Hunley (submarine)">H. L. Hunley</a></i> is first tested successfully.<sup id="cite_ref-Hunley_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hunley-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a> – Slavery is abolished in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Empire" title="Dutch Empire">Dutch colonies</a> of <a href="/wiki/Suriname" title="Suriname">Suriname</a> (independent from 1975) and <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao_and_Dependencies" title="Curaçao and Dependencies">Curaçao and Dependencies</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a>–<a href="/wiki/July_3" title="July 3">3</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a> – Union forces under <a href="/wiki/George_G._Meade" class="mw-redirect" title="George G. Meade">George G. Meade</a> turn back a Confederate invasion by <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Lee" title="Robert E. Lee">Robert E. Lee</a> in the largest battle of the war (28,000 Confederate casualties, 23,000 Union).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_4" title="July 4">July 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Vicksburg" title="Siege of Vicksburg">Siege of Vicksburg</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ulysses_S._Grant" title="Ulysses S. Grant">Ulysses S. Grant</a> and the Union army capture the Confederate city <a href="/wiki/Vicksburg,_Mississippi" title="Vicksburg, Mississippi">Vicksburg, Mississippi</a>, after the town surrenders. The siege lasts 47 days.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_6" title="July 6">July 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a> issues <a href="/wiki/Letters_Patent" class="mw-redirect" title="Letters Patent">Letters Patent</a> annexing to <a href="/wiki/South_Australia" title="South Australia">South Australia</a> the part of the colony of <a href="/wiki/New_South_Wales" title="New South Wales">New South Wales</a> that will later become the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Territory" title="Northern Territory">Northern Territory</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Port_Hudson" title="Siege of Port Hudson">Siege of Port Hudson</a> ends and the Union controls the entire Mississippi River for the first time.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_13" title="July 13">July 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_draft_riots" title="New York City draft riots">New York City draft riots</a>: In New York City, opponents of <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">conscription</a> begin 3 days of violent rioting, which will be regarded as the worst in the history of the United States with around 120 killed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_16" title="July 16">July 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Shimonoseki_Straits" title="Battle of Shimonoseki Straits">Battle of Shimonoseki Straits</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Screw_sloop" title="Screw sloop">screw sloop</a> <a href="/wiki/USS_Wyoming_(1859)" title="USS Wyoming (1859)">USS&#160;<i>Wyoming</i></a> engages with the <a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dsh%C5%AB_Domain" title="Chōshū Domain">Chōshū Domain</a> fleet before withdrawing, Japan's first naval engagement between elements of modern navies.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_17" title="July 17">July 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Wars" title="New Zealand Wars">New Zealand Wars</a> against the <a href="/wiki/M%C4%81ori_people" title="Māori people">Māori people</a> resume as British forces in New Zealand led by <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Cameron_(British_Army_officer)" title="Duncan Cameron (British Army officer)">Duncan Cameron</a> begin their <a href="/wiki/Invasion_of_the_Waikato" title="Invasion of the Waikato">Invasion of the Waikato</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_17" title="July 17">July 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Honey_Springs" title="Battle of Honey Springs">Battle of Honey Springs</a> was a Union strategic victory over the Confederates for control of <a href="/wiki/Indian_Territory" title="Indian Territory">Indian Territory</a> north of the <a href="/wiki/Arkansas_River" title="Arkansas River">Arkansas River</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_18" title="July 18">July 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: The first formal African American military unit, the <a href="/wiki/54th_Massachusetts_Volunteer_Infantry" class="mw-redirect" title="54th Massachusetts Volunteer Infantry">54th Massachusetts Volunteer Infantry</a>, unsuccessfully assaults Confederate-held <a href="/wiki/Fort_Wagner" title="Fort Wagner">Fort Wagner</a> but their valiant fighting still proves the worth of African American soldiers during the war. Their commander, Colonel <a href="/wiki/Robert_Gould_Shaw" title="Robert Gould Shaw">Robert Shaw</a> is shot leading the attack and was buried with his men (450 Union, 175 Confederate).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_26" title="July 26">July 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Morgan%27s_Raid" title="Morgan's Raid">Morgan's Raid</a>: At <a href="/wiki/Salineville,_Ohio" title="Salineville, Ohio">Salineville, Ohio</a>, Confederate cavalry leader <a href="/wiki/John_Hunt_Morgan" title="John Hunt Morgan">John Hunt Morgan</a> and 375 of his volunteers are captured by Union forces.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_30" title="July 30">July 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Indian_Wars" title="American Indian Wars">American Indian Wars</a>: Representatives of the United States and tribal leaders including <a href="/wiki/Chief_Pocatello" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief Pocatello">Chief Pocatello</a> (of the <a href="/wiki/Shoshone" title="Shoshone">Shoshone</a>) sign the <a href="/wiki/Box_Elder_Treaty" title="Box Elder Treaty">Treaty of Box Elder</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_3" title="August 3">August 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Otago_Boys%27_High_School" title="Otago Boys' High School">Otago Boys' High School</a> founded in New Zealand.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_8" title="August 8">August 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: Following his defeat in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Gettysburg" title="Battle of Gettysburg">Battle of Gettysburg</a>, General Robert E. Lee sends a letter of resignation to Confederate President <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Davis" title="Jefferson Davis">Jefferson Davis</a> (Davis refuses the request upon receipt).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_15" title="August 15">August 15</a>–<a href="/wiki/August_17" title="August 17">17</a> – <a href="/wiki/Bombardment_of_Kagoshima" title="Bombardment of Kagoshima">Bombardment of Kagoshima</a>: The British <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> bombards the town of <a href="/wiki/Kagoshima" title="Kagoshima">Kagoshima</a> in Japan in retribution after the <a href="/wiki/Namamugi_Incident" title="Namamugi Incident">Namamugi Incident</a> of 1862.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_16" title="August 16">August 16</a> – After Spain's annexation of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a>, rebels raise the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="Flag of the Dominican Republic">Dominican flag</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santiago_de_los_Caballeros" title="Santiago de los Caballeros">Santiago</a> to begin the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Restoration_War" title="Dominican Restoration War">Dominican Restoration War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_17" title="August 17">August 17</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: In <a href="/wiki/Charleston,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston, South Carolina">Charleston, South Carolina</a>, Union batteries and ships bombard Confederate-held <a href="/wiki/Fort_Sumter" title="Fort Sumter">Fort Sumter</a> (the bombardment does not end until <a href="/wiki/December_31" title="December 31">December 31</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_21" title="August 21">August 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lawrence" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Lawrence">Battle of Lawrence</a>: <a href="/wiki/Lawrence,_Kansas" title="Lawrence, Kansas">Lawrence, Kansas</a> is attacked by <a href="/wiki/William_Quantrill" title="William Quantrill">William Quantrill</a>'s raiders, who kill an estimated 200 men and boys. The raid becomes notorious in the North as one of the most vicious atrocities of the Civil War.</li>
<li>American clipper <i>Anglo Saxon</i> westbound is captured and burned by <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederate</a> <a href="/wiki/Privateer" title="Privateer">privateer</a> <i>Florida</i> off the south coast of Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>September – The <a href="/wiki/Western_Railroad_(North_Carolina)" title="Western Railroad (North Carolina)">Western Railroad</a> from <a href="/wiki/Fayetteville,_North_Carolina" title="Fayetteville, North Carolina">Fayetteville</a>, North Carolina to the coal fields of <a href="/wiki/Cumnock,_North_Carolina" title="Cumnock, North Carolina">Egypt</a>, North Carolina is completed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_6" title="September 6">September 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">Confederates</a> evacuate <a href="/wiki/Battery_Wagner" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery Wagner">Battery Wagner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Morris_Island" title="Morris Island">Morris Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">South Carolina</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_16" title="September 16">September 16</a> – <a href="/wiki/Robert_College" title="Robert College">Robert College</a> of <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>–<a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, the first American educational institution outside the United States, is founded by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Robert" title="Christopher Robert">Christopher Robert</a>, an American philanthropist.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_19" title="September 19">September 19</a>–<a href="/wiki/September_20" title="September 20">20</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: Confederate forces turn back a Union invasion of <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chickamauga" title="Battle of Chickamauga">Battle of Chickamauga</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_30" title="September 30">September 30</a> – <a href="/wiki/Georges_Bizet" title="Georges Bizet">Georges Bizet</a>'s opera <i><a href="/wiki/Les_p%C3%AAcheurs_de_perles" title="Les pêcheurs de perles">Les pêcheurs de perles</a></i> debuts at the <a href="/wiki/Th%C3%A9%C3%A2tre_Lyrique" title="Théâtre Lyrique">Théâtre Lyrique</a> in Paris.</li>
</ul>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_3" title="October 3">October 3</a> – US President Lincoln proclaims a national <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving_(United_States)" title="Thanksgiving (United States)">Thanksgiving</a> day to be celebrated the final Thursday in November in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_5" title="October 5">October 5</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_Bath_and_Coney_Island_Rail_Road" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, Bath and Coney Island Rail Road">Brooklyn, Bath and Coney Island Rail Road</a> starts operations in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Brooklyn, New York">Brooklyn, New York</a>; this is now the oldest <a href="/wiki/Right-of-way_(railroad)" class="mw-redirect" title="Right-of-way (railroad)">right-of-way</a> on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_City_Subway" title="New York City Subway">New York City Subway</a>, the largest <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">rapid transit</a> system in the United States and one of the largest in the world.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_14" title="October 14">October 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bristoe_Station" title="Battle of Bristoe Station">Battle of Bristoe Station</a> – Confederate General Robert E. Lee forces fail to drive the Union army out of <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_15" title="October 15">October 15</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>: The Confederate submarine <i><a href="/wiki/H._L._Hunley_(submarine)" title="H. L. Hunley (submarine)">H. L. Hunley</a></i> sinks during a test, killing <a href="/wiki/Horace_Lawson_Hunley" title="Horace Lawson Hunley">Horace Lawson Hunley</a> (its inventor) and a crew of seven.<sup id="cite_ref-Hunley_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hunley-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_23" title="October 23">October 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ffestiniog_Railway" title="Ffestiniog Railway">Ffestiniog Railway</a> in <a href="/wiki/North_Wales" title="North Wales">North Wales</a> introduces <a href="/wiki/Steam_locomotive" title="Steam locomotive">steam locomotives</a> into general service, the first time this has been done anywhere in the world on a public railway of such a <a href="/wiki/Narrow_gauge_railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Narrow gauge railway">narrow gauge</a> (2&#160;ft (60&#160;cm)).<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">The Football Association</a> is formed in London.<sup id="cite_ref-The_People.27s_Chronology_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_People.27s_Chronology-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_26" title="October 26">October 26</a>–<a href="/wiki/October_29" title="October 29">29</a> – The Resolutions of the Geneva International Conference are signed by sixteen countries meeting in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a> agreeing to form the <a href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="International Red Cross">International Red Cross</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_29" title="October 29">October 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Wauhatchie" title="Battle of Wauhatchie">Battle of Wauhatchie</a>: Forces under Union General Ulysses S. <li><a href="/wiki/November_23" title="November 23">November 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Chattanooga_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Battle of Chattanooga III">Battle of Chattanooga III</a> begins: Union forces led by General Ulysses S.
