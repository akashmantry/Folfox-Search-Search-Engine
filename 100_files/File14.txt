<p><br />
<b>1874 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCLXXIV</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">starting on Thursday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> D) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>&#160;and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Tuesday" title="Common year starting on Tuesday">common year starting on Tuesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> F) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1874th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 874th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd&#160;millennium</a>, the 74th year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th&#160;century</a>, and the 5th year of the <a href="/wiki/1870s" title="1870s">1870s</a> decade. As of the start of 1874, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1874&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January.E2.80.93March">January–March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1874&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January–March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>January – The <a href="/wiki/Pangkor_Treaty" class="mw-redirect" title="Pangkor Treaty">Pangkor Treaty</a> (also known as the Pangkor Engagement), by which the British extended their control over, first the Sultanate of <a href="/wiki/Perak" title="Perak">Perak</a> and later the other independent Malay States, is signed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a> – New York City annexes <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">The Bronx</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_2" title="January 2">January 2</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ignacio_Mar%C3%ADa_Gonz%C3%A1lez_(politician)" title="Ignacio María González (politician)">Ignacio María González</a> becomes head of state of the <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">Dominican Republic</a> for the first time.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_3" title="January 3">January 3</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Caspe" title="Battle of Caspe">Battle of Caspe</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): Campaigning on the Ebro in Aragon for the Spanish Republican Government, Colonel Eulogio Despujol surprised a Carlist force under Manuel Marco de Bello at Caspe, northeast of Alcañiz. In a brilliant action the Carlists were routed, losing 200 prisoners and 80 horses, while Despujol was promoted to Brigadier and became Conde de Caspe.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_23" title="January 23">January 23</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alfred,_Duke_of_Saxe-Coburg_and_Gotha" title="Alfred, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha">Prince Alfred, Duke of Edinburgh</a>, second son of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Victoria" title="Queen Victoria">Queen Victoria</a>, marries <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchess_Maria_Alexandrovna_of_Russia" title="Grand Duchess Maria Alexandrovna of Russia">Grand Duchess Maria Alexandrovna of Russia</a>, only daughter of Tsar <a href="/wiki/Alexander_III_of_Russia" title="Alexander III of Russia">Alexander III of Russia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Camille_Saint-Sa%C3%ABns" title="Camille Saint-Saëns">Camille Saint-Saëns</a>' composition <i><a href="/wiki/Danse_macabre_(Saint-Sa%C3%ABns)" title="Danse macabre (Saint-Saëns)">Danse macabre</a></i> receives its première.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_21" title="February 21">February 21</a> – The <i><a href="/wiki/Oakland_Daily_Tribune" class="mw-redirect" title="Oakland Daily Tribune">Oakland Daily Tribune</a></i> publishes its first issue.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_23" title="February 23">February 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Walter_Clopton_Wingfield" title="Walter Clopton Wingfield">Walter Clopton Wingfield</a> patents a game called "sphairistike" which is more commonly called <a href="/wiki/Lawn_tennis" class="mw-redirect" title="Lawn tennis">lawn tennis</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_24" title="February 24">February 24</a>-<a href="/wiki/February_25" title="February 25">February 25</a> –First Battle of Somorrostro (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): Determined to raise the siege of Bilbao by the Pretender Don Carlos VII, Republican commander Marshal Francisco Serrano sent General Domingo Moriones with a relief force of 14,000 men. Carlists, under General Nicolás Ollo, entrenched at Somorrostro outside Bilbao drive back a courageous assault by General Fernando Primo de Rivera and then the entire Republican army. The republicans lose 1,200 men, and Moriones loses his nerve demanding reinforcements and a replacement for himself. Moriones men entrenched and waited.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_14" title="March 14">March 14</a> – <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Castellfollit_de_la_Roca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Battle of Castellfollit de la Roca (page does not exist)">Battle of Castellfollit de la Roca</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): Appointed to command the Spanish Republican army in the north, General Ramón Nouvilas attempted to relieve the Carlist siege of Olot in Girona. But at Castellfollit de la Roca, in one of the Government’s worst defeats, Nouvilas was routed by Carlist General <a href="/w/index.php?title=Francesc_Savalls&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Francesc Savalls (page does not exist)">Francesc Savalls</a>, and captured along with about 2,000 of his men. Olot capitulated two days later.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_18" title="March 18">March 18</a>
<ul>
<li>Hawaii signs a treaty with the United States granting exclusive trading rights.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Dresden_English_Football_Club" title="Dresden English Football Club">Dresden English Football Club</a> is founded, first soccer club on European mainland.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_25" title="March 25">March 25</a>–<a href="/wiki/March_27" title="March 27">27</a> – <a href="/w/index.php?title=Second_Battle_of_Somorrostro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Second Battle of Somorrostro (page does not exist)">Second Battle of Somorrostro</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): In a renewed attempt to raise the siege of Bilbao by Don Carlos VII, Republican commander Marshal Francisco Serrano himself arrived with 27,000 men and 70 cannon. However, in three days of fierce fighting, the Carlist General Joaquín Elío, with just 17,000 men, once again drove off the attack at nearby Somorrostro, and it was another six weeks before Serrano managed to relieve Bilbao.</li>
<li>March – The Young Men's Hebrew Association in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a> (which still operates today as the <a href="/wiki/92nd_Street_Y" title="92nd Street Y">92nd Street Y</a>) is founded.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1874&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_15" title="April 15">April 15</a>–<a href="/wiki/May_15" title="May 15">May 15</a> – First exhibition of the group of young painters, <i>Société Anonyme Coopérative des Artistes, Peintres, Sculpteurs, Graveurs</i>, at the studio of the photographer <a href="/wiki/Nadar_(photographer)" title="Nadar (photographer)">Nadar</a> in Paris. <a href="/wiki/Louis_Leroy" title="Louis Leroy">Louis Leroy</a>'s critical review of it published on <a href="/wiki/25_April" class="mw-redirect" title="25 April">25 April</a> gives rise to the term <a href="/wiki/Impressionism" title="Impressionism">Impressionism</a> for the movement, with reference to <a href="/wiki/Claude_Monet" title="Claude Monet">Claude Monet</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Impression,_Sunrise" title="Impression, Sunrise">Impression, Sunrise</a></i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_2" title="May 2">May 2</a> – <a href="/w/index.php?title=Siege_of_Bilbao&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Siege of Bilbao (page does not exist)">Siege of Bilbao</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): The siege is lifted.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_9" title="May 9">May 9</a> – The first commercial horse-drawn carriage debuts in the city of <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Bombay</a>, plying two routes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_20" title="May 20">May 20</a> – <a href="/wiki/Levi_Strauss" title="Levi Strauss">Levi Strauss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacob_Davis_(inventor)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jacob Davis (inventor)">Jacob Davis</a> receive a U.S. <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patent</a> for blue <a href="/wiki/Jeans" title="Jeans">jeans</a> with copper rivets. The price is $13.50 per dozen.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_27" title="May 27">May 27</a> – The first group of <a href="/wiki/Dorsland_Trek" title="Dorsland Trek"><i>Dorsland Trekkers</i></a>, a series of expeditions by <i><a href="/wiki/Trekboer" title="Trekboer">Trekboere</a></i> in search of political independence and better farming conditions, departs South Africa to settle in <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Gert_Alberts" title="Gert Alberts">Gert Alberts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_14" title="June 14">June 14</a> – <a href="/wiki/Michel_Domingue" title="Michel Domingue">Michel Domingue</a> becomes head of state of <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">Haiti</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_22" title="June 22">June 22</a> – <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Taylor_Still" title="Andrew Taylor Still">Andrew Taylor Still</a> starts the movement for <a href="/wiki/Osteopathic_medicine_in_the_United_States" title="Osteopathic medicine in the United States">osteopathic medicine in the United States</a> at <a href="/wiki/Kirksville,_Missouri" title="Kirksville, Missouri">Kirksville, Missouri</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_25" title="June 25">June 25</a>–<a href="/wiki/June_27" title="June 27">June 27</a> – <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Monte_Muro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Battle of Monte Muro (page does not exist)">Battle of Monte Muro</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): <a href="/wiki/Carlism" title="Carlism">Carlist</a> forces entrenched around <a href="/wiki/Ab%C3%A1rzuza" title="Abárzuza">Abárzuza</a>, on the approach to <a href="/wiki/Estella-Lizarra" title="Estella-Lizarra">Estella</a> in <a href="/wiki/Navarre" title="Navarre">Navarre</a>, repel an attack by Isabelino/Liberal (supporters of Queen Isabella II) troops led by General <a href="/wiki/Manuel_Guti%C3%A9rrez_de_la_Concha" title="Manuel Gutiérrez de la Concha">Manuel Gutiérrez de la Concha</a>, Marqués del Duero, who is killed on the third day of fighting.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1874&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: July–September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_1" title="July 1">July 1</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Universal_Postal_Union" title="Universal Postal Union">Universal Postal Union</a> is established.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Zoo" title="Philadelphia Zoo">Philadelphia Zoo</a> opens, the first public zoo in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sholes_and_Glidden_typewriter" title="Sholes and Glidden typewriter">Sholes and Glidden typewriter</a>, with cylindrical platen and <a href="/wiki/QWERTY" title="QWERTY">QWERTY</a> keyboard, first marketed, in the United States.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Bank_of_Spain" title="Bank of Spain">Bank of Spain</a> emits the first <a href="/wiki/Spanish_peseta" title="Spanish peseta">peseta</a> banknotes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_14" title="July 14">July 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Fire_of_1874" title="Chicago Fire of 1874">Chicago Fire of 1874</a> burns down 47 acres of the city, destroying 812 buildings, killing 20, and resulting in the fire insurance industry demanding municipal reforms from Chicago's city council.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_23" title="July 23">July 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Aires_de_Ornelas_e_Vasconcelos" title="Aires de Ornelas e Vasconcelos">Aires de Ornelas e Vasconcelos</a> is appointed the Archbishop of the Portuguese colonial enclave of <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">Goa</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mathew_Evans" title="Mathew Evans">Mathew Evans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henry_Woodward_(inventor)" title="Henry Woodward (inventor)">Henry Woodward</a> patent the first <a href="/wiki/Incandescent_light_bulb" title="Incandescent light bulb">incandescent lamp</a> with an electric light bulb.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sack_of_Cuenca&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sack of Cuenca (page does not exist)">Sack of Cuenca</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): After Carlist forces successfully defended Estella, Don Alfonso de Bourbon, brother of the Don Carlos VII, led 14,000 Catalan Carlists south to attack Cuenca 136&#160;km from Madrid, held by Republicans under Don Hilario Lozano. After two days the outnumbered garrison capitulated but Don Alfonso permitted a terrible slaughter. The city is sacked. Subsequently another republican force defeats the disorderly Catalans who flee back to the Ebro.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_31" title="July 31">July 31</a> –July 31, 1874 - <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Francis_Healy" title="Patrick Francis Healy">Patrick Francis Healy</a>, S.J., first Black man to receive a PhD, inaugurated as president of Georgetown University, the oldest Catholic University in America, and became the first Black to head a predominantly white university</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_11" title="August 11">August 11</a> – <a href="/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Oteiza&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Battle of Oteiza (page does not exist)">Battle of Oteiza</a> (<a href="/wiki/Third_Carlist_War" title="Third Carlist War">Third Carlist War</a>): Two months after Government forces were repulsed from Carlist-held Estella, in Navarre, Republican General Domingo Moriones made a fresh diversionary attack a few miles to the southeast at Oteiza. In heavy fighting Moriones secured a costly tactical victory over Carlist General Torcuato Mendíri, but the war continued another 18 months before Estella finally fell.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_14" title="September 14">September 14</a> — <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Liberty_Place" title="Battle of Liberty Place">Battle of Liberty Place</a>: In <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">New Orleans</a>, former Confederate Army members of the <a href="/wiki/White_League" title="White League">White League</a> temporarily drive Republican Governor <a href="/wiki/William_P._Kellogg" class="mw-redirect" title="William P. Kellogg">William P. Kellogg</a> from office, replacing him with former Democratic Governor <a href="/wiki/John_McEnery_(Louisiana)" title="John McEnery (Louisiana)">John McEnery</a>. U.S. Army troops restore Kellogg to office five days later.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_28" title="September 28">September 28</a> — <a href="/wiki/Texas%E2%80%93Indian_wars" title="Texas–Indian wars">Texas–Indian wars</a>: U.S. Army Colonel <a href="/wiki/Ranald_S._Mackenzie" title="Ranald S. Mackenzie">Ranald S. Mackenzie</a> leads his force of 600 men on the successful raid of the last sanctuary of the <a href="/wiki/Kiowa_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiowa people">Kiowa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comanche_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Comanche people">Comanche</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheyenne_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheyenne people">Cheyenne</a> Indian tribes, a village inside the <a href="/wiki/Palo_Duro_Canyon" title="Palo Duro Canyon">Palo Duro Canyon</a> in Texas, and carries out their removal to the designated Indian reservations in <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October.E2.80.93December">October–December</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1874&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: October–December">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_9" title="October 9">October 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Bern" title="Treaty of Bern">Treaty of Bern</a> establishes the <a href="/wiki/General_Postal_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="General Postal Union">General Postal Union</a> to coordinate the exchange of international mail.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_19" title="October 19">October 19</a> – The modern <a href="/wiki/University_of_Zagreb" title="University of Zagreb">University of Zagreb</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Zagreb" title="Zagreb">Zagreb</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_2" title="November 2">November 2</a> – The first issue of <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese language</a> newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Yomiuri_Shinbun" class="mw-redirect" title="Yomiuri Shinbun">Yomiuri Shinbun</a></i> published in <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_4" title="November 4">November 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">Democrats</a> gain control of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> for the first time since 1860.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_7" title="November 7">November 7</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/Harper%27s_Weekly" title="Harper's Weekly">Harper's Weekly</a></i> publishes a <a href="/wiki/Cartoon" title="Cartoon">cartoon</a> by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Nast" title="Thomas Nast">Thomas Nast</a> which is the first use of an <a href="/wiki/Elephant" title="Elephant">elephant</a> as a symbol for the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_9" title="November 9">November 9</a> – New York Zoo hoax, a supposed breakout of animals from the <a href="/wiki/Central_Park_Zoo" title="Central Park Zoo">Central Park Zoo</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_10" title="November 10">November 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Ernst_Worrell_Keely" title="John Ernst Worrell Keely">John Ernst Worrell Keely</a> demonstrates his "induction resonance motion motor", a <a href="/wiki/Perpetual_motion_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Perpetual motion machine">perpetual motion machine</a> which eventually turns out to be a fraud.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_11" title="November 11">November 11</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Gamma_Phi_Beta" title="Gamma Phi Beta">Gamma Phi Beta</a> <a href="/wiki/Sorority" class="mw-redirect" title="Sorority">sorority</a> is founded at <a href="/wiki/Syracuse_University" title="Syracuse University">Syracuse University</a>. This is the first women's Greek letter organization to be called a <a href="/wiki/Sorority" class="mw-redirect" title="Sorority">sorority</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/November_25" title="November 25">November 25</a> – The <a href="/wiki/United_States_Greenback_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="United States Greenback Party">United States Greenback Party</a> is established as a <a href="/wiki/Political_party" title="Political party">political party</a>, made primarily of farmers financially hurt by the <a href="/wiki/Panic_of_1873" title="Panic of 1873">Panic of 1873</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_1" title="December 1">December 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> is granted a <a href="/wiki/Constitution" title="Constitution">constitution</a> and limited home rule from Denmark.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/December_29" title="December 29">December 29</a> – General Martínez and Brigadier General Luís Daban stage a <i>pronunciamento</i> at <a href="/wiki/Sagunto" title="Sagunto">Sagunto</a> and proclaim Isabel's son <a href="/wiki/Alfonso_XII" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfonso XII">Alfonso</a> as King of Spain. Subsequently the Madrid garrison follows suit and the <a href="/wiki/First_Spanish_Republic" title="First Spanish Republic">First Spanish Republic</a> comes to an end.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Date_unknown">Date unknown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1874&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Date unknown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Agra_Canal" title="Agra Canal">Agra Canal</a> opens in India.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/St._Nicholas%27_Church,_Hamburg" title="St. Nicholas' Church, Hamburg">St. Nicholas' Church, Hamburg</a>, designed by <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> <a href="/wiki/George_Gilbert_Scott" title="George Gilbert Scott">George Gilbert Scott</a>, is completed.
