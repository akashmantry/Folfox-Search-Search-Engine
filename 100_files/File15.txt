<b>1896 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MDCCCXCVI</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap year</a> <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Wednesday" title="Leap year starting on Wednesday">starting on Wednesday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> ED) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>&#160;and a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Monday" title="Leap year starting on Monday">leap year starting on Monday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> GF) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1896th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 896th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd&#160;millennium</a>, the 96th year of the <a href="/wiki/19th_century" title="19th century">19th&#160;century</a>, and the 7th year of the <a href="/wiki/1890s" title="1890s">1890s</a> decade. As of the start of 1896, the Gregorian calendar was 12 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<ul>
<li>January – <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Ashanti_wars#Fourth_Anglo-Ashanti_War" title="Anglo-Ashanti wars">Fourth Anglo-Ashanti War</a>: British <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">redcoats</a> enter the <a href="/wiki/Ashanti_people" title="Ashanti people">Ashanti</a> capital, <a href="/wiki/Kumasi" title="Kumasi">Kumasi</a>, and Asantehene Agyeman <a href="/wiki/Prempeh_I" title="Prempeh I">Prempeh I</a> is deposed.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_2" title="January 2">January 2</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Jameson_Raid" title="Jameson Raid">Jameson Raid</a> comes to an end, as Jameson surrenders to the <a href="/wiki/Boer" title="Boer">Boers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CBH_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_4" title="January 4">January 4</a> – <a href="/wiki/Utah" title="Utah">Utah</a> is admitted as the 45th <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">U.S. state</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_5" title="January 5">January 5</a> – An Austrian newspaper reports that <a href="/wiki/Wilhelm_R%C3%B6ntgen" title="Wilhelm Röntgen">Wilhelm Röntgen</a> has discovered a type of <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation" title="Electromagnetic radiation">radiation</a> later known as <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-rays</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_6" title="January 6">January 6</a> – <a href="/wiki/Cecil_Rhodes" title="Cecil Rhodes">Cecil Rhodes</a> is forced to resign as Prime Minister of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape of Good Hope</a> for his involvement in the <a href="/wiki/Jameson_Raid" title="Jameson Raid">Jameson Raid</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_7" title="January 7">January 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Fannie_Farmer" title="Fannie Farmer">Fannie Farmer</a> publishes her first cookbook.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_12" title="January 12">January 12</a> – H. L. Smith takes the first <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-ray</a> photograph.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_18" title="January 18">January 18</a> – The <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">X-ray</a> machine is exhibited for the first time.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_28" title="January 28">January 28</a> – Walter Arnold, of <a href="/wiki/East_Peckham" title="East Peckham">East Peckham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>, England, is fined 1 shilling for <a href="/wiki/Speeding" class="mw-redirect" title="Speeding">speeding</a> at 8&#160;mph (13&#160;km/h) (exceeding the contemporary <a href="/wiki/Speed_limit" title="Speed limit">speed limit</a> of 2&#160;mph (3.2&#160;km/h)), the first speeding fine.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Giacomo_Puccini" title="Giacomo Puccini">Puccini</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Opera" title="Opera">opera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/La_boh%C3%A8me" title="La bohème">La bohème</a></i> premieres in <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">Turin</a>, Italy.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_4" title="February 4">February 4</a> – International Association of Bridge and Structural Iron Workers is established in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_11" title="February 11">February 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Wilde" title="Oscar Wilde">Oscar Wilde</a>'s play <i><a href="/wiki/Salome_(play)" title="Salome (play)">Salomé</a></i> premieres in Paris.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_19" title="February 19">February 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Braamfontein_Explosion" title="Braamfontein Explosion">Braamfontein Explosion</a>: A train carrying 56 tons of <a href="/wiki/Dynamite" title="Dynamite">dynamite</a> explodes at <a href="/wiki/Braamfontein" title="Braamfontein">Braamfontein</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johannesburg" title="Johannesburg">Johannesburg</a>, killing more than 78 people.<sup id="cite_ref-Blumberg_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Blumberg-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_1" title="March 1">March 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Adwa" title="Battle of Adwa">Battle of Adwa</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a> defends its independence from Italy, ending the <a href="/wiki/First_Italo-Ethiopian_War" title="First Italo-Ethiopian War">First Italo-Ethiopian War</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a> – Publication begins of the world's first magazine with an orientation to male <a href="/wiki/Homosexuality" title="Homosexuality">homosexuality</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Eigene" title="Der Eigene">Der Eigene</a></i>, by <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Brand" title="Adolf Brand">Adolf Brand</a> in Berlin.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_9" title="March 9">March 9</a> – Responding to national outrage at the defeat at Adwa, Italian Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Francesco_Crispi" title="Francesco Crispi">Francesco Crispi</a> resigns.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_23" title="March 23">March 23</a> – The <a href="/wiki/New_York_State_Legislature" title="New York State Legislature">New York State Legislature</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Raines_law" title="Raines law">Raines law</a>, restricting Sunday alcoholic beverage sales to hotels.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April.E2.80.93June">April–June</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1896&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: April–June">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April" title="April">April</a> – The first study of <a href="/wiki/Svante_Arrhenius#Greenhouse_effect" title="Svante Arrhenius">the sensitivity of global climate to atmospheric carbon dioxide</a> is published. <a href="/wiki/Svante_Arrhenius" title="Svante Arrhenius">Svante Arrhenius</a> presents his findings in his paper, "On the Influence of Carbonic Acid in the Air upon the Temperature of the Ground", the <i>London, Edinburgh, and Dublin Philosophical Magazine and Journal of Science</i>, as an extract of a paper that had been presented to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Swedish_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences">Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences</a> on December 11, 1895.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_3" title="April 3">April 3</a> – The first edition of the Italian sports newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/La_Gazzetta_dello_Sport" title="La Gazzetta dello Sport">La Gazzetta dello Sport</a></i> is published.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_4" title="April 4">April 4</a> – The first known <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_basketball" title="Women's basketball">women's basketball</a> game between two colleges is played between <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford</a> and <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley" title="University of California, Berkeley">California</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_6" title="April 6">April 6</a> – The opening ceremonies of the <a href="/wiki/1896_Summer_Olympics" title="1896 Summer Olympics">1896 Summer Olympics</a>, the first modern <a href="/wiki/Olympic_Games" title="Olympic Games">Olympic Games</a>, are held in Athens.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_9" title="April 9">April 9</a> – The National Farm School (later <a href="/wiki/Delaware_Valley_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Delaware Valley College">Delaware Valley College</a>) is chartered in Doylestown, PA.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_8" title="May 8">May 8</a> – <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">Cricket</a>: Against <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Warwickshire County Cricket Club">Warwickshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire_County_Cricket_Club" title="Yorkshire County Cricket Club">Yorkshire</a> sets a still-standing <a href="/wiki/County_Championship" title="County Championship">County Championship</a> record when they accumulate an innings total of 887.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_13" title="May 13">May 13</a> – The Franchise Bill is passed by the <a href="/wiki/Colony_of_Natal" title="Colony of Natal">Colony of Natal</a>'s Legislative Assembly, disfranchising natives of other countries.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_18" title="May 18">May 18</a> – <i><a href="/wiki/Plessy_v._Ferguson" title="Plessy v. Ferguson">Plessy v. Ferguson</a></i>: The <a href="/wiki/U.S._Supreme_Court" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Supreme Court">U.S. Supreme Court</a> introduces the "<a href="/wiki/Separate_but_equal" title="Separate but equal">separate but equal</a>" doctrine and upholds <a href="/wiki/Racial_segregation" title="Racial segregation">racial segregation</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_26" title="May 26">May 26</a> – Eleven years after its foundation, a group of 12 purely industrial stocks were chosen to form the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Industrial_Average" title="Dow Jones Industrial Average">Dow Jones Industrial Average</a>. The index was composed entirely of industrial shares for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_27" title="May 27">May 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/1896_St._Louis%E2%80%93East_St._Louis_tornado" title="1896 St. Louis–East St. Louis tornado">St. Louis–East St. Louis tornado</a>: The costliest and third deadliest <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">tornado</a> in U.S. history levels a mile wide swath of downtown <a href="/wiki/St._Louis,_Missouri" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Louis, Missouri">St. Louis, Missouri</a>, incurring US$2.9 billion (1997 USD) in normalized damages, killing more than 255 and injuring over 1,000 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_4" title="June 4">June 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Ford_Quadricycle" title="Ford Quadricycle">Ford Quadricycle</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Ford" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford">Ford</a> vehicle ever developed, is completed, eventually leading <a href="/wiki/Henry_Ford" title="Henry Ford">Henry Ford</a> to build the empire that "put America on wheels".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_7" title="June 7">June 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mahdist_War" title="Mahdist War">Mahdist War</a>: British and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egyptian</a> victory at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ferkeh" title="Battle of Ferkeh">Battle of Ferkeh</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_12" title="June 12">June 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/J._T._Hearne" title="J. T. Hearne">J. T. Hearne</a> sets a record for the earliest date of taking 100 wickets in cricket (it is equalled by <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Parker_(cricketer)" title="Charlie Parker (cricketer)">Charlie Parker</a> in <a href="/wiki/1931" title="1931">1931</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/June_15" title="June 15">June 15</a> – The <a href="/wiki/1896_Sanriku_earthquake" title="1896 Sanriku earthquake">1896 Sanriku earthquake</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> in Japan kills 27,000.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July.E2.80.93September">July–September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1896&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: July–September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_9" title="July 9">July 9</a> – <a href="/wiki/William_Jennings_Bryan" title="William Jennings Bryan">William Jennings Bryan</a> delivers his <a href="/wiki/Cross_of_Gold_speech" title="Cross of Gold speech">Cross of Gold speech</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Convention" title="Democratic National Convention">Democratic National Convention</a>, which nominates him for President of the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – <a href="/wiki/Wilfrid_Laurier" title="Wilfrid Laurier">Wilfrid Laurier</a> becomes Canada's seventh <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Canada" title="Prime Minister of Canada">prime minister</a> and the first French-speaker to hold that office.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_21" title="July 21">July 21</a> – In Washington, D.C., in response to a "call to confer" issued by <a href="/wiki/Josephine_St._Pierre_Ruffin" title="Josephine St. Pierre Ruffin">Josephine St. Pierre Ruffin</a> to all women of color, the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Colored_Women%27s_Clubs" title="National Association of Colored Women's Clubs">National Association of Colored Women's Clubs</a> is organized.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_26" title="July 26">July 26</a> – <a href="/wiki/International_Socialist_Workers_and_Trade_Union_Congress,_London_1896" title="International Socialist Workers and Trade Union Congress, London 1896">International Socialist Workers and Trade Union Congress</a> opens in London.</li>
