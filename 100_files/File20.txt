<p><b>1933 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCMXXXIII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Common year starting on Sunday">starting on Sunday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> A) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, the 1933rd year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 933rd year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd&#160;millennium</a>, the 33rd year of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th&#160;century</a>, and the 4th year of the <a href="/wiki/1930s" title="1930s">1930s</a> decade.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_1" title="February 1">February 1</a> – <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a> gives his "Proclamation to the German People" in Berlin.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_2" title="February 2">February 2</a> – A second international conference on disarmament ends without results. It tries to limit the army sizes of the major powers, while Germany is entitled to 200,000; Germany leaves the conference because a plan postpones the limitations for 4 years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_5" title="February 5">February 5</a> – A <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">mutiny</a> starts on the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Netherlands_Navy" title="Royal Netherlands Navy">Royal Netherlands Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/Coastal_defence_ship" title="Coastal defence ship">coastal defence ship</a> <a href="/wiki/HNLMS_De_Zeven_Provinci%C3%ABn_(1909)" title="HNLMS De Zeven Provinciën (1909)"><i>De Zeven Provinciën</i></a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">Dutch East Indies</a>. After 6 days, it is bombed by a Dutch aircraft, killing 23 men, and the remaining mutineers surrender.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_6" title="February 6">February 6</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Twentieth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Twentieth Amendment to the United States Constitution">Twentieth Amendment to the United States Constitution</a> goes into effect.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_6" title="February 6">February 6</a>–<a href="/wiki/February_7" title="February 7">7</a> – Officers on the <a href="/wiki/USS_Ramapo_(AO-12)" title="USS Ramapo (AO-12)">USS <i>Ramapo</i></a> record a 34-meter high sea-wave in the Pacific Ocean.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/February_10" title="February 10">February 10</a> – The New York City-based Postal Telegraph Company introduces the first <a href="/wiki/Singing_telegram" title="Singing telegram">singing telegram</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_15" title="February 15">February 15</a> – In <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">Miami</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Zangara" title="Giuseppe Zangara">Giuseppe Zangara</a> attempts to assassinate President-elect <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>, but instead fatally wounds the <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Chicago" title="Mayor of Chicago">Mayor of Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anton_Cermak" title="Anton Cermak">Anton Cermak</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_17" title="February 17">February 17</a>
<ul>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/Newsweek" title="Newsweek">Newsweek</a></i> magazine is published for the first time in the United States.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Blaine_Act" title="Blaine Act">Blaine Act</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">United States Senate</a>, submitting the proposed <a href="/wiki/Twenty-first_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution" title="Twenty-first Amendment to the United States Constitution">Twenty-first Amendment to the Constitution</a> to the states for ratification. The amendment is ratified on December 5, ending <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_in_the_United_States" title="Prohibition in the United States">prohibition in the United States</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_27" title="February 27">February 27</a> – <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_fire" title="Reichstag fire">Reichstag fire</a>: Germany's parliament building in Berlin, the <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_building" title="Reichstag building">Reichstag building</a>, is set on fire under controversial circumstances.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_28" title="February 28">February 28</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_Fire_Decree" title="Reichstag Fire Decree">Reichstag Fire Decree</a> is passed in response to the Reichstag fire, nullifying many German civil liberties.</li>
</ul>

Roosevelt</a> declares a "<a href="/wiki/Emergency_Banking_Act" title="Emergency Banking Act">Bank holiday</a>", closing all United States banks and freezing all financial transactions (the 'holiday' ends on <a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">March 13</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/German_election,_1933" class="mw-redirect" title="German election, 1933">German election, 1933</a>: <a href="/wiki/Nazism" title="Nazism">National Socialists</a> gain 43.9% of the votes.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_6" title="March 6">March 6</a> – Mayor <a href="/wiki/Anton_Cermak" title="Anton Cermak">Anton Cermak</a> of Chicago dies of the wound he received on February 15.</li>

Roosevelt</a> declares a national emergency and issues <a href="/wiki/Executive_Order_6102" title="Executive Order 6102">Executive Order 6102</a>, making it illegal for U.S. citizens to own substantial amounts of monetary gold or <a href="/wiki/Bullion" title="Bullion">bullion</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/August_7" title="August 7">August 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Simele_massacre" title="Simele massacre">Simele massacre</a>: More than 3,000 <a href="/wiki/Assyrian_people" title="Assyrian people">Assyrian</a> Iraqis are killed by <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> government troops.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_12" title="August 12">August 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a> makes his first public speech warning of the dangers of German rearmament.<sup id="cite_ref-CBH_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBH-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_14" title="August 14">August 14</a> – Loggers cause a <a href="/wiki/Forest_fire" class="mw-redirect" title="Forest fire">forest fire</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Coast_Range" title="Oregon Coast Range">Coast Range</a> of <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">Oregon</a>, later known as the first forest fire of the <a href="/wiki/Tillamook_Burn" title="Tillamook Burn">Tillamook Burn</a>. It is extinguished on <a href="/wiki/September_5" title="September 5">September 5</a>, after destroying 240,000 acres (970&#160;km<sup>2</sup>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_25" title="August 25">August 25</a> – The <a href="/wiki/1933_Diexi_earthquake" title="1933 Diexi earthquake">Diexi earthquake</a> shakes <a href="/wiki/Mao_County" title="Mao County">Mao County</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sichuan" title="Sichuan">Sichuan</a>, China and kills 9,000 people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/August_30" title="August 30">August 30</a> – German Jewish philosopher <a href="/wiki/Theodor_Lessing" title="Theodor Lessing">Theodor Lessing</a> is assassinated in Marienbad (<a href="/wiki/Mari%C3%A1nsk%C3%A9_L%C3%A1zn%C4%9B" title="Mariánské Lázně">Mariánské Lázně</a>), <a href="/wiki/Czechoslovakia" title="Czechoslovakia">Czechoslovakia</a>, dying the following day.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="October">October</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1933&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: October">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/October_1933" title="October 1933">October 1933</a></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_1" title="October 1">October 1</a> – A failed assassination attempt against <a href="/wiki/Engelbert_Dollfuss" title="Engelbert Dollfuss">Engelbert Dollfuss</a>, leader of the <a href="/wiki/Fatherland%27s_Front" class="mw-redirect" title="Fatherland's Front">Fatherland's Front</a> in Austria, seriously injures him.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_7" title="October 7">October 7</a> – <a href="/wiki/Air_France" title="Air France">Air France</a> is formed by the merger of five French airline companies, beginning operations with 250 planes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_10" title="October 10">October 10</a> – <a href="/wiki/1933_United_Airlines_Boeing_247_mid-air_explosion" title="1933 United Airlines Boeing 247 mid-air explosion">1933 United Airlines Boeing 247 mid-air explosion</a>: A bomb destroys a <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines" title="United Airlines">United Airlines</a> <a href="/wiki/Boeing_247" title="Boeing 247">Boeing 247</a> on a transcontinental flight in mid-air near <a href="/wiki/Chesterton,_Indiana" title="Chesterton, Indiana">Chesterton, Indiana</a>, killing all 7 on board, in the first proven case of sabotage in civil aviation, although no suspect is ever identified.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_12" title="October 12">October 12</a> – The United States Army Disciplinary Barracks on <a href="/wiki/Alcatraz" class="mw-redirect" title="Alcatraz">Alcatraz</a> is acquired by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice" title="United States Department of Justice">United States Department of Justice</a>, which plans to incorporate the island into its <a href="/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Prisons" title="Federal Bureau of Prisons">Federal Bureau of Prisons</a> as a federal <a href="/wiki/Prison" title="Prison">penitentiary</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_13" title="October 13">October 13</a> – The <a href="/wiki/British_Interplanetary_Society" title="British Interplanetary Society">British Interplanetary Society</a> is founded.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_14" title="October 14">October 14</a> – Germany announces its withdrawal from the <a href="/wiki/League_of_Nations" title="League of Nations">League of Nations</a> and the World Disarmament Conference, after the U.S., the U.K. and France deny its request to increase its defense armaments under the Versailles Treaty.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_14" title="October 14">October 14</a>-<a href="/wiki/October_16" title="October 16">16</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Estonia#Second_Constitution_.281934.E2.80.931938.29" title="Constitution of Estonia">new constitution</a> of <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a> is approved only on the <a href="/wiki/Estonian_constitutional_referendum,_October_1933" title="Estonian constitutional referendum, October 1933">third consecutive referendum</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/October_17" title="October 17">October 17</a> – Scientist <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">Albert Einstein</a> arrives in the United States where he settles permanently as a refugee from <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> and takes up a position at the <a href="/wiki/Institute_for_Advanced_Study" title="Institute for Advanced Study">Institute for Advanced Study</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princeton,_New_Jersey" title="Princeton, New Jersey">Princeton, New Jersey</a>.</li>
</ul>
