<p>The <b>1951 Giro d'Italia</b> was the 34th&#160;edition of the <a href="/wiki/Giro_d%27Italia" title="Giro d'Italia">Giro d'Italia</a>, one of cycling's <a href="/wiki/Grand_Tour_(cycling)" title="Grand Tour (cycling)">Grand Tours</a>. The Giro started off in <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> on 19 May with a 202&#160;km (125.5&#160;mi) flat stage and concluded back in Milan with a 172&#160;km (106.9&#160;mi) relatively flat mass-start stage on 10 June. Fourteen teams entered the race, which was won by Italian <a href="/wiki/Fiorenzo_Magni" title="Fiorenzo Magni">Fiorenzo Magni</a> of the Ganna team. Second and third respectively were Belgian <a href="/wiki/Rik_Van_Steenbergen" title="Rik Van Steenbergen">Rik Van Steenbergen</a> and Swiss rider <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_K%C3%BCbler" title="Ferdinand Kübler">Ferdinand Kübler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1951_elm_final_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1951_elm_final-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LS_Final_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LS_Final-2">[2]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification_leadership">Classification leadership</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1951_Giro_d%27Italia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Classification leadership">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The leader of the <a href="/wiki/General_classification_in_the_Giro_d%27Italia" title="General classification in the Giro d'Italia">general classification</a> – calculated by adding the stage finish times of each rider – wore a pink jersey. This classification is the most important of the race, and its winner is considered as the winner of the Giro.<sup id="cite_ref-demystified_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-demystified-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two additional jerseys were in use. The green jersey was given to the best foreign cyclist in the general classification; it was won by Belgian <a href="/wiki/Rik_Van_Steenbergen" title="Rik Van Steenbergen">Rik Van Steenbergen</a>. The white jersey was given to the best cyclist riding with a licence for independents; this was won by <a href="/wiki/Arrigo_Padovan" class="mw-redirect" title="Arrigo Padovan">Arrigo Padovan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the mountains classification, the race organizers selected different mountains that the route crossed and awarded points to the riders who crossed them first.<sup id="cite_ref-demystified_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-demystified-6">[6]</a></sup> The winner of the team classification was determined by adding the finish times of the best three cyclists per team together and the team with the lowest total time was the winner.<sup id="cite_ref-BRI_1951_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BRI_1951-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IL_Finalsp2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IL_Finalsp2-8">[8]</a></sup> If a team had fewer than three riders finish, they were not eligible for the classification.<sup id="cite_ref-BRI_1951_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BRI_1951-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IL_Finalsp2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IL_Finalsp2-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
