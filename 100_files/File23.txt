<p>The <b>1970 <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a> season</b> was the 88th season for the franchise in Philadelphia. The Phillies finished in fifth place in the <a href="/wiki/National_League_East" title="National League East">National League East</a> with a record of 73–88, 15<span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>2</sub></span> games behind the <a href="/wiki/1970_Pittsburgh_Pirates_season" title="1970 Pittsburgh Pirates season">Pittsburgh Pirates</a>. The Phillies were playing their final season of home games at <a href="/wiki/Connie_Mack_Stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Connie Mack Stadium">Connie Mack Stadium</a>, before moving into their new facility, <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Stadium" title="Veterans Stadium">Veterans Stadium</a>, at the start of the following season.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Offseason">Offseason</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Philadelphia_Phillies_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Offseason">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>October 7, 1969: <a href="/wiki/Dick_Allen" title="Dick Allen">Dick Allen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Johnson_(baseball)" title="Jerry Johnson (baseball)">Jerry Johnson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cookie_Rojas" title="Cookie Rojas">Cookie Rojas</a> were traded by the Phillies to the <a href="/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals" title="St. Louis Cardinals">St. Louis Cardinals</a> for <a href="/wiki/Curt_Flood" title="Curt Flood">Curt Flood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Byron_Browne" title="Byron Browne">Byron Browne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Hoerner" title="Joe Hoerner">Joe Hoerner</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tim_McCarver" title="Tim McCarver">Tim McCarver</a>. Curt Flood refused to report to his new team. The Cardinals sent <a href="/wiki/Willie_Monta%C3%B1ez" title="Willie Montañez">Willie Montañez</a> to the Phillies on April 8, 1970, and Bob Browning (minors) to the Phillies on August 30 as compensation.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>November 17, 1969: <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Callison" title="Johnny Callison">Johnny Callison</a> and a player to be named later were traded by the Phillies to the <a href="/wiki/Chicago_Cubs" title="Chicago Cubs">Chicago Cubs</a> for <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Gamble" title="Oscar Gamble">Oscar Gamble</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dick_Selma" title="Dick Selma">Dick Selma</a>. The Phillies completed the deal by sending <a href="/wiki/Larry_Colton" title="Larry Colton">Larry Colton</a> to the Cubs in January 1970.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
<li>January 1970: <a href="/wiki/Rich_Barry" title="Rich Barry">Rich Barry</a> was purchased from the Phillies by the <a href="/wiki/Hawaii_Islanders" title="Hawaii Islanders">Hawaii Islanders</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Prior to 1970 season: <a href="/wiki/Doc_Edwards" title="Doc Edwards">Doc Edwards</a> was released by the Phillies.<sup id="cite_ref-doc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-doc-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Regular_season">Regular season</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1970_Philadelphia_Phillies_season&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Regular season">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In June 1970, a series of injuries left the Phillies short a catcher and they activated <a href="/wiki/Doc_Edwards" title="Doc Edwards">Doc Edwards</a>, who at the time was their bullpen coach. Edwards responded with two hits in his first game back and then caught a <a href="/wiki/Jim_Bunning" title="Jim Bunning">Jim Bunning</a>–<a href="/wiki/Dick_Selma" title="Dick Selma">Dick Selma</a> two-hitter.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
