<p><b>The 2003 <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia_Phillies" title="Philadelphia Phillies">Philadelphia Phillies</a> season</b> was the 121st season in the history of the franchise. The Phillies finished in third-place in the <a href="/wiki/National_League_East" title="National League East">National League East</a>, 15 games behind the <a href="/wiki/Atlanta_Braves" title="Atlanta Braves">Atlanta Braves</a>, and five games behind the <a href="/wiki/2003_World_Series" title="2003 World Series">2003 World Series</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Florida_Marlins" class="mw-redirect" title="Florida Marlins">Florida Marlins</a>, who were the NL's wild-card winner. The Phillies were managed by their former shortstop <a href="/wiki/Larry_Bowa" title="Larry Bowa">Larry Bowa</a>, as they played their final season of home games at <a href="/wiki/Veterans_Stadium" title="Veterans Stadium">Veterans Stadium</a>, before moving the club to <a href="/wiki/Citizens_Bank_Park" title="Citizens Bank Park">Citizens Bank Park</a> in 2004.</p>
<p>The Phillies missed the playoffs for the ninth straight season, tying a record set between 1984-92</p>
