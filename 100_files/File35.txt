<p>The <b>2009 <a href="/wiki/National_League_Championship_Series" title="National League Championship Series">National League Championship Series</a></b> (<b>NLCS</b>) was a best-of-seven baseball game series pitting the <a href="/wiki/2009_Los_Angeles_Dodgers_season" title="2009 Los Angeles Dodgers season">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> against the <a href="/wiki/2009_Philadelphia_Phillies_season" title="2009 Philadelphia Phillies season">Philadelphia Phillies</a> for the National League Championship and the right to represent the National League in the <a href="/wiki/2009_World_Series" title="2009 World Series">2009 World Series</a>. The Phillies defeated the Dodgers four games to one. Los Angeles, whose NL-best 95–67 record topped Philadelphia's 93–69 record, retained home-field advantage. The series, the 40th in league history, began on October 15 and finished on October 21. <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball_on_TBS" title="Major League Baseball on TBS">TBS</a> carried the championship on television.</p>
<p>The Phillies won the series, four games to one, advancing to the <a href="/wiki/World_Series" title="World Series">World Series</a> for the second consecutive year. They were, however, defeated by the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a>, 4–2.</p>
<p>This was the second consecutive NLCS between the Dodgers and Phillies and the fifth overall. The first two meetings were won by the Dodgers in <a href="/wiki/1977_National_League_Championship_Series" title="1977 National League Championship Series">1977</a> and <a href="/wiki/1978_National_League_Championship_Series" title="1978 National League Championship Series">1978</a>, and the third by the Phillies in <a href="/wiki/1983_National_League_Championship_Series" title="1983 National League Championship Series">1983</a>; none of the three resulted in a World Series Championship by either team. The Phillies defeated the Dodgers in five games in <a href="/wiki/2008_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2008 National League Championship Series">2008</a> <i>en route</i> to their <a href="/wiki/2008_World_Series" title="2008 World Series">2008 World Series title</a>. This match-up is the most frequent in the history of the NLCS (as of 2009) tied with the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh_Pirates" title="Pittsburgh Pirates">Pirates</a> vs <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Reds" title="Cincinnati Reds">Reds</a>.</p>
<p>In 2009, the Dodgers won the regular season series, four games to three, outscoring the Phillies 26–25.</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/James_Loney_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Loney (baseball)">James Loney</a> gave the Dodgers an early lead with a solo <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> in the second inning. Dodger <a href="/wiki/Starting_pitcher" title="Starting pitcher">starter</a> <a href="/wiki/Clayton_Kershaw" title="Clayton Kershaw">Clayton Kershaw</a> was solid through the first four innings, but the Phillies finally got to him in the fifth inning. The Phillies scored five runs that inning on a <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ruiz_(baseball)" title="Carlos Ruiz (baseball)">Carlos Ruiz</a> three-run home run, and later a <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Howard" title="Ryan Howard">Ryan Howard</a> two-<a href="/wiki/Run_batted_in" title="Run batted in">RBI</a> <a href="/wiki/Double_(baseball)" title="Double (baseball)">double</a>. The Dodgers made up most of the deficit in the bottom half of the fifth, when <a href="/wiki/Andre_Ethier" title="Andre Ethier">Andre Ethier</a> reached base on a <a href="/wiki/Chase_Utley" title="Chase Utley">Chase Utley</a> throwing <a href="/wiki/Error_(baseball)" title="Error (baseball)">error</a> that scored <a href="/wiki/Russell_Martin" title="Russell Martin">Russell Martin</a>, immediately followed by a <a href="/wiki/Manny_Ramirez" title="Manny Ramirez">Manny Ramirez</a> two-run home run. With the Phillies ahead by one run in the top of the eighth, Philadelphia outfielder <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Ib%C3%A1%C3%B1ez" class="mw-redirect" title="Raúl Ibáñez">Raúl Ibáñez</a> padded his team's lead with a three-run home run off <a href="/wiki/George_Sherrill" title="George Sherrill">George Sherrill</a>, his former teammate in <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Mariners" title="Seattle Mariners">Seattle</a>. The Dodgers scored two runs in the bottom half of the eighth on Martin's RBI single and a <a href="/wiki/Rafael_Furcal" title="Rafael Furcal">Rafael Furcal</a> <a href="/wiki/Sacrifice_fly" title="Sacrifice fly">sacrifice fly</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Madson" title="Ryan Madson">Ryan Madson</a> shut down the eighth-inning rally and <a href="/wiki/Brad_Lidge" title="Brad Lidge">Brad Lidge</a>, despite allowing a <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hit</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Base_on_balls" title="Base on balls">walk</a>, pitched a scoreless ninth to earn the <a href="/wiki/Save_(baseball)" title="Save (baseball)">save</a>.</p>

<p>Game 2 featured a pitching duel between <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Padilla" title="Vicente Padilla">Vicente Padilla</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Martinez" class="mw-redirect" title="Pedro Martinez">Pedro Martinez</a>; two pitchers who were not even on their respective teams' <a href="/wiki/Opening_Day" title="Opening Day">Opening Day</a> roster. Martinez pitched seven innings of scoreless ball and Padilla nearly matched him with <span class="nowrap"> </span><span class="frac nowrap">7<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>⁄<sub>3</sub></span> of one run ball. The only run came on a <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Howard" title="Ryan Howard">Ryan Howard</a> solo shot in the fourth. That <a href="/wiki/Run_batted_in" title="Run batted in">RBI</a> gave Howard six for the post-season which was tied for a record in the span of six post-season games. However, in the eighth the Phillies <a href="/wiki/Bullpen" title="Bullpen">bullpen</a> ran into trouble when <a href="/wiki/Casey_Blake" title="Casey Blake">Casey Blake</a> singled to start off the inning and <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Belliard" title="Ronnie Belliard">Ronnie Belliard</a> <a href="/wiki/Bunt_(baseball)" title="Bunt (baseball)">bunted</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Hit_(baseball)" title="Hit (baseball)">hit</a>. On a 3–2 pitch <a href="/wiki/Russell_Martin" title="Russell Martin">Russell Martin</a> grounded into a tailor-made double play to <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Feliz" title="Pedro Feliz">Pedro Feliz</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Chase_Utley" title="Chase Utley">Chase Utley</a> threw the ball away which allowed the Dodgers' first run of the game. It was Utley's second <a href="/wiki/Error_(baseball)" title="Error (baseball)">error</a> in two games; he had just three in 156 games during the 2009 regular season. Later in the inning, with the bases loaded, <a href="/wiki/Andre_Ethier" title="Andre Ethier">Andre Ethier</a> drew a walk which scored the go-ahead run to win the game for the Dodgers. Five relievers were used in the bottom of the eighth. <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Broxton" title="Jonathan Broxton">Jonathan Broxton</a> closed out the game and the Dodgers evened the series 1–1.</p>

<p>The Phillies once again jumped out to an early lead in the first inning when <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Howard" title="Ryan Howard">Ryan Howard</a> hit a two-run home run off Dodgers starter <a href="/wiki/Randy_Wolf" title="Randy Wolf">Randy Wolf</a>. As a result, Howard tied <a href="/wiki/Lou_Gehrig" title="Lou Gehrig">Lou Gehrig</a>'s record of most consecutive posteason games with an RBI, at eight. The Dodgers tied the game in the top of the fourth inning when Phillies starter <a href="/wiki/Joe_Blanton" title="Joe Blanton">Joe Blanton</a> allowed RBI singles to <a href="/wiki/James_Loney_(baseball)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Loney (baseball)">James Loney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russell_Martin" title="Russell Martin">Russell Martin</a>. The Dodgers took the lead in the fifth inning on a <a href="/wiki/Matt_Kemp" title="Matt Kemp">Matt Kemp</a> solo home run, and tacked on another run in the sixth on an RBI hit by <a href="/wiki/Casey_Blake" title="Casey Blake">Casey Blake</a> to make it 4–2. <a href="/wiki/Chase_Utley" title="Chase Utley">Chase Utley</a> answered in the bottom of the sixth with an RBI single of his own, cutting the Dodger lead to 4–3, which is where it would stand until the ninth inning. <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Broxton" title="Jonathan Broxton">Jonathan Broxton</a>, trying to work a four-out save for the Dodgers, retired <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Ib%C3%A1%C3%B1ez" class="mw-redirect" title="Raúl Ibáñez">Raúl Ibáñez</a> to start the ninth inning. He then walked <a href="/wiki/Matt_Stairs" title="Matt Stairs">Matt Stairs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hit_by_pitch" title="Hit by pitch">hit</a> <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Ruiz_(baseball)" title="Carlos Ruiz (baseball)">Carlos Ruiz</a>. After a <a href="/wiki/Greg_Dobbs" title="Greg Dobbs">Greg Dobbs</a> line out to third base, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Rollins" title="Jimmy Rollins">Jimmy Rollins</a> stepped to the plate with runners on the first and second base with two outs. On a 1–1 count, Rollins hit a line drive into the right-center field gap, easily scoring pinch runner <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bruntlett" title="Eric Bruntlett">Eric Bruntlett</a> and Ruiz, giving the Phillies a 5–4 walk-off win. It was later reported that Manny Ramirez hit the showers before the game was over.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<p>The Dodgers took an early lead in the first inning after Cole Hamels gave up a solo home run to Andre Ethier. The Phillies would come right back in the bottom of the first after Jayson Werth deposited a three-run home run into the right field seats. The Phillies did not look back after taking the lead on their way to clinching the NL pennant with a 10–4 victory over the Dodgers in Game 5.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
