<p>The <b>2014 Saint Jean sur Richelieu ramming attack</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Vehicular_assault_as_a_terrorist_tactic" class="mw-redirect" title="Vehicular assault as a terrorist tactic">terror car ramming</a> that occurred in <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a> on October 20, 2014. Two <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Armed_Forces" title="Canadian Armed Forces">Canadian Forces</a> members were hit by a <a href="/wiki/Lone_wolf_terrorist" class="mw-redirect" title="Lone wolf terrorist">lone wolf terrorist</a>, Martin Couture-Rouleau. <a href="/wiki/Warrant_officer" title="Warrant officer">Warrant officer</a> Patrice Vincent died from injuries, while another soldier was injured, but survived. The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">RCMP</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Canada" title="Government of Canada">Government of Canada</a> has characterized the homicide as a terrorist act by an <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">ISIL</a>-inspired terrorist.<sup id="cite_ref-speeches_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-speeches-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Attack_and_pursuit">Attack and pursuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu_ramming_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Attack and pursuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On October 20, 2014, Martin Couture-Rouleau <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_ramming_as_a_terrorism_tactic" class="mw-redirect" title="Vehicle ramming as a terrorism tactic">deliberately rammed</a> a car into a pair of <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Armed_Forces" title="Canadian Armed Forces">Canadian Armed Forces</a> soldiers in a shopping centre parking lot in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu" title="Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu">Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, at 11:30&#160;a.m. ET. He had been seen sitting in his car and watching for over two hours before the attack.<sup id="cite_ref-prevarrest_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prevarrest-2">[2]</a></sup> A police chase ensued. The <i><a href="/wiki/Toronto_Sun" title="Toronto Sun">Toronto Sun</a></i> reported that the suspect called <a href="/wiki/9-1-1" title="9-1-1">911</a> during the chase to say that he carried out his acts in the name of <a href="/wiki/Allah" title="Allah">Allah</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TSun_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The chase ended when Martin Couture-Rouleau lost control of his car, rolling it into a ditch and onto its roof. A witness said that a spike belt was deployed. According to witnesses, Couture-Rouleau exited the car and charged a female police officer with a knife.<sup id="cite_ref-TSun_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSun-3">[3]</a></sup> Police shot him up to seven times and he was pronounced dead that evening in hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-TSun_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSun-3">[3]</a></sup> A large knife was seen stuck in the ground according to eyewitnesses.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>There were two victims. <a href="/wiki/Warrant_officer" title="Warrant officer">Warrant officer</a> Patrice Vincent (53) died in hospital the next day while another unnamed soldier sustained non-life-threatening injures.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Guardian.2C_re:Rouleau_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Guardian.2C_re:Rouleau-5">[5]</a></sup> One of the soldiers was in uniform and one was not.<sup id="cite_ref-TSun_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSun-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>The attack was linked to terrorism by government and police officials including in a statement by Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Harper" title="Stephen Harper">Stephen Harper</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<p>Warrant officer <b>Patrice Vincent</b>, 53, had served in the military for 28 years and was considering retiring to become a cabinet maker.<sup id="cite_ref-Retirement_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Retirement-8">[8]</a></sup> He had previously served as a refrigeration and mechanical technician engineer as well as a military firefighter.<sup id="cite_ref-prevarrest_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prevarrest-2">[2]</a></sup> Vincent's funeral was held on November 1, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-Retirement_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Retirement-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>A second Canadian Forces member was injured in the attack.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Perpetrator">Perpetrator</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu_ramming_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Perpetrator">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Martin Couture-Rouleau</b> (died October 20, 2014, age 25) was a francophone Canadian living with his parents in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu" title="Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu">Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu</a> at the time of the attack. He was separated from his partner and their young child. Couture-Rouleau had converted to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a> in the previous year, and was praying at a mosque "regularly."<sup id="cite_ref-Austen_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Austen-10">[10]</a></sup> He had owned a small power-washing business that failed.<sup id="cite_ref-Bruemmer_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bruemmer-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>Legal documents show Couture-Rouleau became a <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> <a href="/wiki/Religious_conversion" title="Religious conversion">convert</a> in 2013 and started to refer to himself as Ahmad LeConverti (Ahmad the Converted).<sup id="cite_ref-prevarrest_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prevarrest-2">[2]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> activity and a <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Broadcasting_Corporation" title="Canadian Broadcasting Corporation">CBC</a> interview show that he had become a supporter of <a href="/wiki/Islamic_State_of_Iraq_and_the_Levant" title="Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant">ISIL</a>. He posted images and links that were <a href="/wiki/Anti-Americanism" title="Anti-Americanism">anti-American foreign policy</a>, and links to <a href="/wiki/Antisemitism" title="Antisemitism">anti-Semitic</a> <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> videos. <a href="/wiki/Global_Television_Network" title="Global Television Network">Global</a> reported Facebook posts in both English and French about Allah and graphic posters with references to Islam’s superiority over <a href="/wiki/Christianity" title="Christianity">Christianity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Global_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global-12">[12]</a></sup> A Facebook friend who corresponded with Couture-Rouleau said, "So he was really mad that Canada actually supported the American bombing of [ISIS] in Syria and Iraq so I think that was the main motive in killing that Canadian soldier."<sup id="cite_ref-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Another told Radio-Canada that Couture-Rouleau spent hours on the internet and devoured <a href="/wiki/Jihadism" title="Jihadism">jihadist</a> literature, and that Couture-Rouleau dreamed of dying as a <a href="/wiki/Martyr" title="Martyr">martyr</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>According to information Radio-Canada obtained, he had convinced at least four or five people in his circle of friends to convert to Islam, but the friend said that Couture-Rouleau got carried away with an extreme interpretation of the <a href="/wiki/Quran" title="Quran">Qur'an</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau-13">[13]</a></sup> A friend said he was obsessed with practicing his new religion and trying to convert his friends.<sup id="cite_ref-Global_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Canadian_Mounted_Police" title="Royal Canadian Mounted Police">RCMP</a> became aware of Couture-Rouleau after a relative alerted police to the man’s terrorist leanings.<sup id="cite_ref-TSun_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSun-3">[3]</a></sup> He wanted to travel to Iraq to fight with ISIL the summer before he perpetrated the attack in Quebec.<sup id="cite_ref-Bruemmer_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bruemmer-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ForeignFighter_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ForeignFighter-15">[15]</a></sup> His Canadian passport was revoked in June 2014 over concerns that he had "become radicalized after converting to Islam."<sup id="cite_ref-prevarrest_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prevarrest-2">[2]</a></sup> RCMP Commissioner <a href="/wiki/Bob_Paulson" title="Bob Paulson">Bob Paulson</a> confirmed he was one of 90 Canadians under surveillance<sup id="cite_ref-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Who_is_Martin_Couture-Rouleau-13">[13]</a></sup> by the RCMP because they were suspected of wanting to join terrorist organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responses">Responses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu_ramming_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Responses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Conservative government immediately labelled the act as a terrorist attack. According to <a href="/wiki/Steven_Blaney" title="Steven Blaney">Steven Blaney</a>, Canadian <a href="/wiki/Minister_of_Public_Safety" class="mw-redirect" title="Minister of Public Safety">Minister of Public Safety</a>, the attack was "clearly linked to terrorist ideology."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The Prime Minister's office released a statement that said in part "The individual who struck the two CAF members with his car is known to federal authorities, including the <a href="/wiki/Integrated_National_Security_Enforcement_Teams" title="Integrated National Security Enforcement Teams">Integrated National Security Enforcement Teams</a> (the Integrated National Security Enforcement Teams are an anti-terrorism police unit). Federal authorities have confirmed that there are clear indications that the individual had become radicalized."<sup id="cite_ref-Global_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2014_shootings_at_Parliament_Hill,_Ottawa" title="2014 shootings at Parliament Hill, Ottawa">2014 shootings at Parliament Hill, Ottawa</a>, Prime Minister Stephen Harper gave a nationally televised address in which he said "Warrant Officer Patrice Vincent... was killed earlier this week by an ISIL-inspired terrorist" and "this week's events are a grim reminder that Canada is not immune to the types of terrorist attacks we have seen elsewhere around the world. We are also reminded that attacks on our security personnel and on our institutions of governance are by their very nature, attacks on our country, on our values, on our society, on us Canadians as a free and democratic people who embrace human dignity for all."<sup id="cite_ref-speeches_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-speeches-1">[1]</a></sup> Conservative MP <a href="/wiki/Erin_O%27Toole" title="Erin O'Toole">Erin O'Toole</a> said on <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Play_(Canadian_political_affairs_series)" title="Power Play (Canadian political affairs series)">Power Play</a></i> that he stands behind the terrorist act label, both in the case of Zehaf-Bibeau and Martin Couture-Rouleau. "Warrant Officer Vincent and Nathan Cirillo weren’t attacked for who they were, they were attacked for what their uniform represented, so inherently that’s terrorism."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Rouleau's next-door neighbour said, "Mr. Rouleau’s father, Gilles, as a peaceful man who tried to help his troubled son. She said Mr. Rouleau had been “brainwashed” by online radicals. "I don’t think it’s a terrorist act," she said. "He needed help. We could see that he needed psychiatric help."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> "To convert to a new religion and to adopt such violent, radical ideology suggests that he was influenced by others, in person or online," said Wagdy Loza, head of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Psychological_Association" title="Canadian Psychological Association">Canadian Psychological Association</a>'s extremism and terrorism section.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an article on the <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Beast" title="The Daily Beast">The Daily Beast</a></i> website, Jacob Siegel equated both Couture-Rouleau and Zehaf-Bibeau, "the killers who brought terror to Canada this week", with the <a href="/wiki/2014_Queens_hatchet_attack" title="2014 Queens hatchet attack">2014 New York City hatchet attacker</a>, saying "like the killers who brought terror to Canada this week, the New York attacker is being called a "lone wolf", meaning he acted essentially on his own, [Couture-Rouleau and Zehab-Bibeau] seem custom made for the stray dog profile, and the kind of terrorist the West could be seeing a lot more of in the future". He cites a paper by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Michael_Jenkins" title="Brian Michael Jenkins">Brian Michael Jenkins</a> of the <a href="/wiki/RAND_Corporation" title="RAND Corporation">RAND Corporation</a>. Jenkins described them as "stray dogs," rather than <a href="/wiki/Lone_wolf_(terrorism)" title="Lone wolf (terrorism)">lone wolves</a>, characterizing them as "misfits" who are "who are moved from seething anger to spontaneous deadly action" by exposure to <a href="/wiki/Islamism" title="Islamism">Islamist</a> propaganda.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_reactions">Media reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu_ramming_attack&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Media reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Commentators have cited the attacks against Canadian soldiers in Quebec and Ottawa while arguing against Canadian Government changes to anti-terrorism legislation, although the legislation was prepared long before the attacks occurred and is not in response specifically to the two attacks.</p>
<p>James Baxter, editor-in-chief of ipolitics.ca, an Ottawa political <a href="/wiki/Zine" title="Zine">zine</a>, said "the government is exploiting two isolated murders to push through sweeping new surveillance powers" and pointed out "the would-be 'terrorists' involved were armed only with a car and a vintage hunting rifle".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>In an opinion piece, <a href="/wiki/Linda_McQuaig" title="Linda McQuaig">Linda McQuaig</a>, said "lots of hype about Canada and our institutions being under attack — even as it got harder to explain the difference between the 'terrorist' murders of two soldiers and <a href="/wiki/2014_Moncton_shootings" title="2014 Moncton shootings">the 'non-terrorist' murders of three RCMP officers in Moncton</a>. The main difference appeared to be that the shooter in Moncton was not a follower of Islam".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize" title="Pulitzer Prize">Pulitzer Prize</a>-winning American journalist <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Greenwald" title="Glenn Greenwald">Glenn Greenwald</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Intercept" title="The Intercept">The Intercept</a>,</i> referring to this case and the Parliament Hill shooting, "The statements of those accused by the west of terrorism, and even the Pentagon’s own commissioned research, have made conclusively clear what motivates these acts: namely, anger over the violence, abuse and interference by Western countries in that part of the world, with the world’s Muslims overwhelmingly the targets and victims. The very policies of militarism and civil liberties erosions justified in the name of stopping terrorism are actually what fuels terrorism and ensures its endless continuation."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Andre Picard, in an opinion piece for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Globe_and_Mail" title="The Globe and Mail">The Globe and Mail</a></i>, acknowledged the Couture-Rouleau and Zehaf-Bibeau were both attracted to radical elements in Islam, and dismissed arguments that mental illness caused their actions.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Vincent and Nathan Cirillo, the victim in the Ottawa shootings, were jointly named the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Newsmaker_of_the_Year" title="Canadian Newsmaker of the Year">Canadian Newsmakers of the Year</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Press" class="mw-redirect" title="Canadian Press">Canadian Press</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup></p>
