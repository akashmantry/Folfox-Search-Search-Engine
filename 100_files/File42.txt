<p><b>Aaron Josef Hernandez</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[A]</a></sup> (November 6, 1989 – April 19, 2017)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[4]</a></sup> was an <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">American football</a> <a href="/wiki/Tight_end" title="Tight end">tight end</a> and convicted <a href="/wiki/Murderer" class="mw-redirect" title="Murderer">murderer</a>. Hernandez played <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Florida_Gators_football" title="Florida Gators football">University of Florida</a>, where he was a member of a <a href="/wiki/BCS_National_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="BCS National Championship">BCS National Championship</a> team and was recognized as an <a href="/wiki/All-American" class="mw-redirect" title="All-American">All-American</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[5]</a></sup> He was drafted by <a href="/wiki/National_Football_League" title="National Football League">NFL</a>'s <a href="/wiki/New_England_Patriots" title="New England Patriots">New England Patriots</a> as the 15th pick in the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_NFL_Draft" title="2010 NFL Draft">2010 NFL Draft</a>. Hernandez was <a href="/wiki/Dismissal_(employment)#High-profile_firings" title="Dismissal (employment)">released</a> by the Patriots in June 2013 immediately after his arrest for the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Odin_Lloyd" title="Murder of Odin Lloyd">murder of Odin Lloyd</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RTJAN17_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RTJAN17-7">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 22, 2013, Hernandez was indicted by a <a href="/wiki/Grand_jury" title="Grand jury">grand jury</a> for the June 2013 murder of Lloyd, who was a semi-professional football player.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">[7]</a></sup> He was found guilty of first-degree murder in the Lloyd case, and sentenced to serve <a href="/wiki/Life_imprisonment_in_the_United_States" title="Life imprisonment in the United States">life in prison</a> without the possibility of <a href="/wiki/Parole#United_States" title="Parole">parole</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Souza-Baranowski_Correctional_Center" title="Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center">Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[8]</a></sup> He was also <a href="/wiki/Indictment#United_States" title="Indictment">indicted</a> for the 2012 double <a href="/wiki/Homicide" title="Homicide">homicide</a> of Daniel de Abreu and Safiro Furtado,<sup id="cite_ref-RTJAN17_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RTJAN17-7">[6]</a></sup> but was found not guilty.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[9]</a></sup> On April 19, 2017, Hernandez committed <a href="/wiki/Suicide_by_hanging" title="Suicide by hanging">suicide by hanging</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-globe-2017-04-19_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-2017-04-19-11">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[12]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span></h2>
<p>Aaron Josef Hernandez was born in <a href="/wiki/Bristol,_Connecticut" title="Bristol, Connecticut">Bristol, Connecticut</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-pfrprofile_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pfrprofile-14">[13]</a></sup> the son of Dennis Hernandez, who was of <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Ricans" title="Puerto Ricans">Puerto Rican</a> descent, and Terri Valentine-Hernandez, who is of <a href="/wiki/Italians" title="Italians">Italian</a> descent. Dennis died from complications from <a href="/wiki/Hernia" title="Hernia">hernia</a> surgery in January 2006, when Hernandez was 16.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[14]</a></sup> According to Hernandez's mother, his father's death greatly affected him, leading him to rebel against authority figures.<sup id="cite_ref-kelly_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kelly-16">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hernandez attended <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Central_High_School" title="Bristol Central High School">Bristol Central High School</a> and played for the Rams as a <a href="/wiki/Wide_receiver" title="Wide receiver">wide receiver</a> until becoming a <a href="/wiki/Tight_end" title="Tight end">tight end</a>, and also played <a href="/wiki/Defensive_end" title="Defensive end">defensive end</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ufprofile_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufprofile-18">[17]</a></sup> As a senior, he was Connecticut's <a href="/wiki/Gatorade_Player_of_the_Year_awards" title="Gatorade Player of the Year awards">Gatorade Football Player of the Year</a> after making 67 <a href="/wiki/Reception_(American_football)" class="mw-redirect" title="Reception (American football)">receptions</a> for 1,807 yards and 24 <a href="/wiki/Touchdown" title="Touchdown">touchdowns</a> on offense and 72 <a href="/wiki/Tackle_(football_move)" title="Tackle (football move)">tackles</a>, 12 <a href="/wiki/Quarterback_sack" title="Quarterback sack">sacks</a>, three forced <a href="/wiki/Fumble" title="Fumble">fumbles</a>, two fumble recoveries, and four blocked kicks on defense.<sup id="cite_ref-ufprofile_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufprofile-18">[17]</a></sup> The 1,807 receiving yards and 24 touchdowns were state records, and his 31 career touchdowns tied the state record. He also set the state record for receiving yards in a single game with 376, the seventh-best in national high school history, and set a national high school record for yards receiving per game with 180.7. Hernandez was considered the top tight-end recruit in 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Scout.com" title="Scout.com">Scout.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="College_career">College career</span></h2>
<p>At first Hernandez committed to play at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Connecticut" title="University of Connecticut">University of Connecticut</a><sup id="cite_ref-kelly_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kelly-16">[15]</a></sup> with his brother D.J., but ultimately chose to play for the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a> under coach <a href="/wiki/Urban_Meyer" title="Urban Meyer">Urban Meyer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ufmediaguide_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufmediaguide-20">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a freshman in <a href="/wiki/2007_Florida_Gators_football_team" title="2007 Florida Gators football team">2007</a>, Hernandez started three games for the Florida Gators. He finished the season with nine receptions for 151 yards and two touchdowns. As a sophomore in <a href="/wiki/2008_Florida_Gators_football_team" title="2008 Florida Gators football team">2008</a>, he started 11 of 13 games in place of the injured <a href="/wiki/Cornelius_Ingram" title="Cornelius Ingram">Cornelius Ingram</a>, and finished the season with 34 receptions for 381 yards and five touchdowns. In the <a href="/wiki/2009_BCS_National_Championship_Game" title="2009 BCS National Championship Game">2009 BCS National Championship Game</a> against the <a href="/wiki/2008_Oklahoma_Sooners_football_team" title="2008 Oklahoma Sooners football team">Oklahoma Sooners</a>, Hernandez led the Gators in receiving yards with 57 on five receptions, as the Gators defeated the Sooners 24–14 to win their second BCS championship in three seasons.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[20]</a></sup></p>
<p>As a junior in <a href="/wiki/2009_Florida_Gators_football_team" title="2009 Florida Gators football team">2009</a>, Hernandez won the <a href="/wiki/John_Mackey_Award" title="John Mackey Award">John Mackey Award</a>, given annually to the nation's best tight-end, after leading the team in receptions with 68 for 850 yards and five touchdowns. He was also a first-team All-<a href="/wiki/Southeastern_Conference" title="Southeastern Conference">Southeastern Conference</a> selection and was recognized as a first-team <a href="/wiki/2009_College_Football_All-America_Team" title="2009 College Football All-America Team">All-American</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/College_Football_News" title="College Football News">College Football News</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sporting_News" class="mw-redirect" title="The Sporting News">The Sporting News</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-ufmediaguide_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ufmediaguide-20">[19]</a></sup> Hernandez finished his college career with 111 receptions for 1,382 yards and 12 touchdowns.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[21]</a></sup></p>
<p>After his junior year, he decided to forgo his senior season and enter the <a href="/wiki/2010_NFL_Draft" title="2010 NFL Draft">2010 NFL Draft</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[22]</a></sup></p>

<p>Hernandez was drafted by the <a href="/wiki/New_England_Patriots" title="New England Patriots">New England Patriots</a> in the fourth round (113th overall) of the <a href="/wiki/2010_NFL_Draft" title="2010 NFL Draft">2010 NFL Draft</a>. The previous day, the Patriots drafted another tight end, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Gronkowski" title="Rob Gronkowski">Rob Gronkowski</a>. Shortly after Hernandez was drafted, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_Globe" title="The Boston Globe">The Boston Globe</a></i> reported from multiple sources that he had admitted to <a href="/wiki/Marijuana" class="mw-redirect" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a> use and had failed multiple drug tests while in college, causing his draft stock to drop.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[24]</a></sup> Later that day, the Patriots released a statement from Hernandez, who said he had failed only one drug test while in college and was candid about it to interested teams at the <a href="/wiki/NFL_Scouting_Combine" title="NFL Scouting Combine">NFL Scouting Combine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hernandez signed a four-year contract on June 8, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[26]</a></sup> The contract included a signing bonus of $200,000, less than half the bonus received by Patriots fourth-round pick placekicker <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Gostkowski" title="Stephen Gostkowski">Stephen Gostkowski</a> in <a href="/wiki/2006_New_England_Patriots_season" title="2006 New England Patriots season">2006</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[27]</a></sup> To compensate for the smaller signing bonus, the deal also included a series of roster and workout bonuses up to an additional $700,000, which meant Hernandez could wind up getting the money a third-rounder would over four years, but would have to "walk the straight and narrow line to do so".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[28]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010_season">2010 season</span></h3>
<p>Hernandez started the 2010 season as the youngest player on any active roster in the NFL. In Week 2, against the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Jets" title="New York Jets">New York Jets</a>, he caught six passes for 101 yards, making him the youngest player since 1960 to have 100 yards receiving in a single game. In the Patriots' Week 3 victory over the <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bills" title="Buffalo Bills">Buffalo Bills</a>, Hernandez led all Patriots receivers with six catches for 65 yards, and had one rush for 13 yards. In the Patriots' Week 9 loss against the <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Browns" title="Cleveland Browns">Cleveland Browns</a>, Hernandez caught his first career touchdown on a one-yard pass from quarterback <a href="/wiki/Tom_Brady" title="Tom Brady">Tom Brady</a>, and added another touchdown later in the game. In Week 15, Hernandez caught two touchdown passes from Brady in a win over the <a href="/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers" title="Green Bay Packers">Green Bay Packers</a>, earning Hernandez <a href="/wiki/Pepsi_NFL_Rookie_of_the_Week" title="Pepsi NFL Rookie of the Week">Pepsi NFL Rookie of the Week</a> honors. He finished the 2010 season with 45 receptions for 563 yards and six touchdowns in 14 games played (7 starts).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_season">2011 season</span></h3>

<p>For his first season in the NFL, Hernandez wore the #85 jersey. In July 2011 during training camp, wide receiver <a href="/wiki/Chad_Johnson_(wide_receiver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Chad Johnson (wide receiver)">Chad Johnson</a> came to the team from a trade with the <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati_Bengals" title="Cincinnati Bengals">Cincinnati Bengals</a>. Hernandez immediately let Johnson, who then legally had his last name as "Ochocinco" based on his uniform number, have the #85, choosing to go back to his college number of #81, which had been taken by <a href="/wiki/Randy_Moss" title="Randy Moss">Randy Moss</a> from 2007 until his mid-season trade to the Vikings in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[29]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hernandez played in 12 of the Patriots' first 14 games, starting 10. (For the second season in a row, he missed two regular-season games with a knee injury.) In Week 15, against the <a href="/wiki/Denver_Broncos" title="Denver Broncos">Denver Broncos</a>, he set career bests with 129 yards on nine receptions, including one touchdown.</p>
<p>In December 2011, Hernandez was named a <a href="/wiki/Pro_Bowl" title="Pro Bowl">Pro Bowl</a> alternate during the season.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[30]</a></sup> In the NFL playoffs, Hernandez had the longest run of the postseason, a 42-yard run against the Broncos on the Patriots' first offensive drive of the game. Hernandez helped lead the Patriots to <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI" title="Super Bowl XLVI">Super Bowl XLVI</a>, scoring a touchdown during the game, but the Patriots lost to the Giants 21–17.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_season">2012 season</span></h3>
<p>On August 27, 2012, the Patriots signed Hernandez to a five-year contract extension, running through 2018. The $12.5 million signing bonus was the largest ever given to an NFL tight end,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[31]</a></sup> and the $40 million total was the second-largest extension ever, after teammate Rob Gronkowski's $53 million. Hernandez was sidelined during the Patriot's Week 2 game against the <a href="/wiki/Arizona_Cardinals" title="Arizona Cardinals">Arizona Cardinals</a> with a high ankle sprain and missed several weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[32]</a></sup> On December 10, during the <a href="/wiki/Monday_Night_Football" title="Monday Night Football">Monday Night Football</a> game against the Houston Texans, Hernandez recorded 8 receptions for 58 yards and two touchdowns.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[33]</a></sup> Hernandez's last NFL appearance was the 2012 AFC Championship game on January 20, 2013 against the <a href="/wiki/Baltimore_Ravens" title="Baltimore Ravens">Baltimore Ravens</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gronkowski.E2.80.93Hernandez_tandem">Gronkowski–Hernandez tandem</span></h3>
<p>At the start of the 2011 season, there were only two tight ends on the Patriots roster: Hernandez, and his fellow 2010 draftee <a href="/wiki/Rob_Gronkowski" title="Rob Gronkowski">Gronkowski</a>.</p>
<p>Although Hernandez's stats were eclipsed by Gronkowski, Hernandez still ranked in the top 5 in receptions, yards, and touchdowns among tight ends. According to <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sports" title="NBC Sports">NBC Sports</a>, Hernandez and Gronkowski were the first pair of tight ends in NFL history to catch at least five touchdowns each in consecutive seasons for the same team. Both ranked in the top 20 among all receivers in number of catches. In 2011, they also set NFL records for yardage, receptions, and touchdowns by tight ends on one team, combining for 169 receptions, 2,237 yards, and 24 touchdowns. The previous records for receptions and yards by multiple tight ends on a single team was set in <a href="/wiki/1984_San_Diego_Chargers_season" title="1984 San Diego Chargers season">1984</a> by the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Chargers" class="mw-redirect" title="San Diego Chargers">San Diego Chargers</a>, who had four tight ends combine for 163 receptions and 1,927 yards;<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[34]</a></sup> the 24 touchdowns eclipsed the record of 18 touchdowns caught by Gronkowski, Hernandez, and <a href="/wiki/Alge_Crumpler" title="Alge Crumpler">Alge Crumpler</a> in 2010.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span></h2>
<p>Hernandez began dating Shayanna Jenkins-Hernandez in 2007. They had a daughter, Avielle Janelle Jenkins-Hernandez, who was born in November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">[35]</a></sup> They became engaged in the same month their daughter was born. That same month, Hernandez purchased a 7,100 square feet (660&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) four-story home, with an in-ground pool, in <a href="/wiki/North_Attleborough,_Massachusetts" title="North Attleborough, Massachusetts">North Attleborough, Massachusetts</a>, for $1.3 million.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">[36]</a></sup></p>
<p>After his death, Newsweek reported on claims that Hernandez was <a href="/wiki/Bisexual" class="mw-redirect" title="Bisexual">bisexual</a>, and suggested it as a motive for the murder of Odin Lloyd.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">[37]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal_issues">Legal issues</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_Gainesville_bar_fight">2007 Gainesville bar fight</span></h3>
<p>On April 28, 2007, according to a police report in <a href="/wiki/Gainesville,_Florida" title="Gainesville, Florida">Gainesville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a>, 17-year-old Hernandez consumed two alcoholic drinks in a restaurant, refused to pay the bill, and was escorted out by a restaurant employee. As the employee walked away, Hernandez punched him on the side of the head and ruptured his eardrum. Although the police department recommended charging Hernandez with felony battery, the incident was settled out of court with a <a href="/wiki/Deferred_prosecution" title="Deferred prosecution">deferred prosecution</a> agreement.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">[40]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2007_Gainesville_double_shooting">2007 Gainesville double shooting</span></h3>
<p>On September 30, 2007, five shots were fired into a car containing Randall Carson, Justin Glass, and Corey Smith while they were waiting at a Gainesville stoplight after having left a nightclub. Carson, a passenger sitting in the back seat who was uninjured, told police that the shooter was a "Hawaiian" or "Hispanic" male with a large build weighing about 230 pounds with a lot of tattoos. Glass, the driver, was shot in the arm, and Smith was shot in the back of the head and still suffers seizures as a result of the shooting. Hernandez invoked his right to counsel and refused to talk to police, and no charges were filed at the time. However, due to his 2013 arrest and subsequent conviction for the murder of Odin Lloyd, Massachusetts authorities reached out to police in Florida to determine whether Hernandez may have had a role in the 2007 shooting.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[41]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Boston_double_homicide">2012 Boston double homicide</span></h3>
<p>Hernandez was investigated in connection with a double murder that took place on July 16, 2012, in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>'s <a href="/wiki/South_End,_Boston" title="South End, Boston">South End</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[42]</a></sup> when Daniel Jorge Correia de Abreu, 29, and Safiro Teixeira Furtado, 28, both of <a href="/wiki/Dorchester,_Massachusetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Dorchester, Massachusetts">Dorchester</a>, were killed by gunshots fired into their vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[43]</a></sup> On May 15, 2014, Hernandez was indicted on murder charges for the killings of de Abreu and Furtado,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[44]</a></sup> with additional charges of armed assault and attempted murder associated with shots fired at the surviving occupants in the vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[45]</a></sup> The trial began March 1, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[46]</a></sup> On April 14, 2017, Hernandez was found not guilty of the murders and most of the other charges. He was found guilty of illegal possession of a handgun.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[47]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_Miami_shooting">2013 Miami shooting</span></h3>
<p>On June 13, 2013, Alexander S. Bradley, described as a friend of Hernandez,<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[48]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[49]</a></sup> filed a lawsuit against Hernandez in a Florida federal court. Bradley claimed that on February 13, 2013, Hernandez had shot him while the two were riding in a car on Interstate 95 in <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Palm Beach County">Palm Beach County</a>, following an altercation at a Miami <a href="/wiki/Strip_club" title="Strip club">strip club</a>; Bradley alleged that he lost his right eye as a result. When police came to assist Bradley, he declined to name his assailant, and no arrest was made at the time. Bradley's lawsuit was dismissed on June 17, 2013, as a result of incorrect paperwork, and refiled on June 19, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[50]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[51]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 3, 2013, Hernandez's lawyers filed a postponement request in federal court until his murder charges were resolved. They said it would be legally unfair to Hernandez to permit the lawsuit to continue while he was defending himself in the shooting death of 27-year-old Odin Lloyd.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>On May 11, 2015, Hernandez was indicted for witness intimidation in relation to the 2013 Miami shooting of Alexander Bradley, since Bradley was reportedly a witness to the 2012 Boston double homicide. The intimidation charge for Hernandez carried a maximum penalty of 10 years in prison.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[52]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">[53]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">[54]</a></sup> This charge was included in the trial that began March 1, 2017, for the 2012 Boston double homicide.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[55]</a></sup></p>

<p>On June 18, 2013, the police searched Hernandez's house in <a href="/wiki/North_Attleboro" class="mw-redirect" title="North Attleboro">North Attleboro</a> for several hours in connection with an investigation into the shooting death of a friend, Odin Lloyd. Lloyd's body was found in an industrial park about a mile from Hernandez's house with multiple gunshot wounds to the back and chest.<sup id="cite_ref-APcharged_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-APcharged-57">[56]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[57]</a></sup> The Massachusetts State Police obtained a search warrant after evidence surfaced that Hernandez intentionally destroyed his home security system. A cell phone belonging to Hernandez was turned over to police "in pieces" and Hernandez allegedly hired a "team of house cleaners" the same day Lloyd's body was discovered, raising additional suspicion.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 20, 2013, the <i><a href="/wiki/Boston_Herald" title="Boston Herald">Boston Herald</a></i> reported the Patriots had "barred" Hernandez from <a href="/wiki/Gillette_Stadium" title="Gillette Stadium">Gillette Stadium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Herald_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Herald-60">[59]</a></sup> According to NFL.com, Patriots owner <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kraft" title="Robert Kraft">Robert Kraft</a> decided to have Patriots staff ask Hernandez to leave because he did not want Gillette to be "the site of a media stakeout".<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[60]</a></sup> However, the <i>Boston Globe</i> reported that Kraft, head coach <a href="/wiki/Bill_Belichick" title="Bill Belichick">Bill Belichick</a> (who is also effectively the Patriots' general manager) and the rest of the team's brain trust had decided to cut ties with Hernandez if he were arrested on any charge related to the case, even an <a href="/wiki/Obstruction_of_justice" title="Obstruction of justice">obstruction of justice</a> charge. Reportedly, this decision was made a week before Hernandez' arrest.<sup id="cite_ref-patience_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patience-62">[61]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 26, 2013, Hernandez was taken from his home in handcuffs and into police custody.<sup id="cite_ref-espnboston_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espnboston-63">[62]</a></sup> The Patriots released Hernandez from the team about 90 minutes later, before officially knowing the charges against him.<sup id="cite_ref-patience_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-patience-62">[61]</a></sup> Their press release stated:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>A young man was murdered last week and we extend our sympathies to the family and friends who mourn his loss. Words cannot express the disappointment we feel knowing that one of our players was arrested as a result of this investigation. We realize that law enforcement investigations into this matter are ongoing. We support their efforts and respect the process. At this time, we believe this transaction is simply the right thing to do.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">[63]</a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Later that day, Hernandez was charged with <a href="/wiki/Murder_(United_States_law)#Degrees_of_murder_in_the_United_States" title="Murder (United States law)">first-degree murder</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">[64]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">[65]</a></sup> in addition to five gun-related charges;<sup id="cite_ref-WCVB_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WCVB-67">[66]</a></sup> he was held without bail at the <a href="/wiki/Bristol_County,_Massachusetts" title="Bristol County, Massachusetts">Bristol County</a> Jail.<sup id="cite_ref-CBS_News_68-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBS_News-68">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>Two other men were also arrested in connection with Lloyd's death: Carlos Ortiz, on June 27, 2013, and Ernest Wallace on June 28, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[68]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[69]</a></sup> Ortiz revealed to the police that Hernandez had secretly rented an apartment in <a href="/wiki/Franklin,_Massachusetts" title="Franklin, Massachusetts">Franklin, Massachusetts</a>. A subsequent search of the apartment, according to the Associated Press, "turned up ammunition and clothing that police believe could be evidence in the murder case against him".<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[70]</a></sup></p>
<p>On August 22, 2013, Hernandez was indicted by a <a href="/wiki/Grand_jury" title="Grand jury">grand jury</a> for the murder of Lloyd.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[71]</a></sup> On September 6, 2013, he was <a href="/wiki/Arraignment#United_States" title="Arraignment">arraigned</a> and pleaded not guilty to <a href="/wiki/First-degree_murder" class="mw-redirect" title="First-degree murder">first-degree murder</a>. He was held without bail but reserved the right to request bail later.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[72]</a></sup></p>
<p>On September 27, 2013, Jenkins, his girlfriend, was indicted on a <a href="/wiki/Perjury" title="Perjury">perjury</a> charge in connection with Lloyd's killing.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">[73]</a></sup></p>
<p>On April 15, 2015, Hernandez was found guilty of murder in the first degree, a charge that in Massachusetts automatically carries a sentence of life in prison without a possibility of parole, as well as five firearm charges.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">[74]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">[75]</a></sup> Hernandez did not face the death penalty, as the Commonwealth of Massachusetts <a href="/wiki/Capital_punishment_in_the_United_States" title="Capital punishment in the United States">abolished</a> the <a href="/wiki/Death_penalty" class="mw-redirect" title="Death penalty">death penalty</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">[76]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-78">[77]</a></sup> Immediately following the conviction, Hernandez was temporarily transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Correctional_Institution_%E2%80%93_Cedar_Junction" title="Massachusetts Correctional Institution – Cedar Junction">Massachusetts Correctional Institution – Cedar Junction</a>, a maximum security intake facility located only 1.5 miles from <a href="/wiki/Gillette_Stadium" title="Gillette Stadium">Gillette Stadium</a> where he formerly played, to begin serving his sentence. He was transferred to serve the remainder of his life sentence at the <a href="/wiki/Souza-Baranowski_Correctional_Center" title="Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center">Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center</a>, a maximum security facility adjacent to the medium security <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Correctional_Institution_%E2%80%93_Shirley" title="Massachusetts Correctional Institution – Shirley">Massachusetts Correctional Institution – Shirley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">[78]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">[79]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span></h3>
<p>Hernandez's arrest and subsequent termination led to financial and other consequences for both Hernandez and the New England Patriots.</p>
<ul>
<li>Hernandez's release meant he automatically forfeited his 2015–18 salaries, totaling $19.3 million, which were not guaranteed. The <i>Boston Globe</i> reported that the Patriots voided all remaining guarantees, including his 2013 and 2014 salaries, on the grounds that those guarantees were for skill, injury, or salary cap room, and did not include being cut for "conduct detrimental to the best interests of professional football." Furthermore, the Patriots planned to withhold $3.25 million of Hernandez's 2012 signing bonus that was due to be paid in 2014, and to recoup the signing bonus already paid.<sup id="cite_ref-guarantees_81-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guarantees-81">[80]</a></sup></li>
<li>Since Hernandez had not completed his fourth season in the league, the Patriots were required to place him on <a href="/wiki/Waivers_(American_football)" title="Waivers (American football)">waivers</a> after releasing him. However, he went unclaimed. After Hernandez cleared waivers on June 28, NFL commissioner <a href="/wiki/Roger_Goodell" title="Roger Goodell">Roger Goodell</a> announced that, while charges against Hernandez are pending, the NFL will not approve any contract signed by Hernandez until Goodell holds a hearing to determine if Hernandez should face suspension or other action under the league's Personal Conduct Policy.<sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">[81]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/CytoSport" title="CytoSport">CytoSport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Puma_SE" title="Puma SE">Puma</a> canceled their endorsement deals with Hernandez. Puma canceled its deal after Hernandez's arrest, while CytoSport canceled its deal a few days prior.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">[82]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">[83]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">[84]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/EA_Sports" title="EA Sports">EA Sports</a> announced that Hernandez's likeness would be dropped from its <i><a href="/wiki/NCAA_Football_14" title="NCAA Football 14">NCAA Football 14</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Madden_NFL_25" title="Madden NFL 25">Madden NFL 25</a></i> video games.<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">[85]</a></sup></li>
<li>A prize-winning photo of Hernandez from his rookie season was removed from the <a href="/wiki/Pro_Football_Hall_of_Fame" title="Pro Football Hall of Fame">Pro Football Hall of Fame</a> in <a href="/wiki/Canton,_Ohio" title="Canton, Ohio">Canton, Ohio</a>, after visitor complaints.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">[86]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Panini_America" class="mw-redirect" title="Panini America">Panini America</a>, a sports memorabilia and trading card company, removed stickers of Hernandez from approximately 500,000 sticker books which had yet to be sent to collectors. The company replaced the stickers, as well as trading cards, with cards depicting <a href="/wiki/Tim_Tebow" title="Tim Tebow">Tim Tebow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">[87]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Florida" title="University of Florida">University of Florida</a> removed Hernandez's name and likeness from various locations at its football facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-nesn.com_89-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nesn.com-89">[88]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The NFL <a href="/wiki/Salary_cap" title="Salary cap">salary cap</a> allows teams to <a href="/wiki/Pro-rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Pro-rate">pro-rate</a> signing bonuses over the life of a contract or a five-year period, whichever is shorter. By cutting Hernandez, the Patriots accelerated all of Hernandez's remaining guaranteed money into the 2013 and 2014 salary caps: the team took a $2.55 million hit in 2013, and another $7.5 million in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-guarantees_81-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guarantees-81">[80]</a></sup></p>
<p>Within hours of Hernandez's arrest, all of his memorabilia and merchandise was removed from the team's official pro shop at <a href="/wiki/Patriot_Place" title="Patriot Place">Patriot Place</a> and from its website.<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">[89]</a></sup> The Patriots ProShop exchanged about 2,500 previously sold Hernandez jerseys for other jerseys, destroying and recycling the Hernandez jerseys for a loss of about $250,000.<sup id="cite_ref-nesn.com_89-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nesn.com-89">[88]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kraft_Breaks_Silence_91-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kraft_Breaks_Silence-91">[90]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_reversal_of_conviction">Possible reversal of conviction</span></h3>
<p>In the state of <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a>, it is possible for Hernandez (through his attorneys) to request to have his murder conviction <a href="/wiki/Vacated_judgment" title="Vacated judgment">vacated</a> due to his death. If that request were to be granted, Hernandez technically will have died an innocent man, due to the legal principle of <i><a href="/wiki/Abatement_in_pleading" title="Abatement in pleading">abatement</a> <a href="/wiki/Ab_initio" title="Ab initio">ab initio</a></i>. This principle asserts that when a criminal defendant dies but has not exhausted all legal appeals, the case reverts to its status "at the beginning"; technically, the conviction is vacated and the defendant is rendered "innocent". Hernandez was in the process of filing an appeal for his 2015 conviction in the <a href="/wiki/Murder_of_Odin_Lloyd" title="Murder of Odin Lloyd">murder of Odin Lloyd</a>. Lawyers for Hernandez plan to file a <a href="/wiki/Motion_(legal)" title="Motion (legal)">motion</a> to vacate his murder conviction once they obtain a copy of his death certificate. State prosecutors, however, reserve the right to object to Hernandez's request; the family of Odin Lloyd may also petition the court not to vacate the conviction and to keep the appeal alive.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">[91]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span></h2>
<p>On April 19, 2017, at 3:05&#160;a.m. <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">EDT</a>, five days after he was found not guilty of the 2012 Boston double homicide, correction officers found Hernandez hanging by his bedsheets from his window in his cell at the <a href="/wiki/Souza-Baranowski_Correctional_Center" title="Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center">Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lancaster,_Massachusetts" title="Lancaster, Massachusetts">Lancaster, Massachusetts</a>. He was transported to <a href="/wiki/UMass_Memorial_Health_Care#Facilities" title="UMass Memorial Health Care">UMass Memorial Hospital-Leominster</a>, where he was pronounced dead at 4:07&#160;a.m.<sup id="cite_ref-globe-2017-04-19_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-globe-2017-04-19-11">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cnn-death_93-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnn-death-93">[92]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bostonherald-death_94-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bostonherald-death-94">[93]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-auto_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-12">[11]</a></sup> State Department of Correction spokesman Christopher Fallon first said no suicide note was found in Hernandez's single-occupant cell, but on April 20, 2017, investigators reported that three handwritten notes were next to a <a href="/wiki/Bible" title="Bible">Bible</a> opened to <a href="/wiki/John_3:16" title="John 3:16">John 3:16</a> and "John 3:16" was written on his forehead in ink.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">[94]</a></sup> One note said he was entering the "timeless realm" and would see his family in heaven while another note said he loved his family and told them not to shed a tear; soap was found covering the floor and there were drawings in blood on the walls showing an unfinished pyramid and the <a href="/wiki/Eye_of_Providence" title="Eye of Providence">all-seeing eye of God</a> with the word "<a href="/wiki/Illuminati" title="Illuminati">Illuminati</a>" written in capital letters underneath.<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">[95]</a></sup> Prison officials had not observed any signs that Hernandez was at risk for suicide, so he was not put on around-the-clock watch.<sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">[96]</a></sup> Upon completion of the autopsy by the medical examiner, the death has been officially ruled a <a href="/wiki/Suicide_by_hanging" title="Suicide by hanging">suicide</a>. Hernandez's brain was released to Boston University to be studied for signs of <a href="/wiki/Chronic_traumatic_encephalopathy" title="Chronic traumatic encephalopathy">chronic traumatic encephalopathy</a> (CTE), a progressive degenerative disease found in people who have had a severe blow or repeated blows to the head.<sup id="cite_ref-CTE_98-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CTE-98">[97]</a></sup> His lawyer-agent, <a href="/wiki/Jose_Baez_(lawyer)" title="Jose Baez (lawyer)">Jose Baez</a>, disputed any claim of suicide, initiating his own investigation of the death.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">[98]</a></sup></p>
