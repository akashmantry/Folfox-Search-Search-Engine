<p><b>Sayyed Abolhassan Banisadr</b> (<span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Abolhassan_Bani-_sadr_from_Iran_pronunciation.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="20" data-file-height="20" /></a>&#160;</span><a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2d/Abolhassan_Bani-_sadr_from_Iran_pronunciation.ogg" class="internal" title="Abolhassan Bani- sadr from Iran pronunciation.ogg">pronunciation</a></span>&#160;<small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Abolhassan_Bani-_sadr_from_Iran_pronunciation.ogg" title="File:Abolhassan Bani- sadr from Iran pronunciation.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span>&#160;;<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Pronunciation" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Pronunciation"><span title="IPA transcription needed for this word. You can help!">needs IPA</span></a></i>]</sup> <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a>: <span lang="fa" dir="rtl" xml:lang="fa">سیِّدابوالحسن بنی‌صدر</span>‎‎; born 22 March 1933) is an <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> politician and economist. He was the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_Iran" title="President of Iran">President</a> of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> after the 1979 <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Revolution" title="Iranian Revolution">Iranian Revolution</a> abolished the <a href="/wiki/Pahlavi_dynasty" title="Pahlavi dynasty">monarchy</a>, serving from 4 February 1980 until he was impeached by <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Iran" class="mw-redirect" title="Parliament of Iran">parliament</a> on 21 June 1981. Prior to his presidency, he was the <a href="/wiki/Foreign_Minister_of_Iran" class="mw-redirect" title="Foreign Minister of Iran">minister of foreign affairs</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Interim_Government_of_Iran" title="Interim Government of Iran">interim government</a>. He has resided for many years in <a href="/wiki/Iranians_in_France" title="Iranians in France">France</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolhassan_Banisadr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banisadr was born on 22 March 1933 in <a href="/wiki/Hamedan" class="mw-redirect" title="Hamedan">Hamedan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> His father was an ayatollah and close to <a href="/wiki/Ruhollah_Khomeini" title="Ruhollah Khomeini">Ruhollah Khomeini</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-twquarterly_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twquarterly-4">[4]</a></sup> He studied finance and economics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Paris" title="University of Paris">Sorbonne</a>. In 1972, Banisadr's father died and he attended the funeral in Iraq where he first met Ayatollah Khomeini.<sup id="cite_ref-rubin80_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rubin80-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banisadr had participated in the anti-Shah student movement during the early 1960s and was imprisoned twice, and was wounded during an uprising in 1963. He then fled to France. He later joined the Iranian resistance group led by Khomeini, becoming one of his hard-liner advisors.<sup id="cite_ref-twquarterly_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twquarterly-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-rubin80_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rubin80-5">[5]</a></sup> Banisadr returned to Iran together with Khomeini as the revolution was beginning in February 1979. He wrote a book on Islamic finance, <i>Eghtesad Tohidi,</i> an Arabic phrase that roughly translates as "The Economics of <a href="/wiki/Tawhid" title="Tawhid">Monotheism</a>."</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolhassan_Banisadr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Revolution" title="Iranian Revolution">Iranian Revolution</a>, Banisadr became deputy minister of finance on 4 February 1979 and was in office until 27 February 1979. He also became a member of the revolutionary council when Bazargan and others left the council to form <a href="/wiki/Interim_Government_of_Iran_(1979)" class="mw-redirect" title="Interim Government of Iran (1979)">the interim government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> After the resignation of the interim finance minister Ali Ardalan on 27 February 1979, he was appointed finance minister by then <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Iran" title="Prime Minister of Iran">prime minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Mehdi_Bazargan" title="Mehdi Bazargan">Mehdi Bazargan</a>. On 12 November 1979, Banisadr was appointed foreign minister to replace <a href="/wiki/Ebrahim_Yazdi" title="Ebrahim Yazdi">Ebrahim Yazdi</a> in the government that was led by <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Islamic_Revolution_(Iran)" class="mw-redirect" title="Council of Islamic Revolution (Iran)">Council of Islamic Revolution</a> when the interim government resigned.</p>
<p>Banisadr was elected to a four-year term as president on 25 January 1980, receiving 78.9 percent of the vote in the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_presidential_election,_1980" title="Iranian presidential election, 1980">election</a>, and was inaugurated on 4 February. Khomeini remained the Supreme Leader of Iran with the constitutional authority to dismiss the president. The inaugural ceremonies were held at the hospital where Khomeini was recovering from a heart ailment.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banisadr was not an <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamic</a> cleric; Khomeini had insisted that clerics should not run for positions in the government. In August and September 1980, Banisadr survived two <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> crashes near the Iran–<a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> border. During the <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War" title="Iran–Iraq War">Iran–Iraq War</a>, Banisadr was appointed acting commander-in-chief by Khomeini on 10 June 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-m93_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-m93-8">[8]</a></sup></p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Majlis" title="Majlis">Majlis</a> (Iran's Parliament) <a href="/wiki/Impeachment" title="Impeachment">impeached</a> Banisadr in his absence on 21 June 1981,<sup id="cite_ref-pay13_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pay13-9">[9]</a></sup> allegedly because of his moves against the clerics in power,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> in particular <a href="/wiki/Mohammad_Beheshti" title="Mohammad Beheshti">Mohammad Beheshti</a>, then head of the judicial system. Khomeini himself appears to have instigated the impeachment, which he signed the next day.</p>
<p>Even before Khomeini had signed the impeachment papers, the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Revolutionary_Guards_Corps" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps">Revolutionary Guard</a> had seized the Presidential buildings and gardens, and imprisoned writers at a newspaper closely tied to Banisadr. Over the next few days, they executed several of his closest friends, including Hossein Navab, Rashid Sadrolhefazi and Manouchehr Massoudi. <a href="/wiki/Ayatollah" title="Ayatollah">Ayatollah</a> <a href="/wiki/Hussein_Ali_Montazeri" class="mw-redirect" title="Hussein Ali Montazeri">Hussein Ali Montazeri</a> was among the few people in the government in support of Banisadr, but he was soon stripped of his powers.</p>
<p>At the same time, the Iranian government outlawed all political parties, except the <a href="/wiki/Islamic_Republic_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamic Republic Party">Islamic Republic Party</a>. Government forces arrested and imprisoned members of other parties, such as the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Mujahedin_of_Iran" title="People's Mujahedin of Iran">People's Mujahedin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iranian_People%27s_Fedai_Guerrillas" title="Iranian People's Fedai Guerrillas">Fadaian Khalq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tudeh" class="mw-redirect" title="Tudeh">Tudeh</a>, and Paikar.</p>
<p>Banisadr went into hiding for a few days before his removal, and hid in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a>, protected by the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Mujahedin_of_Iran" title="People's Mujahedin of Iran">People's Mujahedin</a> (PMOI). He attempted to organize an alliance of anti-Khomeini factions to retake power, including the PMOI, KDP, and the <a href="/wiki/Fedaian_Organisation_(Minority)" title="Fedaian Organisation (Minority)">Fedaian Organisation (Minority)</a>, while eschewing any contact with pro-Shah exile groups. He met numerous times while in hiding with PMOI leader <a href="/wiki/Massoud_Rajavi" title="Massoud Rajavi">Massoud Rajavi</a> to plan an alliance, but after the execution on 27 July of PMOI member Mohammadreza Saadati, Banisadr and Rajavi concluded that it was unsafe to remain in Iran.<sup id="cite_ref-zabih_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zabih-11">[11]</a></sup></p>

<p>In Banisadr's view, this impeachment was a coup d'état against the democracy in Iran. In order to settle the political differences in the country, President Banisadr had asked for a <a href="/wiki/Referendum" title="Referendum">referendum</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flight_and_exile">Flight and exile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolhassan_Banisadr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flight and exile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>When Banisadr was impeached on 21 June 1981 he had fled and had been hiding in western Iran.<sup id="cite_ref-pay13_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pay13-9">[9]</a></sup> On 29 July, Banisadr and <a href="/wiki/Masoud_Rajavi" class="mw-redirect" title="Masoud Rajavi">Masoud Rajavi</a> were smuggled aboard an <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Air_Force" class="mw-redirect" title="Iranian Air Force">Iranian Air Force</a> <a href="/wiki/Boeing_707" title="Boeing 707">Boeing 707</a> piloted by Colonel Behzad Moezzi.<sup id="cite_ref-twquarterly_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twquarterly-4">[4]</a></sup> It followed a routine flight plan before deviating out of Iranian groundspace to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> airspace and eventually landing in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pay13_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pay13-9">[9]</a></sup></p>
<p>Banisadr and Rajavi found political asylum in Paris, conditional on abstaining from anti-Khomeini activities in France. This restriction was effectively ignored after France evacuated its embassy in Tehran. Banisadr, Rajavi and <a href="/wiki/Kurdish_Democratic_Party_of_Iran" class="mw-redirect" title="Kurdish Democratic Party of Iran">the Kurdish Democratic Party</a> set up the <a href="/wiki/National_Council_of_Resistance_of_Iran" title="National Council of Resistance of Iran">National Council of Resistance of Iran</a> in Paris in October 1981.<sup id="cite_ref-twquarterly_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twquarterly-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-zabih_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zabih-11">[11]</a></sup> However, Banisadr soon fell out with Rajavi, accusing him of ideologies favouring dictatorship and violence. Furthermore, Banisadr opposed the armed opposition as initiated and sustained by Rajavi, and sought support for Iran during the war with Iraq.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="My_Turn_to_Speak"><i>My Turn to Speak</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolhassan_Banisadr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: My Turn to Speak">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1991, Banisadr released an English translation of his 1989 text <i>My Turn to Speak: Iran, the Revolution and Secret Deals with the U.S.</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Foreign_Affairs.3B_Winter_1991.2F92_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Foreign_Affairs.3B_Winter_1991.2F92-13">[13]</a></sup> In the book, Banisadr alleged <a href="/wiki/October_surprise_conspiracy_theory" class="mw-redirect" title="October surprise conspiracy theory">covert dealings</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan_presidential_campaign,_1980" title="Ronald Reagan presidential campaign, 1980">Ronald Reagan presidential campaign</a> and leaders in Tehran to prolong the <a href="/wiki/Iran_hostage_crisis" title="Iran hostage crisis">Iran hostage crisis</a> before the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_1980" title="United States presidential election, 1980">1980 U.S. presidential election</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT20090731_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT20090731-14">[14]</a></sup> He also claimed that <a href="/wiki/Henry_Kissinger" title="Henry Kissinger">Henry Kissinger</a> plotted to set up a <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_state" class="mw-redirect" title="Palestinian state">Palestinian state</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Iran" title="Provinces of Iran">Iranian province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Khuzistan" class="mw-redirect" title="Khuzistan">Khuzistan</a> and that <a href="/wiki/Zbigniew_Brzezinski" title="Zbigniew Brzezinski">Zbigniew Brzezinski</a> conspired with <a href="/wiki/Saddam_Hussein" title="Saddam Hussein">Saddam Hussein</a> to plot <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War#1980:_Iraqi_invasion" title="Iran–Iraq War">Iraq's 1980 invasion of Iran</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Foreign_Affairs.3B_Winter_1991.2F92_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Foreign_Affairs.3B_Winter_1991.2F92-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In a review for <i><a href="/wiki/Foreign_Affairs" title="Foreign Affairs">Foreign Affairs</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/William_B._Quandt" title="William B. Quandt">William B. Quandt</a> described the book as "a rambling, self-serving series of reminiscences" and "long on sensational allegations and devoid of documentation that might lend credence to Bani-Sadr's claims."<sup id="cite_ref-Foreign_Affairs.3B_Winter_1991.2F92_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Foreign_Affairs.3B_Winter_1991.2F92-13">[13]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Kirkus_Reviews" title="Kirkus Reviews">Kirkus Reviews</a></i> called it "an interesting-though frequently incredible and consistently self-serving-memoir" and said "frequent sensational accusations render his tale an eccentric, implausible commentary on the tragic folly of the Iranian Revolution."<sup id="cite_ref-Kirkus_Reviews_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kirkus_Reviews-15">[15]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Views">Views</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolhassan_Banisadr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Views">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Banisadr, in a 2008 interview with the Voice of America in the 29th anniversary of the revolution, claimed that Khomeini is directly responsible for the violence originated from the Muslim world and that Khomeini did turn against his promises stated in exile following the revolution.<sup id="cite_ref-vofa8_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vofa8-16">[16]</a></sup> In July 2009, Banisadr publicly denounced the Iranian government's conduct after the disputed <a href="/wiki/Iranian_presidential_election,_2009" title="Iranian presidential election, 2009">presidential election</a>: "Khamenei ordered the fraud in the presidential elections and the ensuing crackdown on protesters." He said the government was "holding on to power solely by means of violence and terror" and accused its leaders of amassing wealth for themselves, to the detriment of other Iranians.<sup id="cite_ref-WT20090707_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WT20090707-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>In published articles on the <a href="/wiki/2009_Iranian_election_protests" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Iranian election protests">2009 Iranian election protests</a>, he ascribed the unusually open political climate before the election to the government's great need to prove its legitimacy.<sup id="cite_ref-DW20090703_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DW20090703-18">[18]</a></sup> However, he said the government had lost all legitimacy. In particular, the spontaneous uprising had cost it its political legitimacy, and with <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Leader_of_Iran" title="Supreme Leader of Iran">Supreme Leader</a> <a href="/wiki/Ayatollah" title="Ayatollah">Ayatollah</a> <a href="/wiki/Ali_Khamenei" title="Ali Khamenei">Ali Khamenei</a>'s threats &#160;–  leading to the violent crackdown &#160;–  its religious legitimacy.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abolhassan_Banisadr&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Banisadr lives in <a href="/wiki/Versailles,_Yvelines" title="Versailles, Yvelines">Versailles</a>, near Paris, in a <a href="/wiki/Villa" title="Villa">villa</a> closely guarded by <a href="/wiki/French_police" class="mw-redirect" title="French police">French police</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WT20090707_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WT20090707-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DW20090703_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DW20090703-18">[18]</a></sup> Banisadr's daughter, Firoozeh, married <a href="/wiki/Masoud_Rajavi" class="mw-redirect" title="Masoud Rajavi">Masoud Rajavi</a> in Paris following their exile.<sup id="cite_ref-twquarterly_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twquarterly-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-habilian_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-habilian-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> They later divorced and the alliance between him and Rajavi also ended.<sup id="cite_ref-twquarterly_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twquarterly-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-habilian_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-habilian-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
