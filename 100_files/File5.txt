<p>On 5 September 2016, <a href="/wiki/Suicide_bomber" class="mw-redirect" title="Suicide bomber">suicide bombers</a> simultaneously exploded in the <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> cities of <a href="/wiki/Tartus" title="Tartus">Tartus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Homs" title="Homs">Homs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hasakah" class="mw-redirect" title="Hasakah">Hasakah</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Car_bomb" title="Car bomb">car bomb</a> detonated on a coastal highway in Tartus, killing more than 5 people, followed by a suicide bomber exploding in the crowd gathering in the area. A car bomb then hit a <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Army" title="Syrian Army">Syrian Army</a> checkpoint in the Zahra district of Homs and killed 2 soldiers. A <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycle</a> bomb detonated near an <a href="/wiki/Asayish_(Syria)" class="mw-redirect" title="Asayish (Syria)">Asayish</a> checkpoint in Hasakah and killed at least 5 people, with an explosion in the capital city of Damascus following soon after.<sup id="cite_ref-multiple_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-multiple-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mee_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mee-2">[2]</a></sup> Two explosions were said to hit a <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> naval base.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
