<p><b>Al-Sabe' Biyar</b> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl" xml:lang="ar"><big>السبع بيار</big></span>‎‎, also spelled <b>Saba'a Biar</b>) is a locality in central <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, administratively part of the <a href="/wiki/Rif_Dimashq_Governorate" title="Rif Dimashq Governorate">Rif Dimashq Governorate</a>, located northeast of <a href="/wiki/Damascus" title="Damascus">Damascus</a>. It is situated in the <a href="/wiki/Syrian_Desert" title="Syrian Desert">Syrian Desert</a>, along the highway connecting <a href="/wiki/Dumayr" class="mw-redirect" title="Dumayr">Dumayr</a> in the west to the <a href="/wiki/Al_Waleed_(town)" class="mw-redirect" title="Al Waleed (town)">al-Waleed</a> border crossing with <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, with no other localities in its vicinity. It is the administrative center and only locality in the al-Sabe' Biyar <i><a href="/wiki/Nahiyah" title="Nahiyah">nahiyah</a></i> ("subdistrict"). According to the <a href="/wiki/Central_Bureau_of_Statistics_(Syria)" title="Central Bureau of Statistics (Syria)">Syria Central Bureau of Statistics</a> (CBS), al-Sabe' Biyar had a population of 395 in the 2004 census.<sup id="cite_ref-CBS_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBS-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
