<p><b>Alan Hugh Dale</b> (born 6 May 1947) is a New Zealand actor. As a child, Dale developed a love of theatre and also became a <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby</a> player. After retiring from the sport, he took on a number of professions to support his family, before deciding to become a professional actor at age 27. With work limited in New Zealand, Dale moved to Australia, where he played Dr. John Forrest in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Doctors" title="The Young Doctors">The Young Doctors</a></i> from 1979 to 1982. He later appeared as <a href="/wiki/Jim_Robinson_(Neighbours)" title="Jim Robinson (Neighbours)">Jim Robinson</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbours" title="Neighbours">Neighbours</a></i>, a part he played from 1985 until 1993. He left the series when he fell out with the producers over the pay he and the rest of the cast received.</p>
<p>After leaving <i>Neighbours</i>, Dale found he had become <a href="/wiki/Typecast_(acting)" class="mw-redirect" title="Typecast (acting)">typecast</a> as Robinson in Australia and struggled to find work. His career was revitalised after he relocated to the United States in 2000. Since then, he has had roles in many American series including prominent parts in <i><a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a></i> (as <a href="/wiki/Caleb_Nichol" class="mw-redirect" title="Caleb Nichol">Caleb Nichol</a>) and <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i> (as <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Meade" title="Bradford Meade">Bradford Meade</a>), as well as recurring and guest roles in <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/24_(TV_series)" title="24 (TV series)">24</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing" title="The West Wing">The West Wing</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(TV_series)" title="Once Upon a Time (TV series)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>. Dale has also appeared in minor roles in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Nemesis" class="mw-redirect" title="Star Trek Nemesis">Star Trek Nemesis</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Homicide" title="Hollywood Homicide">Hollywood Homicide</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones_and_the_Kingdom_of_the_Crystal_Skull" title="Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull">Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_with_the_Dragon_Tattoo_(2011_film)" title="The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2011 film)">The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Captain_America:_The_Winter_Soldier" title="Captain America: The Winter Soldier">Captain America: The Winter Soldier</a></i>, as well as the London West End production of <i><a href="/wiki/Spamalot" title="Spamalot">Spamalot</a></i>. Dale has been married to former <a href="/wiki/Miss_Australia" title="Miss Australia">Miss Australia</a> Tracey Pearson since 1990 and has four children.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_work">Early life and work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: left; clear: left; margin: 0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0; width:25em; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"When I was 27, I started to get really jumpy and thought, what could I do that would give me the same buzz rugby did? Acting was what came to mind. I told my first wife, 'Look, I really can't stand it any more. I'm going to have to go and be an actor'."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">— Dale on why he turned to acting professionally.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>Dale was born on 6 May 1947 in <a href="/wiki/Dunedin" title="Dunedin">Dunedin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Otago" title="Otago">Otago</a>, New Zealand.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> One of four children, Dale enjoyed his childhood, but his family was relatively poor.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> Although he was a gentle child, Dale was bullied so he learned to defend and stand up for himself.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Growing up in New Zealand without television until the 1960s, Dale developed a love of theatre and <a href="/wiki/Amateur_dramatics" class="mw-redirect" title="Amateur dramatics">amateur dramatics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> His first performance was for a school concert, at the age of 13, doing an impression of comedian <a href="/wiki/Shelley_Berman" title="Shelley Berman">Shelley Berman</a>. His parents became founding members of an amateur theatre in <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a> called "The Little Dolphin Theatre". Dale often operated the stage equipment used to produce weather effects,<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> and whenever he was on stage he loved the applause.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> Although a skilled <a href="/wiki/Rugby_union" title="Rugby union">rugby</a> player, Dale opted to move into drama instead because "the acting fraternity didn't like footballers and the footballers didn't like actors. [...] Acting gave me the same buzz and there was the chance of a longer career."<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> He gave up rugby at the age of 21 because it was not considered a workable career at the time, and he had to support his family.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> Acting roles were limited in New Zealand so Dale worked in multiple jobs, including as a <a href="/wiki/Male_model" class="mw-redirect" title="Male model">male model</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Car_salesman" class="mw-redirect" title="Car salesman">car salesman</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Realtor" class="mw-redirect" title="Realtor">realtor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> While working as a <a href="/wiki/Milkman" title="Milkman">milkman</a> he heard the disc jockey at his local radio station resign during a broadcast. Dale went over to the station and told the managers he could do a better job. They gave him a trial and then signed him up for the afternoon show, which led to him landing his first TV role,<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> and at the age of 27 he decided to become a professional actor.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_roles_and_Neighbours">Early roles and <i>Neighbours</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early roles and Neighbours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dale's first professional acting job was playing an Indian in a production of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Royal_Hunt_of_the_Sun" title="The Royal Hunt of the Sun">The Royal Hunt of the Sun</a></i> at the Grafton Theatre in Auckland.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> His first on-screen role came in the New Zealand television drama <i>Radio Waves</i>, which although not successful, he described as "nine months of solid work and great fun."<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> In the late 1970s, Dale moved to Australia at the age of 32,<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-times_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> due to the limited acting work in New Zealand. He applied to the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Dramatic_Art" title="National Institute of Dramatic Art">National Institute of Dramatic Art</a> in Sydney, but was rejected because he "was a lot older than anybody else on the course."<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> He was soon cast as Dr. John Forrest in the Australian soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Doctors" title="The Young Doctors">The Young Doctors</a></i>, where he remained for three-and-a-half years.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1985, Dale was cast in the continuing role of <a href="/wiki/Jim_Robinson_(Neighbours)" title="Jim Robinson (Neighbours)">Jim Robinson</a> in the Australian soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Neighbours" title="Neighbours">Neighbours</a></i>, earning him acclaim across the world, including the United Kingdom. He appeared on the show from the first episode and stayed for eight years before his character was killed off in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> He found working on <i>Neighbours</i> "exciting" and it enabled him to provide for his sons, but he noted: "You were a totally replaceable commodity; [the production company] didn't put any value on any of the people appearing in the show."<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> He expanded: "I didn't like it there, they were not nice people. When we decided that we hated each other, the company and me, one of the things the company did was to market everything they could out of us and pay us nothing."<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> Dale and the company (<a href="/wiki/Reg_Grundy_Organisation" title="Reg Grundy Organisation">Grundy Television</a>) parted on "bad terms".<sup id="cite_ref-metro_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>After <i>Neighbours</i>, Dale struggled to find work in Australia because he was <a href="/wiki/Typecast_(acting)" class="mw-redirect" title="Typecast (acting)">typecast</a> as Jim Robinson.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> His only regular sources of income were <a href="/wiki/Voice-over" title="Voice-over">voice-overs</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Publishing" title="Publishing">publishing</a> magazines about his former show which he "made quite a lot of money out of". He lost most of his profits investing in a failed children's magazine.<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> In 1999, he was cast in the American TV film <i><a href="/wiki/First_Daughter_(1999_film)" title="First Daughter (1999 film)">First Daughter</a></i>, which was filmed in Australia. After discovering he could perform a convincing American accent, Dale attended the film's premiere, finally moving with his family to the United States permanently in January 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> Dale, his second wife Tracey, and their then two-year-old son Nick moved into an "awful little flat" in Los Angeles and found an agent. Dale recalled telling his wife in Melbourne that "there's no way this is going to work. But if it does, it proves you can do anything."<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wider_success">Wider success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wider success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>At the age of 52, he began to revive his career and started taking acting classes,<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> something he had not thought about after being cast in <i>Neighbours</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> He described his age, unknown status and willingness to work for a relatively low fee as being his main assets for getting work in America. His drama teacher, who he has remained with ever since,<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> told him "that you might want to play great roles, but truth is you will get cast as a specific type. Just work out your type. The others in the class said I was a bit <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Hopkins" title="Anthony Hopkins">Anthony Hopkins</a> and a bit <a href="/wiki/Sean_Connery" title="Sean Connery">Sean Connery</a> and that went into my head. I thought if I go for roles those guys would go for I'm more likely to get them."<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> The first role he was offered was a part in a series called <i>Sign of Life</i>, a show about a rock band, which eventually fell through.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> Dale only received a couple of auditions during his first year in America, but his break came when he was cast as the <a href="/wiki/South_African" class="mw-redirect" title="South African">South African</a> Al Patterson in four episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> Since then, Dale has been "busier than ever".<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> He has appeared in many television series including guest appearances on <i><a href="/wiki/The_West_Wing_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="The West Wing (TV series)">The West Wing</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Torchwood" title="Torchwood">Torchwood</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lone_Gunmen" title="The Lone Gunmen">The Lone Gunmen</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Californication_(TV_series)" title="Californication (TV series)">Californication</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-hro_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hro-7">[7]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Practice" title="The Practice">The Practice</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> Many of these have been recurring roles, such as the part of Tom Morrow in <i><a href="/wiki/JAG_(TV_series)" title="JAG (TV series)">JAG</a></i> and its spin-off <i><a href="/wiki/NCIS_(TV_series)" title="NCIS (TV series)">NCIS</a></i>, as well as appearing in three episodes, including the <a href="/wiki/Series_finale" title="Series finale">series finale</a>, of <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i>, playing the "<a href="/wiki/List_of_The_X-Files_characters#Toothpick_Man" title="List of The X-Files characters">Toothpick Man</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> He played the recurring role of Vice President of the United States <a href="/wiki/Jim_Prescott" class="mw-redirect" title="Jim Prescott">Jim Prescott</a> for seven episodes of the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/24_(TV_series)" title="24 (TV series)">24</a></i>, a part which was originally supposed to be a single scene.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> Dale had recurring roles in the serial <i><a href="/wiki/Midnight_Man_(TV_serial)" class="mw-redirect" title="Midnight Man (TV serial)">Midnight Man</a></i> and the Australian series <i><a href="/wiki/Sea_Patrol_(TV_series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea Patrol (TV series)">Sea Patrol</a></i> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> He also had recurring parts on <i><a href="/wiki/Undercovers_(TV_series)" title="Undercovers (TV series)">Undercovers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Entourage_(U.S._TV_series)" title="Entourage (U.S. TV series)">Entourage</a></i> as John Ellis, the fictional owner of <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros." title="Warner Bros.">Warner Bros.</a>, and the British series <i><a href="/wiki/Moving_Wallpaper" title="Moving Wallpaper">Moving Wallpaper</a></i> as a fictional version of himself.<sup id="cite_ref-hro_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hro-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-undercovers_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-undercovers-8">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-renaissance_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-renaissance-9">[9]</a></sup> He had a recurring role as King George in the series <i><a href="/wiki/Once_Upon_a_Time_(TV_series)" title="Once Upon a Time (TV series)">Once Upon a Time</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-hro_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hro-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-once_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-once-10">[10]</a></sup> and Emmett in <i><a href="/wiki/Hot_in_Cleveland" title="Hot in Cleveland">Hot in Cleveland</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> before joining the main cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Dominion_(TV_series)" title="Dominion (TV series)">Dominion</a></i> in 2014, playing General Edward Riesen.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>From 2003 to 2010, Dale appeared in his longest running American roles. He starred in the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> TV series, <i><a href="/wiki/The_O.C." title="The O.C.">The O.C.</a></i> playing <a href="/wiki/Caleb_Nichol" class="mw-redirect" title="Caleb Nichol">Caleb Nichol</a>, a wealthy tycoon. The producers saw that the character had further potential, and made his initially recurring role a regular character in the series.<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> After 35 appearances,<sup id="cite_ref-tattoo_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tattoo-13">[13]</a></sup> Caleb was killed off in the second season episode "<a href="/wiki/The_O.Sea" class="mw-redirect" title="The O.Sea">The O.Sea</a>" in 2005. Dale was disappointed that Caleb was written out and described it as a mistake by the production staff.<sup id="cite_ref-ign_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ign-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2006, Dale was cast in the starring role of <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Meade" title="Bradford Meade">Bradford Meade</a>, the owner of Meade Publications in the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/Ugly_Betty" title="Ugly Betty">Ugly Betty</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-ign_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ign-14">[14]</a></sup> Although he impressed the producers in his audition, he initially lost the role to a "bigger star". After said star began "causing trouble" and was fired, Dale was given the part.<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> Bradford was killed off during the show's second season.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Dale appeared in the second season finale of <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>, "<a href="/wiki/Live_Together,_Die_Alone" title="Live Together, Die Alone">Live Together, Die Alone</a>", as <a href="/wiki/Charles_Widmore" title="Charles Widmore">Charles Widmore</a>, a businessman and leader of the <a href="/wiki/Others_(Lost)" class="mw-redirect" title="Others (Lost)">Others</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hro_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hro-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ign_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ign-14">[14]</a></sup> Dale's publicist was initially worried that Widmore (who was an integral part of the show's mystery) would become a starring role, meaning it would be hard for Dale to appear in both <i>Lost</i> and <i>Ugly Betty</i> at the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-ign_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ign-14">[14]</a></sup> The part became a recurring role, with Dale appearing numerous times between <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_2)" title="Lost (season 2)">seasons two</a> (2006) and <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_6)" title="Lost (season 6)">six</a> (2010). He enjoyed the role but often found it difficult due to his character's unclear motivation.<sup id="cite_ref-media_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-media-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2008, Dale replaced <a href="/wiki/Peter_Davison" title="Peter Davison">Peter Davison</a> in the lead role of <a href="/wiki/King_Arthur" title="King Arthur">King Arthur</a> in the London West End production of <a href="/wiki/Monty_Python" title="Monty Python">Monty Python</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Spamalot" title="Spamalot">Spamalot</a></i> at the Palace Theatre.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> He accepted the role because he was a fan of <i>Monty Python</i> and considered that "life's too short" for him to have turned down a West End part.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> Although he has seen all of the shows and the other films, Dale had never seen <i><a href="/wiki/Monty_Python_and_the_Holy_Grail" title="Monty Python and the Holy Grail">Monty Python and the Holy Grail</a></i> (from which <i>Spamalot</i> is "lovingly ripped off") and had to buy a copy to prepare for the role.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> It was not his first experience in musical theatre because he appeared in a 1984 Australian production of <i><a href="/wiki/Applause_(musical)" title="Applause (musical)">Applause</a></i>, but Dale found the comic timing of the part to be the hardest task. "On stage, the battle is to find all of the humorous moments and not skip over them. [...] There's an art to Python humour and I'm aiming to try and get every single joke just right."<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> He was succeeded in the role by <a href="/wiki/Sanjeev_Bhaskar" title="Sanjeev Bhaskar">Sanjeev Bhaskar</a> on 23 June 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dale has also made several film appearances. He appeared as the <a href="/wiki/Romulan" title="Romulan">Romulan</a> Praetor Hiren in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Nemesis" class="mw-redirect" title="Star Trek Nemesis">Star Trek Nemesis</a></i>, a part he got after the actor originally cast fell ill,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> and had small parts in films such as <i><a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Homicide" title="Hollywood Homicide">Hollywood Homicide</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/After_the_Sunset" title="After the Sunset">After the Sunset</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> and the minor part of General Ross in <i><a href="/wiki/Indiana_Jones_and_the_Kingdom_of_the_Crystal_Skull" title="Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull">Indiana Jones and the Kingdom of the Crystal Skull</a></i>. Dale said his script for <i>Kingdom of the Crystal Skull</i> was printed on <a href="/wiki/Tin_foil" title="Tin foil">tin foil</a> so it was impossible to replicate, in order to keep the film's plot a secret.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup> He appeared in four films released in 2011: <i><a href="/wiki/A_Little_Bit_of_Heaven_(2011_film)" title="A Little Bit of Heaven (2011 film)">A Little Bit of Heaven</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Priest_(2011_film)" title="Priest (2011 film)">Priest</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Be_Afraid_of_the_Dark_(2011_film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Don't Be Afraid of the Dark (2011 film)">Don't Be Afraid of the Dark</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-media_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-media-16">[16]</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Girl_with_the_Dragon_Tattoo_(2011_film)" title="The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2011 film)">The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo</a></i>, having joined late in the film's production.<sup id="cite_ref-tattoo_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tattoo-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popularity_and_style">Popularity and style</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Popularity and style">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite his mainstream success since his move to America, Dale is still primarily known for his role as Jim Robinson in <i>Neighbours</i> in the United Kingdom and Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-times_4-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ident_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ident-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sfx_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfx-22">[22]</a></sup> This was spoofed in a promotional <a href="/wiki/Station_identification" title="Station identification">ident</a> for the UK's <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> in 2007 which sees Dale taking part in a mock interview about the sudden upturn in his career, before being accosted by an Australian fan, who recognises him as Jim Robinson.<sup id="cite_ref-ident_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ident-20">[20]</a></sup> Discussing this association after <i>Ugly Betty</i>'s 2007 <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a> win, Dale noted: "Every article I read I'm always, 'Ex-soap star Jim Robinson'. Maybe now people will just get to know me as actor Alan Dale."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> In 2007, <a href="/wiki/Amazon.co.uk" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.co.uk">Amazon.co.uk</a> reported that they had sold more DVDs of films and television shows featuring Dale than any featuring other ex-<i>Neighbours</i> cast members.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Dale's characters on most of the American television shows he has appeared on have shared similar character traits, which Dale describes as the "go-to powerful guy" role.<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup> Following his appearance as Senator Eaton in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Killing_(U.S._TV_series)" title="The Killing (U.S. TV series)">The Killing</a></i> in 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-hro_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hro-7">[7]</a></sup> reviewers commented on his tendency to play powerful, wealthy and mysterious characters in many shows.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> Maureen Ryan of <i><a href="/wiki/TV_Squad" class="mw-redirect" title="TV Squad">TV Squad</a></i> wrote that it was "lovely to see Alan Dale playing a typically Alan Dale-ian character. He's always so great at playing That Sketchy Wealthy Guy With a Hidden Agenda, which he has now played on, I believe, 87 different shows. And he always does it well."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> Coincidentally, Dale's characters in <i>Neighbours</i>, <i>The O.C.</i> and <i>Ugly Betty</i> have all been killed off by a fatal heart attack.<sup id="cite_ref-metro_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-6">[6]</a></sup> Christopher Rosen of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Observer" class="mw-redirect" title="The New York Observer">The New York Observer</a></i> wrote in 2008 that "with his square jaw and seemingly no nonsense attitude, Mr. Dale is the go-to actor when casting directors need a conservative-looking authoritarian. When he comes onto the screen, audiences immediately take him seriously, since he radiates rich, smug and serious. He demands your respect." Rosen says that Dale is "not even...a particularly good actor" but is "fine enough" and "bring[s] a no frills, no gimmicks style to his roles," and "manages to give a consistent performance in every show he appears on."<sup id="cite_ref-yes_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-yes-28">[28]</a></sup></p>
<p>Jayne Nelson, writing in magazine <i><a href="/wiki/SFX_(magazine)" title="SFX (magazine)">SFX</a></i>, named Dale the second most "serial" science-fiction guest star after <a href="/wiki/Mark_Sheppard" title="Mark Sheppard">Mark Sheppard</a>. She wrote: "The thing is, soap-opera origins aside, Dale is always good. Which is why he keeps getting so much work....Dale never lets you down, always (well, usually) summoning up a pitch-perfect accent, too. There's something comforting about his presence on a show, as though the fact he's in it has lent it some weight."<sup id="cite_ref-sfx_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sfx-22">[22]</a></sup> In a profile of his work on "cult shows", Ben Rawson-Jones of <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">Digital Spy</a> called Dale an "institution".<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Dale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:25em; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"I like both places [Australia and New Zealand] but I get a lot more respect and recognition from Australia than I do in New Zealand. New Zealanders don't want to know me at all, really. I've been Australian for 20-odd years. Everywhere I went I was the guy from <i>Neighbours</i> so I was Australian. Then when I came here [Hollywood], because I have a New Zealand passport I became a New Zealander again. It's odd."</p>
</div>
</div>
<cite style="display: block; text-align: left;">— Dale on his nationality.<sup id="cite_ref-nz_5-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nz-5">[5]</a></sup></cite></div>
<p>In 1968, Dale married his girlfriend, Claire. The couple had two children, Simon and Matthew,<sup id="cite_ref-tvguide_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tvguide-30">[30]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hollywood_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hollywood-31">[31]</a></sup> both of whom are involved in the entertainment industry, Simon as a radio announcer at <a href="/wiki/Kiss_100_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Kiss 100 London">Kiss 100</a><sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> and Matthew as a writer, film maker and actor.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> The marriage ended in divorce in 1979.<sup id="cite_ref-fr_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-33">[33]</a></sup> At the time, Dale lived in <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a> but after the divorce he moved to Sydney with his sons.<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> On 8 April 1990,<sup id="cite_ref-fr_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-33">[33]</a></sup> he married Tracey Pearson, the 1986 <a href="/wiki/Miss_Australia" title="Miss Australia">Miss Australia</a>, whom he met at the <a href="/wiki/1986_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Australian Grand Prix">1986 Australian Grand Prix</a>, when she was 21 and he was 39. Dale described it as "the most appropriate relationship I've ever had."<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> Dale also has two children from this marriage, Daniel and Nick, who "keep him young".<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>He and his family now live in <a href="/wiki/Manhattan_Beach,_California" title="Manhattan Beach, California">Manhattan Beach</a>, Los Angeles, California<sup id="cite_ref-cruisy_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cruisy-34">[34]</a></sup> and also own property in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> Dale sold his holiday home in New Zealand in 2011 for <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_dollar" title="New Zealand dollar">$</a>1.25m.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup> Both of Dale's parents died in 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup> and he regrets not spending enough time with them.<sup id="cite_ref-metro_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-metro-6">[6]</a></sup> Dale describes his life philosophy as being <a href="/wiki/Winston_Churchill" title="Winston Churchill">Winston Churchill</a>'s quote "Never, never, never give up",<sup id="cite_ref-sydney_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sydney-1">[1]</a></sup> and counts <a href="/wiki/Gene_Hackman" title="Gene Hackman">Gene Hackman</a> as his "big acting hero".<sup id="cite_ref-twenty_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twenty-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
