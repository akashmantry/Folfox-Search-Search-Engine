<p><b>Alexandra</b> "<b>Alex</b>" <b>Rousseau</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Fictional_character" class="mw-redirect" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> on the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> <a href="/wiki/Television_series" class="mw-redirect" title="Television series">television series</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i> played by <a href="/wiki/Tania_Raymonde" title="Tania Raymonde">Tania Raymonde</a>. She was born 16 years prior to the crash of <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_Flight_815" class="mw-redirect" title="Oceanic Flight 815">Oceanic Flight 815</a>, but was <a href="/wiki/Kidnapping" title="Kidnapping">taken</a> from her mother, <a href="/wiki/Danielle_Rousseau" title="Danielle Rousseau">Danielle Rousseau</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mira_Furlan" title="Mira Furlan">Mira Furlan</a>) by <a href="/wiki/Others_(Lost)" class="mw-redirect" title="Others (Lost)">the Others</a>. She was raised among them, believing her mother to be dead. She has helped the survivors of Oceanic Flight 815 on many occasions, and is reunited with her mother at the end of the third season. Not long after however, she is shot and killed by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Keamy" title="Martin Keamy">Keamy</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kevin_Durand" title="Kevin Durand">Kevin Durand</a>) after her adoptive father, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Linus" title="Ben Linus">Ben</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Emerson" title="Michael Emerson">Michael Emerson</a>), would not listen to his demands. Her death scene was received positively by critics, earning it a spot on multiple "top moments of the season" lists (see <a href="#Reception">Reception</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arc">Arc</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Arc">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Before_the_crash">Before the crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Before the crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A heavily pregnant Danielle Rousseau (<a href="/wiki/Mira_Furlan" title="Mira Furlan">Mira Furlan</a>) and her husband Robert (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Guillaume_Dabinpons&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Guillaume Dabinpons (page does not exist)">Guillaume Dabinpons</a>) along with the rest of their crew, shipwrecked on the island, 16 years before the crash of <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_Flight_815" class="mw-redirect" title="Oceanic Flight 815">Oceanic Flight 815</a>, during a French scientific expedition. According to Rousseau, her team becomes "sick", so she kills them all, and later gives birth to Alexandra.<sup id="cite_ref-Solitary_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Solitary-1">[1]</a></sup> Rousseau claims she saw a column of black smoke on the island a week later. That night, <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Linus" class="mw-redirect" title="Benjamin Linus">Benjamin Linus</a> and a young <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Rom" title="Ethan Rom">Ethan Rom</a> (<a href="/wiki/William_Mapother" title="William Mapother">William Mapother</a>) are ordered by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Widmore" title="Charles Widmore">Charles Widmore</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alan_Dale" title="Alan Dale">Alan Dale</a>) to kill Danielle and, subsequently, her baby, Alex. Instead, unwilling to kill an innocent child, Ben neglected Widmore's orders and kidnapped Alex to raise her as his daughter and let Danielle live.<sup id="cite_ref-Dead_is_Dead_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dead_is_Dead-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_crash">After the crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: After the crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sixteen years later, Alex meets the <a href="/wiki/Pregnancy" title="Pregnancy">pregnant</a> woman <a href="/wiki/Claire_Littleton" title="Claire Littleton">Claire Littleton</a> (<a href="/wiki/Emilie_de_Ravin" title="Emilie de Ravin">Emilie de Ravin</a>), whom the Other, Ethan Rom, had kidnapped to take her baby. Alex tells her that the Others will kill Claire once they get her baby. She leads Claire out of the Others' hatch and tries to make her get back to safety.<sup id="cite_ref-Maternity_Leave_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maternity_Leave-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>When Kate, Sawyer and Jack are captured by the Others, Alex attempts to break Kate and Sawyer out of where they are being held captive, but stops and gives up when another Other, <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#The_Others" class="mw-redirect" title="Characters of Lost">Danny Pickett</a> (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Bowen_(actor)" title="Michael Bowen (actor)">Michael Bowen</a>) pulls a gun on her.<sup id="cite_ref-I_Do_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I_Do-4">[4]</a></sup> In a later rescue attempt, Alex is successful in helping Kate and Sawyer escape. She agrees to let them back to the main island so long as they help rescue her boyfriend, <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#The_Others" class="mw-redirect" title="Characters of Lost">Karl</a> (<a href="/wiki/Blake_Bashoff" title="Blake Bashoff">Blake Bashoff</a>). After finding where he is, they break him out of a mysterious room where he has been undergoing <a href="/wiki/Brainwashing" class="mw-redirect" title="Brainwashing">brainwashing</a>. Alex, Kate, Sawyer, and Karl are about to leave when <a href="/wiki/Juliet_Burke" title="Juliet Burke">Juliet</a> (<a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_Mitchell" title="Elizabeth Mitchell">Elizabeth Mitchell</a>) insists Alex stays. Later Alex helps Jack escape from his cell to stop Juliet's execution. Thanks to their interference, Juliet is not executed, only "marked."<sup id="cite_ref-Not_in_Portland_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Not_in_Portland-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alex, along with the rest of the Others and Jack, returns to her home in <a href="/wiki/Others_(Lost)#The_Barracks" class="mw-redirect" title="Others (Lost)">Barracks</a>. She is surprised when <a href="/wiki/Sayid_Jarrah" title="Sayid Jarrah">Sayid</a> (<a href="/wiki/Naveen_Andrews" title="Naveen Andrews">Naveen Andrews</a>), part of the rescue party that has arrived to get Jack, says that he knows her thought-to-be deceased mother. She later unknowingly walks past Danielle Rousseau, her mother, while taking <a href="/wiki/John_Locke_(Lost)" title="John Locke (Lost)">Locke</a> (<a href="/wiki/Terry_O%27Quinn" title="Terry O'Quinn">Terry O'Quinn</a>) to the Other's submarine at Ben's request.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Man_from_Tallahassee_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Man_from_Tallahassee-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Others leave the Barracks and begin travelling to the Temple with Locke, as he wishes to join them.<sup id="cite_ref-Left_Behind_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Left_Behind-7">[7]</a></sup> After he is initiated,<sup id="cite_ref-The_Brig_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Brig-8">[8]</a></sup> Alex approaches Locke, and gives him a <a href="/wiki/Pistol" title="Pistol">pistol</a>, claiming he'll need it if he was planning to meet the leader of the Others, <a href="/wiki/Characters_of_Lost#The_Others" class="mw-redirect" title="Characters of Lost">Jacob</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Man_Behind_the_Curtain_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Man_Behind_the_Curtain-9">[9]</a></sup> However, when Ben returns alone the next day, he bitterly returns the gun to her, and reveals his plan for a group of Others to go to the survivor's camp that night and kidnap the pregnant women. Sensing trouble, Alex flees into the jungle in search of Karl, who has been living in hiding since his escape. She gives him her gun and tells him he must warn the survivors. The two kiss before Karl heads off.<sup id="cite_ref-Greatest_Hits_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greatest_Hits-10">[10]</a></sup> Ben goes to confront the survivors, bringing Alex with him. On the way there, Ben tells her that he is taking her to her new family, explaining the reason he locked Karl up was he didn't want him getting Alex pregnant. Once they meet the survivors, Ben reunites Alex with her mother.<sup id="cite_ref-Through_the_Looking_Glass_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Through_the_Looking_Glass-11">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>She joins Locke's group who head to the Barracks, fearing people on an incoming freighter.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Beginning_of_the_End_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Beginning_of_the_End-12">[12]</a></sup> Ben sends Alex, Karl and Danielle to the Temple for extra safety, but on the way they are ambushed by mercenaries from the freighter who are looking for Ben, resulting in Karl and Danielle being shot. Alex surrenders shouting that she was Ben's daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-Meet_Kevin_Johnson_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Meet_Kevin_Johnson-13">[13]</a></sup> She is used as a hostage to try to have Ben surrender. Alex sets off a distress signal at the electric fence surrounding the Barracks to warn Ben and the others that the mercenaries were coming. They threaten to kill her if Ben will not surrender himself. Ben refuses, and the lead mercenary, Keamy, executes Alex with one gunshot to the head. Later, Ben goes to Alex's body to say goodbye.<sup id="cite_ref-The_Shape_of_Things_to_Come_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Shape_of_Things_to_Come-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>Alex appeared posthumously as a manifestation of <a href="/wiki/Mythology_of_Lost#The_Monster" title="Mythology of Lost">the Smoke Monster</a> to judge Ben for Alex's death. Ben is spared but as his punishment, the Monster (in the form of Alex) ordered him to follow and listen to John Locke's every word and never again to try to harm him in any way or he will bear the consequences. Crushed by once again seeing Alex, Ben complies.<sup id="cite_ref-Dead_is_Dead_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dead_is_Dead-2">[2]</a></sup> Richard Alpert later explains that he buried Alex's body and shows Ben where.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flash-Sideways">Flash-Sideways</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Flash-Sideways">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alex is a bright high school student in Los Angeles hoping to major in history. Her teacher, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Linus" title="Ben Linus">Ben Linus</a>, holds her in high regard, telling her the future is nothing to worry about. Later, while he is tutoring her, she reveals to Ben about Principal Reynolds' sexual affair with one of the school nurses on campus, which leads to Ben attempting to blackmail Reynolds for his position as principal. However, Reynolds retaliates by saying if he is blackmailed, he will not write Alex a <a href="/wiki/Recommendation_letter" title="Recommendation letter">letter of recommendation</a> for <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a>. Ben backs down, preferring to savor Alex's educational future. Unlike his island counterpart, Ben has chosen Alex over himself. He is eventually invited over for dinner at the Rousseau's home with Alex and her mother, Danielle. Danielle thanks Ben for everything he has done for Alex and says he is the closest thing Alex has to a father, by which Ben is visibly moved.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alex is a renegade Other who often turns her back on her group in order to help the crash survivors.<sup id="cite_ref-Maternity_Leave_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maternity_Leave-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Not_in_Portland_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Not_in_Portland-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Greatest_Hits_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Greatest_Hits-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_%22Sawyer%22_Ford" title="James &quot;Sawyer&quot; Ford">Sawyer</a> nicknames her "Sheena",<sup id="cite_ref-Not_in_Portland_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Not_in_Portland-5">[5]</a></sup> a reference to <i><a href="/wiki/Sheena,_Queen_of_the_Jungle" title="Sheena, Queen of the Jungle">Sheena, Queen of the Jungle</a></i>, a girl raised in the jungle who is an expert in fighting with makeshift weapons.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tania Raymonde was told her character would be called "Jessica" when first cast in the role. Furthermore, her character was promoted as a survivor who would be introduced in <a href="/wiki/Maternity_Leave_(Lost)" title="Maternity Leave (Lost)">Maternity Leave</a>. Cast members often read fake lines with a different names in their audition to limit potential spoilers from leaking.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> In her first appearance, Raymonde was credited as "young girl", preventing viewers knowing in advance that Alex would be introduced.</p>
<p>Mira Furlan met Raymonde for the first time just minutes before shooting their reunion scene in the season three finale.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-16">[16]</a></sup> The actors believed the scene to be a "huge moment" for their characters, which Furlan described as a "basic human moment".<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-16">[16]</a></sup> Furlan was glad to have "such a beautiful partner", and noted their physical resemblance.<sup id="cite_ref-usatoday_familyreunion_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-usatoday_familyreunion-16">[16]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alex_Rousseau&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jeff Jensen from <i><a href="/wiki/Entertainment_Weekly" title="Entertainment Weekly">Entertainment Weekly</a></i> described Alex's death scene as "hardcore", commenting that it will be "sitting very high on this ranking of all-time pivotal <i>Lost</i> moments" by the end of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> Before the <a href="/wiki/There%27s_No_Place_Like_Home" title="There's No Place Like Home">season finale</a> aired, Jensen ranked this as the second best moment of the season<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/IGN" title="IGN">IGN</a>'</i>s Chris Carabott also considered it "one of the more pivotal scenes in the entire series" due to it being "phenomenally shot, edited and acted"<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> Oscar Dahl described it as "perfectly paced&#160;… and provided a huge shock&#160;… and some of the best acting you'll ever see" ranking it the fifth best moment of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Daniel from <i><a href="/wiki/TMZ.com" class="mw-redirect" title="TMZ.com">TMZ</a></i> loved Alex's execution scene, giving it an "A+".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
