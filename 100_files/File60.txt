<p><b>Alistair Edward Brownlee</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">MBE</a> (born 23 April 1988) is a British <a href="/wiki/Triathlete" class="mw-redirect" title="Triathlete">triathlete</a>. He is the <a href="/wiki/Triathlon_at_the_2012_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s" title="Triathlon at the 2012 Summer Olympics – Men's">2012</a> and <a href="/wiki/Triathlon_at_the_2016_Summer_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s" title="Triathlon at the 2016 Summer Olympics – Men's">2016 Olympic gold medallist</a> in the triathlon, and the only athlete to win two Olympic titles in the event. He is also a two-time <a href="/wiki/ITU_Triathlon_World_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="ITU Triathlon World Championships">Triathlon World Champion</a>, winning in 2009 and 2011, a two-time World Team Champion (2011, 2014), a three-time European Champion, and 2014 <a href="/wiki/Triathlon_at_the_2014_Commonwealth_Games_%E2%80%93_Men%27s" title="Triathlon at the 2014 Commonwealth Games – Men's">Commonwealth</a> champion.</p>
<p>His brother, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Brownlee" class="mw-redirect" title="Jonathan Brownlee">Jonathan Brownlee</a>, is also a triathlete, placing second in the 2016 Olympics, third in the 2012 Olympics, winning the 2012 World Championship and the last World Sprint Triathlon Championship, in addition to accompanying his brother in their World and Commonwealth mixed team relay victories.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brownlee was appointed <a href="/wiki/Member_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Member of the Order of the British Empire">Member of the Order of the British Empire</a> (MBE) in the 2013 New Year Honours for services to triathlon.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alistair_Brownlee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Brownlee was educated at <a href="/wiki/Bradford_Grammar_School" title="Bradford Grammar School">Bradford Grammar School</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ITUprofile_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITUprofile-8">[8]</a></sup> a co-educational <a href="/wiki/Independent_school" title="Independent school">independent school</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Bradford" title="Bradford">Bradford</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Girton_College,_Cambridge" title="Girton College, Cambridge">Girton College, Cambridge</a>, where he started a medicine degree.<sup id="cite_ref-Three_times_a_winner_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Three_times_a_winner-9">[9]</a></sup> After just the first eight-week term, Brownlee decided to leave and instead went to the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Leeds" title="University of Leeds">University of Leeds</a>, where he gained a degree in Sports Science and Physiology in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-Three_times_a_winner_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Three_times_a_winner-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ABws_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABws-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> He is now studying for an MSc in Finance at <a href="/wiki/Leeds_Metropolitan_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Leeds Metropolitan University">Leeds Metropolitan University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABws_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABws-10">[10]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brownlee was introduced to triathlon at a young age by his uncle, Simon Hearnshaw, who regularly competed in the sport. As a junior, he was a successful <a href="/wiki/Fell_running" title="Fell running">fell</a><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Cross_country_running" title="Cross country running">cross country</a> <a href="/wiki/Running" title="Running">runner</a>, coming second in the Junior English Cross Country Championships<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> and winning the Yorkshire County title on several occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brownlee has said that "It was only when I won the World Junior Championships in 2006 that I made the conscious decision to really focus on triathlon".<sup id="cite_ref-tcs.cam.ac.uk_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcs.cam.ac.uk-18">[18]</a></sup> Subsequently, Brownlee left <a href="/wiki/Girton_College,_Cambridge" title="Girton College, Cambridge">Girton College, Cambridge</a> where he was reading medicine,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> to move back to Yorkshire, focus on triathlon and study at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Leeds" title="University of Leeds">University of Leeds</a>. He described his decision to leave Cambridge to a student newspaper there in 2013, saying "trying to fit 30+ hours of training around my degree was pretty impossible. I decided that triathlon was my true passion, so I moved back to study at Leeds on a less demanding course, and in a city where the sport was well supported at the time by British Triathlon".<sup id="cite_ref-tcs.cam.ac.uk_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tcs.cam.ac.uk-18">[18]</a></sup> He is a member of the triathlon club based at Leeds Metropolitan University's <a href="/wiki/Headingley_Stadium" title="Headingley Stadium">Carnegie High Performance centre</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LeedsMet1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeedsMet1-4">[4]</a></sup> and is coached by Carnegie Director of Sport Malcolm Brown, and in swimming by Jack Maitland.<sup id="cite_ref-LeedsMet1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LeedsMet1-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brownlee has two brothers, Edward and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Brownlee" class="mw-redirect" title="Jonathan Brownlee">Jonathan</a>. His younger brother Jonathan is also a triathlete, winning the bronze medal at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> and the silver medal at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Summer_Olympics" title="2016 Summer Olympics">2016 Summer Olympics</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Triathlon_career">Triathlon career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alistair_Brownlee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Triathlon career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:162px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg/160px-Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg" width="160" height="219" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg/240px-Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg/320px-Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1596" data-file-height="2184" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alistair_Brownlee_Hyde_Park_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Alistair Brownlee wins the Hyde Park Triathlon, London, August 2011</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Brownlee represented <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_at_the_2008_Summer_Olympics" title="Great Britain at the 2008 Summer Olympics">Great Britain at the 2008 Summer Olympics</a> in the triathlon event, finishing in 12th place and first British competitor. During the 2008 season, his best world cup finish was 3rd place in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a> and he also won the under-23 world <a href="/wiki/Championship" title="Championship">championship</a>.</p>
<p>Brownlee won the 2009 <a href="/wiki/ITU_Triathlon_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="ITU Triathlon World Championship">ITU Triathlon World Championship</a> – winning all five of the season's <a href="/wiki/2009_ITU_Triathlon_World_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 ITU Triathlon World Championships">ITU World Championship Series</a> events in which he competed. He was victorious in Madrid, Washington D.C, Kitzbühel and London before winning the 2009 season <a href="/wiki/Grand_Final" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Final">Grand Final</a>, held on <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gold_Coast,_Queensland" title="Gold Coast, Queensland">Gold Coast</a> on 12 September<sup id="cite_ref-DT1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DT1-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> and thereby became the first person ever to win the ITU world Triathlon titles for Junior Men (2006), Under 23 Men (2008) and Senior Men (2009).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<p>During the 2010 off season, Brownlee sustained a <a href="/wiki/Stress_fracture" title="Stress fracture">stress fracture</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Femur" title="Femur">femur</a> and wasn't able to return to competition until June, which made defence of his ITU title almost impossible.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_fracture_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_fracture-23">[23]</a></sup> He did however mark his return to competitive triathlon with a convincing win in Madrid, followed a month later with the European Triathlon Union (ETU) Championships title.<sup id="cite_ref-Athlone2010_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Athlone2010-24">[24]</a></sup> Collapsing with exhaustion at the London race,<sup id="cite_ref-MailCollapse_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MailCollapse-25">[25]</a></sup> in late July, meant that despite also going on to win the final race of the <a href="/wiki/2010_ITU_Triathlon_World_Championships" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 ITU Triathlon World Championships">2010 ITU Series</a>, in Budapest, he lost his overall World Championship title to <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Javier_G%C3%B3mez_Noya" title="Francisco Javier Gómez Noya">Javier Gómez</a>.</p>
<p>In the April opening event of the 2011 <a href="/wiki/ITU" class="mw-redirect" title="ITU">ITU</a> Championship season in Sydney, Brownlee suffered a heavy fall while in the lead which brought early disappointment and a 29th-place finish.<sup id="cite_ref-Leeds_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Leeds-26">[26]</a></sup> This was contrasted with outstanding success when, in the space of just twenty-one days during June 2011, Brownlee convincingly won the next two ITU series events (in Madrid<sup id="cite_ref-Madrid2011_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Madrid2011-27">[27]</a></sup> and Kitzbühel<sup id="cite_ref-kitzbuhel2011_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kitzbuhel2011-28">[28]</a></sup>) and, following a puncture whilst in the lead and having to make up more than 30 seconds on the new leaders, successfully defended his European title at the 2011 Pontevedra ETU Triathlon European Championships and shared the podium with his brother Jonathan, who took the silver medal.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_b2b_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_b2b-29">[29]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ETUb2b_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ETUb2b-30">[30]</a></sup> Brownlee won the 2011 ITU world title after winning the Grand Final in Beijing and the gold medal at the London 2012 Olympics on 7 August<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> while his brother finished in 3rd, taking the bronze medal.</p>
<p>Ali won the Commonwealth Games Triathlon in 2014, controlling the race with his brother Jonny, from the start, before pulling away on the 1st lap of the run in dominating style. Jonny took Silver.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brownlee also took gold in the mixed triathlon team relay at the 2014 Commonwealth Games, with brother Jonathan, <a href="/wiki/Vicky_Holland" title="Vicky Holland">Vicky Holland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jodie_Stimpson" title="Jodie Stimpson">Jodie Stimpson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup></p>
<p>At the final race of the 2016 World Triathlon Series in Cozumel, Mexico, Alistair Brownlee helped his brother Jonny over the line after his brother, who was leading comfortably heading into the final kilometre of the 10km run, began to show the effects of heat and exhaustion and began to weave across the course and appeared on the verge of collapse. Alistair effectively gave up his chance of winning the race in order to assist his brother finally pushing him over the line and coming in third himself. The South African Henri Schoeman, who won bronze behind the Brownlees in Rio, overtook Jonny to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup></p>
<p>Brownlee planned to make his long-course debut at the half-iron distance Challenge Mogan-Gran Canaria triathlon in April 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[35]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="ITU_competitions">ITU competitions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alistair_Brownlee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: ITU competitions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the six years from 2005 to 2010, Alistair Brownlee took part in 31 ITU competitions and achieved 24 top ten positions, among which were 13 gold medals, 5 silver medals, and 2 bronze medals.</p>
<ul>
<li>2008, won the U23 World Championship and placed 12th at the Olympic Games in Beijing.</li>
<li>2009, won the Dextro Energy World Championship Series placing first in almost all of the circuit's triathlons (i.e. Madrid, Washington DC, Kitzbühel, London, and Gold Coast).</li>
<li>2010, opened the season with the European Championship gold medal but subsequently had to put up with a stress fracture of the femur.</li>
<li>2011, won the World Championship Series triathlons in Madrid and Kitzbühel, leading the ITU World Championship ranking, and crowned himself again European Elite Champion.</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="French_Club_Championship_Series">French Club Championship Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alistair_Brownlee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: French Club Championship Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, only the femur fracture could slow Brownlee's dominance for some time. At the opening triathlon in Dunkirk (23 May 2010), Alistair was not present, his brother Jonathan won the gold medal. At Beauvais (13 June 2010) Alistair placed 9th. At the <i>Triathlon de Paris</i> (18 July 2010), however, and at Tourangeaux (29 August 2010), Brownlee won the gold medals again. At the Grand Final in La Baule (Triathlon Audencia, 18 September 2010), Alistair placed 2nd, Jonathan 3rd, and their rival <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Javier_G%C3%B3mez_Noya" title="Francisco Javier Gómez Noya">Javier Gómez Noya</a> could win gold.</p>
<p>In 2011, Brownlee took part in the French Club Championship Series <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_de_Triathlon" title="Grand Prix de Triathlon"><i>Lyonnaise des Eaux</i></a> representing the club <i><a href="/wiki/ECS_Triathlon" title="ECS Triathlon">ECS Triathlon</a></i>, which, thanks to the Brownlee brothers and <a href="/wiki/Francisco_Javier_G%C3%B3mez_Noya" title="Francisco Javier Gómez Noya">Javier Gómez Noya</a>, had already won the Club Championship in 2010. At Nice (24 April 2011), Dunkirk (22 May 2011), and Paris (9 July 2011) Brownlee was the winner.</p>
