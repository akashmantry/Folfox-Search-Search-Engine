<p>The <b>Alternative Energy Development Board</b> (<a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>: <span lang="ur" dir="rtl" xml:lang="ur"><span class="Nastaliq" title="Nastaliq" style="font-size: 125%; font-family: 'Jameel Noori Nastaleeq', 'Urdu Typesetting', 'Noto Nastaliq Urdu', 'Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft', 'Hussaini Nastaleeq', 'AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq', IranNastaliq, 'Awami Nastaliq', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta4', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta3', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta2', 'Awami Nastaliq Beta1', 'Nafees Nastaleeq', 'Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01', 'Pak Nastaleeq', 'PDMS_Jauhar', 'Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq';"><b>متبادل توانائی ترقی بورڈ</b></span></span>‎) or <b>AEDB</b>, is the sole representing agency of the Federal Government that was established in May 2003 with the main objective to facilitate, promote and encourage development of Renewable Energy in Pakistan and with a mission to introduce Alternative and Renewable Energies (AREs) at an accelerated rate.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The administrative control of AEDB was transferred to Ministry of Water and Power in 2006.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Pakistan" title="Government of Pakistan">Government of Pakistan</a> has inter alia mandated AEDB to:-</p>
<ul>
<li>Implement policies, programs and projects through private sector in the field of ARE<sup id="cite_ref-esmap.org_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esmap.org-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Assist and facilitate development and generation of ARE to achieve sustainable economic growth<sup id="cite_ref-esmap.org_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esmap.org-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Encourage transfer of technology and develop indigenous manufacturing base for ARE Technology<sup id="cite_ref-esmap.org_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esmap.org-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Promote provision of energy services that are based on ARE resources<sup id="cite_ref-esmap.org_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esmap.org-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li>Undertake ARE projects on commercial scale (AEDB Act 2010)<sup id="cite_ref-esmap.org_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-esmap.org-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>The Government of Pakistan has tasked the AEDB to ensure 5% of total national power generation capacity to be generated through renewable energy technologies by the year 2030. In addition, under the remote village electrification program, AEDB has been directed to electrify 7,874 remote villages in Sindh and Balochistan provinces through ARE technologies. The Federal Government established AEDB as a statutory organization by announcing and promulgating the AEDB Act in May 2010. The Act bestowed upon AEDB the authorities and the responsibilities for the promotion and development of AREs.</p>
<p>Although, the AEDB is administrated through the appointed chairman and chief executive officer; all policies and funding research is overseen by the prime minister.</p>
<p>Currently the organization's progress is being heard and examined in the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_Pakistan" title="Supreme Court of Pakistan">Supreme Court of Pakistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
