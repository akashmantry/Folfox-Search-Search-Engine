<p><i><b>American Crime</b></i> is an American <a href="/wiki/Anthology_series" title="Anthology series">anthology</a> <a href="/wiki/Crime_drama" class="mw-redirect" title="Crime drama">crime drama</a> television series that premiered on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a> on March 5, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-TVBTN_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TVBTN-1">[1]</a></sup> The series was created by <a href="/wiki/John_Ridley" title="John Ridley">John Ridley</a>, and stars <a href="/wiki/Felicity_Huffman" title="Felicity Huffman">Felicity Huffman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Hutton" title="Timothy Hutton">Timothy Hutton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The first season centers on race, class, and gender politics as it follows the lives of the participants in a trial who are forever changed during the legal process.<sup id="cite_ref-andreeva508_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-andreeva508-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety508_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety508-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>The series was renewed for a second season on May 7, 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> The second season, which premiered on-demand on December 17, 2015,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> and premiered on ABC on January 6, 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> features a new storyline and characters, with some actors from the first season—including Huffman, Hutton, <a href="/wiki/Regina_King" title="Regina King">Regina King</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Nolasco" title="Elvis Nolasco">Elvis Nolasco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Cabral" title="Richard Cabral">Richard Cabral</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lili_Taylor" title="Lili Taylor">Lili Taylor</a>—playing different characters.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Taylor_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Taylor-10">[10]</a></sup> On May 12, 2016, ABC renewed the series for a third season,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> which stars Huffman, Hutton and King.<sup id="cite_ref-s3huffman_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s3huffman-12">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-s3details_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s3details-13">[13]</a></sup> The third season premiered on March 12, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>All three seasons of <i>American Crime</i> have received critical acclaim. In 2015, the series received ten <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award" title="Primetime Emmy Award">Primetime Emmy Award</a> nominations, including for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Miniseries" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Miniseries">Outstanding Limited Series</a>, <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Writing_for_a_Miniseries,_Movie_or_a_Dramatic_Special" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Miniseries, Movie or a Dramatic Special">Writing for a Miniseries, Movie or a Dramatic Special</a> (Ridley), <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actress_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie">Lead Actress in a Miniseries or Movie</a> (Huffman), <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Lead_Actor_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Miniseries or a Movie">Lead Actor in a Miniseries or Movie</a> (Hutton), <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actor_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Miniseries or a Movie">Supporting Actor in a Miniseries or Movie</a> (Cabral), and a win for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actress_in_a_Miniseries_or_a_Movie" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie">Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or Movie</a> (King).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Crime_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first season takes place in <a href="/wiki/Modesto,_California" title="Modesto, California">Modesto, California</a>, where a war veteran becomes the victim of a home invasion gone wrong, and the lives of four people are forever changed after each one is connected to the crime.</p>
<p>The second season takes place in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis, Indiana</a>, where the captains of a private school's championship basketball team are accused of sexually assaulting a classmate and posting photographs of the incident online.</p>
<p>The third season takes place in <a href="/wiki/Alamance_County,_North_Carolina" title="Alamance County, North Carolina">Alamance County, North Carolina</a>, where five people struggle to survive in a place where the American Dream comes with a price.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Crime_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_casting">Development and casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Crime_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Development and casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In October 2013, ABC announced it was developing a drama <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> created by <a href="/wiki/John_Ridley" title="John Ridley">John Ridley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> In January 2014, ABC greenlighted the pilot, and signed a deal with Ridley to direct.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Elvis_Nolasco" title="Elvis Nolasco">Elvis Nolasco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caitlin_Gerard" title="Caitlin Gerard">Caitlin Gerard</a> were the first regular members to be cast, as announced on February 14, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> Later <a href="/wiki/Richard_Cabral" title="Richard Cabral">Richard Cabral</a>, Johnny Ortiz, <a href="/wiki/Benito_Martinez_(actor)" title="Benito Martinez (actor)">Benito Martinez</a> and <a href="/wiki/W._Earl_Brown" title="W. Earl Brown">W. Earl Brown</a> were cast for regular supporting roles in the pilot.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[47]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In early March, <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Hutton" title="Timothy Hutton">Timothy Hutton</a> was cast in a lead role as Russ, and <a href="/wiki/Penelope_Ann_Miller" title="Penelope Ann Miller">Penelope Ann Miller</a> signed on in a supporting role of the mother of the murdered man's wife.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">[50]</a></sup> Soon thereafter, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Felicity_Huffman" title="Felicity Huffman">Felicity Huffman</a> was cast in the lead role of Barb Skokie, Russ' stoic ex-wife and mother of the murdered young man.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Crime_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The pilot episode, along with further episodes, was filmed in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>, which substitutes for the <a href="/wiki/Central_Valley_(California)" title="Central Valley (California)">California Central Valley</a> city of <a href="/wiki/Modesto,_California" title="Modesto, California">Modesto</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup> On May 8, 2014, ABC picked up the pilot to the series for the 2014–15 television season.<sup id="cite_ref-andreeva508_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-andreeva508-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-variety508_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-variety508-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>Filming for the series moved to <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a> with the third season, as the show's production was awarded a California tax credit.<sup id="cite_ref-s3details_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-s3details-13">[13]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Crime_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=American_Crime_(TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>American Crime</i> has been met with wide acclaim from critics with many praising the writing, directing, and the acting abilities of its cast, particularly <a href="/wiki/Felicity_Huffman" title="Felicity Huffman">Felicity Huffman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Regina_King" title="Regina King">Regina King</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> gave the first season a rating of 95% based on 42 reviews with an average rating of 8.32 out of 10. The site's critical consensus reads, "Raw, emotional portrayals of diverse characters in dire pain, mashed up with chilling narratives and a gutsy attitude make <i>American Crime</i> a must-see."<sup id="cite_ref-rtmt_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rtmt-55">[55]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gave the first season a score of 85 out of 100, based on 35 reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com">Deadline.com</a> critic Dominic Patten said in his review: "the 11-episode limited series marks a resounding flag planting by network television and tells cable that it doesn’t own poignant drama on our small screens."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup> Critic Ed Bark praised the performance of Felicity Huffman and wrote that she would be a favorite to win at <a href="/wiki/67th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="67th Primetime Emmy Awards">next year's Primetime Emmy Awards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">[58]</a></sup></p>
<p>The second season continued to receive critical acclaim from critics. On Rotten Tomatoes, the season has a 97% rating based on 35 reviews with an average score of 8.34 out of 10. The site's critical consensus reads, "<i>American Crime</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s intense second season infuses a complicated, topical story with genuine emotion, and patiently allows its narrative arc to develop without sacrificing momentum."<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> On Metacritic, it has a score of 85 out of 100 based on 26 reviews.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup></p>
<p>The third season has received even higher acclaim than its first two seasons. On Rotten Tomatoes, it has a 100% rating, based on 26 reviews with an average score of 8.9 out of 10. The site's critical consensus reads, "<i>American Crime</i> offers a unique anthology series filled with surprising revelations and compelling inter-connected narratives that opt for original, emotional human commentary instead of tired arguments over current events."<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup> On Metacritic, it has a score of 90 out of 100, based on 26 reviews, indicating "universal acclaim".<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup> Verne Gay of <i><a href="/wiki/Newsday" title="Newsday">Newsday</a></i> calls it "Another brilliant, powerful, moving season of one of TV's best."<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup></p>
