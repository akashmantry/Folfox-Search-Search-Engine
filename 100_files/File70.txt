<p><b>Ameristar Casinos, Inc.</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casino</a> operator based in <a href="/wiki/Paradise,_Nevada" title="Paradise, Nevada">Paradise, Nevada</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> It was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Pinnacle_Entertainment" title="Pinnacle Entertainment">Pinnacle Entertainment</a> in 2013. The company had eight properties in seven markets.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Casinos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The roots of Ameristar Casinos date to 1954 when <a href="/wiki/Cactus_Pete_Piersanti" title="Cactus Pete Piersanti">"Cactus Pete" Piersanti</a> opened Cactus Petes Desert Lodge on <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_93" title="U.S. Route 93">U.S. Route 93</a> just south of the <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a> border in <a href="/wiki/Elko_County,_Nevada" title="Elko County, Nevada">Elko County</a>. The area would later become <a href="/wiki/Jackpot,_Nevada" title="Jackpot, Nevada">Jackpot</a>, Nevada. In 1956, Piersanti and others incorporated the property as <b>Cactus Petes, Inc.</b></p>
<p>The corporation took over management of the neighboring Horseshu Club in 1964. Cactus Petes came under the partial control of Ray Neilsen of <a href="/wiki/Twin_Falls,_Idaho" title="Twin Falls, Idaho">Twin Falls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Idaho" title="Idaho">Idaho</a>, in 1967. Neilsen's construction company had built many of the structures at <a href="/wiki/Cactus_Pete%27s" title="Cactus Pete's">Cactus Pete's</a>. Neilsen's son, Craig, assumed control of day-to-day operations upon the elder Neilsen's death in 1971. <b>Craig H. Neilsen</b> became sole owner in 1984.</p>
<p>A November 1985 automobile accident left Craig H. Neilsen a <a href="/wiki/Quadriplegic" class="mw-redirect" title="Quadriplegic">quadriplegic</a>, but he continued to lead the company and expanded it. Neilsen was named “Best Performing CEO” by the <a href="/wiki/American_Gaming_Association" title="American Gaming Association">American Gaming Association</a> in 2002, and was inducted into the organization’s “Hall of Fame” in 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Craig H. Neilsen remained chairman and CEO until his unexpected death on November 19, 2006.</p>
<p>Ray H. Neilsen succeeded his father, serving as co-chairman with John Boushy, who also was promoted from president to CEO at the time of Craig’s death.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Boushy resigned in June 2008 and was succeeded by Gordon Kanofsky, who previously served as the company's executive vice president, and now holds the dual role of CEO and vice chairman. Ray H. was named chairman at the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2013, <a href="/wiki/Pinnacle_Entertainment" title="Pinnacle Entertainment">Pinnacle Entertainment</a> bought Ameristar for $869 million plus $1.9 billion in assumed debt.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> The company had reportedly been "on the sales block" since Craig Neilsen's death.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion">Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Casinos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG/220px-Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG/330px-Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG/440px-Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2550" data-file-height="2304" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ameristar_Black_Hawk.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
The Ameristar Resort Casino Spa, opened October 2009, in Black Hawk, Colorado</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1992 the company made its first investment outside of Jackpot by acquiring a <a href="/wiki/Vicksburg,_Mississippi" title="Vicksburg, Mississippi">Vicksburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">Mississippi</a>, property known as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Delta_Pointe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Delta Pointe (page does not exist)">Delta Pointe</a>. This property was opened in 1994 as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Casino_Vicksburg&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ameristar Casino Vicksburg (page does not exist)">Ameristar Casino Vicksburg</a>. The company subsequently constructed an adjoining hotel, and in 2008, completed a $100 million expansion of the facility.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> Ameristar opened the first of several riverboat casinos in <a href="/wiki/Council_Bluffs,_Iowa" title="Council Bluffs, Iowa">Council Bluffs, Iowa</a>, in 1996. In 1998, the company opened the Reserve Hotel Casino in <a href="/wiki/Henderson,_Nevada" title="Henderson, Nevada">Henderson</a>, Nevada.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>The company was renamed Ameristar Casinos, Inc. and went public in 1993, on the NASDAQ Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol "ASCA." Company headquarters was moved from Twin Falls to Las Vegas in 1996.</p>
<p>In 2000, Ameristar purchased properties in Kansas City, Missouri, and St. Charles, Missouri, from <a href="/wiki/Station_Casinos,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Station Casinos, Inc.">Station Casinos, Inc.</a>, and in 2001, sold The Reserve Hotel and Casino to Station Casinos, which renamed it <a href="/wiki/Fiesta_Henderson" title="Fiesta Henderson">Fiesta Henderson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2004, Ameristar acquired the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mountain_High_Casino&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mountain High Casino (page does not exist)">Mountain High Casino</a> in <a href="/wiki/Black_Hawk,_Colorado" title="Black Hawk, Colorado">Black Hawk, Colorado</a>. In 2006, the Ameristar brand debuted after an $80 million renovation and expansion project that included additional covered parking; and announced details of its planned 33-story hotel and spa. Ameristar officially opened its new Casino Resort Spa complex October 2009 to become the first destination resort casino in Colorado. The property's $230 million expansion included a 33-story luxury hotel and day spa, an enclosed rooftop swimming pool and 15,000 square feet (1,400&#160;m<sup>2</sup>) of meeting and event space. The 33-story hotel featuring 536 rooms and 64 suites became the tallest structure between Salt Lake City, Utah and Denver.</p>
<p>Ameristar also completed the acquisition of the <a href="/wiki/Resorts_East_Chicago" class="mw-redirect" title="Resorts East Chicago">Resorts East Chicago</a> casino in <a href="/wiki/East_Chicago,_Indiana" title="East Chicago, Indiana">East Chicago, Indiana</a> from <a href="/wiki/Resorts_International_Holdings,_LLC" class="mw-redirect" title="Resorts International Holdings, LLC">Resorts International Holdings</a> in 2007. It was rebranded <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Casino_Hotel_East_Chicago&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ameristar Casino Hotel East Chicago (page does not exist)">Ameristar Casino Hotel East Chicago</a> in June 2008. An outdoor summer concert series promoted the rebranding, and featured <a href="/wiki/Lionel_Richie" title="Lionel Richie">Lionel Richie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smokey_Robinson" title="Smokey Robinson">Smokey Robinson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reba_McEntire" title="Reba McEntire">Reba McEntire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, Ameristar announced plans to renovate all of Ameristar East Chicago's 280 superior guest rooms and 10 luxury suites; and to add 100 rooms and a fitness center to Ameristar Kansas City's existing luxury hotel. The addition will complement the property's existing 184 rooms and hotel lobby, which were extensively renovated in September 2006.</p>
<p>In Massachusetts, where casinos were legalized in November 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> Ameristar applied for the one gaming license allotted to <a href="/wiki/Western_Massachusetts" title="Western Massachusetts">Western Massachusetts</a>, proposing to build a $910-million hotel and casino.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> It acquired a 41-acre site in <a href="/wiki/East_Springfield,_Springfield,_Massachusetts" title="East Springfield, Springfield, Massachusetts">East Springfield</a>, a former <a href="/wiki/Westinghouse_Electric_(1886)" class="mw-redirect" title="Westinghouse Electric (1886)">Westinghouse</a> plant, for $16 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In November 2012, however, Ameristar withdrew its proposal in the face of competing bids from <a href="/wiki/MGM_Resorts_International" title="MGM Resorts International">MGM Resorts International</a> and <a href="/wiki/Penn_National_Gaming" title="Penn National Gaming">Penn National Gaming</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> saying that city officials were more likely to endorse a downtown casino.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>
<p>In March 2012, Ameristar agreed to acquire Creative Casinos, developer of the proposed <a href="/wiki/Mojito_Pointe" class="mw-redirect" title="Mojito Pointe">Mojito Pointe</a> casino resort in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Charles,_Louisiana" title="Lake Charles, Louisiana">Lake Charles, Louisiana</a>, for $32.5 million.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Ameristar planned to spend at least $500 million on the resort, and to open it in mid-2014.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Philanthropy">Philanthropy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Casinos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Philanthropy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the death of Craig H. Neilsen, his estate endowed the foundation in his name. Ameristar regularly partners with the Craig H. Neilsen Foundation to support the rehabilitation of individuals suffering from spinal cord injuries and disease. Ray H. Neilsen serves as Chairman, and Co-Trustee with Kanofsky.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> In addition, Ameristar was one of the first companies to help fund <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">research</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Nevada_Cancer_Institute" title="Nevada Cancer Institute">Nevada Cancer Institute</a> by making a $500,000 donation in 2004.</p>
<p>Ameristar also sponsors an active workplace giving campaign known as <b>Ameristar Cares.</b> In 2012, the company and its employees raised almost $7 million for charitable organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial">Financial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ameristar_Casinos&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Financial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Although the current economic recession has hurt the financial performance of casinos on the Las Vegas Strip, Ameristar and other regional casino operators have fared better, as customers opt for gaming and entertainment destinations closer to home.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tradingmarkets.com_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tradingmarkets.com-24">[24]</a></sup></p>
<p>Ameristar's properties in Kansas City and <a href="/wiki/St._Charles,_Missouri" title="St. Charles, Missouri">St. Charles, Missouri</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Black_Hawk,_Colorado" title="Black Hawk, Colorado">Black Hawk, Colorado</a>, benefitted from casino-related regulatory reform approved by voters in 2008-09. In Missouri, voters repealed the state’s loss limit, which had capped players’ purchases of table game chips and slot machine credits at $500 every two hours. In Black Hawk, voters approved a measure that increased betting limits and operating hours, and added the games of craps and roulette.<sup id="cite_ref-tradingmarkets.com_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tradingmarkets.com-24">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
