<p><b>Archduke Joseph August Viktor Klemens Maria of Austria, Prince of Hungary and Bohemia</b> (9 August 1872 – 6 July 1962) was for a short period <a href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_Hungary" title="List of heads of state of Hungary">head of state</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. He was a member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg-Lorraine" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Habsburg-Lorraine">House of Habsburg-Lorraine</a>, the eldest son of <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph_Karl_of_Austria" title="Archduke Joseph Karl of Austria">Archduke Joseph Karl of Austria</a> (1833–1905) and his wife Princess Clotilde of <a href="/wiki/Saxe-Coburg-Gotha" class="mw-redirect" title="Saxe-Coburg-Gotha">Saxe-Coburg-Gotha</a> (1846–1927). Joseph August's grandfather had been <a href="/wiki/Palatine_Joseph_of_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="Palatine Joseph of Hungary">Palatine Joseph of Hungary</a> (1776–1847), Palatine and Viceroy of Hungary, a younger son of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Leopold_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Emperor Leopold II">Emperor Leopold II</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph_(diamond)" title="Archduke Joseph (diamond)">Archduke Joseph Diamond</a>, a 76.02 carat colourless diamond with internal flawless clarity, is named after the Archduke and officially recorded as his property.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archduke_Joseph_August_of_Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was born at <a href="/wiki/Alcs%C3%BAtdoboz" title="Alcsútdoboz">Alcsút</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. On 15 November 1893, in <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a>, he married <a href="/wiki/Princess_Auguste_Maria_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Princess Auguste Maria of Bavaria">Princess Augusta Maria Louise of Bavaria</a> (1875–1964), daughter of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Leopold_of_Bavaria" title="Prince Leopold of Bavaria">Prince Leopold of Bavaria</a> (1846–1930) and his wife <a href="/wiki/Archduchess_Gisela_of_Austria" title="Archduchess Gisela of Austria">Archduchess Gisela of Austria</a> (1856–1932).</p>
<p>Archduke Joseph August became thus from 1893 "grandson-in-law" to <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Francis_Joseph" class="mw-redirect" title="Emperor Francis Joseph">Emperor Francis Joseph</a>. His wife's mother Archduchess Gisela was the eldest surviving daughter of Austrian Emperor <a href="/wiki/Franz_Joseph_I_of_Austria" title="Franz Joseph I of Austria">Francis Joseph</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elisabeth_of_Bavaria" class="mw-redirect" title="Elisabeth of Bavaria">Empress Elisabeth ("Sisi")</a>. The young couple's children, were born in their great-grandfather's lifetime. They have six children:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph_Francis_of_Austria" title="Archduke Joseph Francis of Austria">Archduke Joseph Francis</a> (1895–1957)</li>
<li>Archduchess Gisela Auguste (1897–1901)</li>
<li>Archduchess Sophie Klementine (1899–1978)</li>
<li>Archduke Ladislaus Luitpold (1901–1946)</li>
<li>Archduke Matthias Joseph (1904–1905)</li>
<li>Archduchess Magdalena Maria (1909–2000)</li>
</ul>
<p>Joseph August began his military career in 1890 when he was commissioned into the 1st Infantry Regiment as a <a href="/wiki/Leutnant" title="Leutnant">Leutnant</a>. He was soon promoted to <a href="/wiki/Oberleutnant" title="Oberleutnant">Oberleutnant</a> and was transferred to 72nd Infantry Regiment in 1893. He was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Dragoon" title="Dragoon">Dragoon</a> Regiment #6 in 1894 and then transferred to the 1st <a href="/wiki/Royal_Hungarian_Landwehr" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Hungarian Landwehr">Honvéd</a> <a href="/wiki/Hussar" title="Hussar">Hussars</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Kaiser" title="Kaiser">Kaiser</a> and promoted to the rank of <a href="/wiki/Major" title="Major">Major</a>. He took command of this regiment in 1904 and then went on to command 79th Honvéd infantry brigade in 1908 then finally the 31st infantry division at <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> in 1911.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archduke_Joseph_August_of_Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>August was highly decorated before <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> broke out. Some of his awards include The Bronze <a href="/wiki/Military_Merit_Medal_(Austria-Hungary)" title="Military Merit Medal (Austria-Hungary)">Military Merit Medal</a>, Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saint_Joseph" title="Order of Saint Joseph">Order of Saint Joseph</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Marianer_Cross_of_the_Deutscher_Ritterorden" class="mw-redirect" title="Marianer Cross of the Deutscher Ritterorden">Marianer Cross of the Deutscher Ritterorden</a>, The <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Black_Eagle" title="Order of the Black Eagle">Order of the Black Eagle</a>, The 1st Class of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_Red_Eagle" title="Order of the Red Eagle">Order of the Red Eagle</a>, The Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victorian_Order" title="Royal Victorian Order">Royal Victorian Order</a>, the Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Charles_III" title="Order of Charles III">Order of Charles III</a>, and the Grand Cross of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Saints_Cyril_and_Methodius" title="Order of Saints Cyril and Methodius">Order of Saints Cyril and Methodius</a>.</p>
<p>In 1914 he was involved in combat in the <a href="/wiki/Galicia_(Central_Europe)" class="mw-redirect" title="Galicia (Central Europe)">Galician</a> theatre and took command of the VII Corps and was involved in fighting in the <a href="/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains" title="Carpathian Mountains">Carpathian Mountains</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> became involved in the war he was transferred to the <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Carinthia" title="Duchy of Carinthia">Carinthian</a> border and involved in fighting the <a href="/wiki/Isonzo" class="mw-redirect" title="Isonzo">Isonzo</a> army. August remained on this front until the 9th battle of the Isonzo in 1916 a period in which once again he was highly decorated. August was highly liked by his troops, especially those of <a href="/wiki/Magyars" class="mw-redirect" title="Magyars">Hungarian</a> nationality.</p>
<p>In November 1916, August was put in command of the Heeresfront fighting against <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russian</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romanian</a> forces. In January 1918 he was put in command of the <a href="/wiki/Sixth_Army_(Austria-Hungary)" class="mw-redirect" title="Sixth Army (Austria-Hungary)">6th Army</a> in the Southern theatre and that July took over the <a href="/wiki/South_Tyrolean_Army_Group" title="South Tyrolean Army Group">South Tyrolean Army Group</a>, which was the 10th and 11th Armies. Finally, on 26 October 1918, he was sent to the Balkan theatre to take command of the Heeresgruppe Kövess, which had lost Serbia, Albania and Montenegro by then. He was the last person to be appointed a <i><a href="/wiki/List_of_Austrian_field_marshals" title="List of Austrian field marshals">Feldmarschall</a></i> (<a href="/wiki/Field_Marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Field Marshal">Field Marshal</a>) of the <a href="/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Army" title="Austro-Hungarian Army">Austro-Hungarian Army</a> on 24 October 1918, as an attempt by <a href="/wiki/Kaiser" title="Kaiser">Kaiser</a> <a href="/wiki/Karl_I_of_Austria" class="mw-redirect" title="Karl I of Austria">Karl</a> to calm Hungarian nationalists.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_war">Post war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archduke_Joseph_August_of_Austria&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 27 October 1918 Emperor Karl made August the "Homo Regius" of <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>, but August asked to be released from his oath of allegiance from the Kaiser. He then began negotiations and appointed Graf <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A1nos_Hadik" title="János Hadik">János Hadik</a> to build a new national government. However the <a href="/wiki/Aster_Revolution" title="Aster Revolution">Aster Revolution</a> broke out on 31 October 1918, deterring his plans. In November, the <a href="/wiki/Socialist" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist">socialist</a> <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Democratic_Republic" class="mw-redirect" title="Hungarian Democratic Republic">Hungarian Democratic Republic</a> was proclaimed, only to be replaced a few months later by the <a href="/wiki/Communist" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist">communist</a> <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Soviet_Republic" title="Hungarian Soviet Republic">Hungarian Soviet Republic</a>. This revolution was to fail: the popular August survived unharmed and once again became the head of state as "Reichsverweser" (regent) and appointed a Prime Minister. Since the Allied forces declined to accept Archduke Joseph, a Habsburg, as Hungary's head of state, he was forced to resign on 23 August 1919.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> In 1920 the Archduke became the first knight of the Hungarian <a href="/wiki/Vit%C3%A9z" class="mw-redirect" title="Vitéz">Order of Vitéz</a>, in 1927 he became a member of the newly established House of Lords. He later became an honorary member of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences and was its president from 1936-1944. He fled Hungary for the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in 1944 but later returned to <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. He died in 1962 at <a href="/wiki/Rain,_Lower_Bavaria" title="Rain, Lower Bavaria">Rain</a> near <a href="/wiki/Straubing" title="Straubing">Straubing</a>.</p>
<p>His eldest son <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph_Francis_of_Austria" title="Archduke Joseph Francis of Austria">Archduke Joseph Francis of Austria</a> had predeceased him, dying in 1957.</p>
<p>Thus Joseph August's main heir was his eldest grandson <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph_Arpad_of_Austria" class="mw-redirect" title="Archduke Joseph Arpad of Austria">Archduke Joseph Arpad of Austria</a> (born 1933), the eldest son of Joseph Francis and his wife <a href="/wiki/Princess_Anna_of_Saxony_(1903%E2%80%931976)" title="Princess Anna of Saxony (1903–1976)">Princess Anna of Saxony</a>. Joseph Arpad is married to <a href="/wiki/Princess_Maria_of_L%C3%B6wenstein-Wertheim-Rosenberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Princess Maria of Löwenstein-Wertheim-Rosenberg">Princess Maria of Löwenstein-Wertheim-Rosenberg</a>, and had children in Joseph August's lifetime. His eldest son is <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph_Karl_of_Austria" title="Archduke Joseph Karl of Austria">Archduke Joseph Karl of Austria</a> (born 1960).</p>
<p>Joseph August's granddaughter <a href="/wiki/Archduchess_Ilona_of_Austria" title="Archduchess Ilona of Austria">Archduchess Ilona of Austria</a> (b. 1927) married <a href="/wiki/Duke_Georg_Alexander_of_Mecklenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Duke Georg Alexander of Mecklenburg">George Alexander, Duke of Mecklenburg</a>. Her son <a href="/wiki/George_Borwin,_Duke_of_Mecklenburg" class="mw-redirect" title="George Borwin, Duke of Mecklenburg">George Borwin, Duke of Mecklenburg</a> is the current head of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Mecklenburg-Strelitz" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Mecklenburg-Strelitz">House of Mecklenburg-Strelitz</a>.</p>
