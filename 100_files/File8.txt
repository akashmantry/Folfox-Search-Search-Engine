<p>The <b>41st Primetime Emmy Awards</b> were held on Sunday, September 17, 1989. The ceremony was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Pasadena_Convention_Center" title="Pasadena Convention Center">Pasadena Civic Auditorium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pasadena,_California" title="Pasadena, California">Pasadena, California</a>. The ceremony saw the guest acting categories double, as they were now based on gender as well as genre. Two networks, <a href="/wiki/Lifetime_(TV_network)" title="Lifetime (TV network)">Lifetime</a>, and <a href="/wiki/USA_(TV_network)" class="mw-redirect" title="USA (TV network)">USA</a>, received their first major nominations this year.</p>
<p>After being nominated and losing the previous four years <i><a href="/wiki/Cheers" title="Cheers">Cheers</a></i> regained the title of <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series">Outstanding Comedy Series</a>. <i><a href="/wiki/L.A._Law" title="L.A. Law">L.A. Law</a></i> also won <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Drama Series">Outstanding Drama Series</a> after losing the previous year. For the second straight year, <i>L.A. Law</i> received 15 major nominations, making it the first show ever to receive more than 14 major nominations multiple times. With nine main cast acting nominations, <i>L.A. Law</i> tied the record set by <i><a href="/wiki/Hill_Street_Blues" title="Hill Street Blues">Hill Street Blues</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/34th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="34th Primetime Emmy Awards">1982</a>.</p>
