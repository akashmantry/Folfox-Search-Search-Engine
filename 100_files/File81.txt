<p><a href="/wiki/Archduke" title="Archduke">Archduke</a> <b>Stephen Francis Victor</b> (<a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>: <span lang="de" xml:lang="de"><i>Stephan Franz Viktor</i></span>, <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <span lang="hu" xml:lang="hu"><i>István nádor</i></span>; 14 September 1817, in <a href="/wiki/Buda,_Hungary" class="mw-redirect" title="Buda, Hungary">Buda</a> – 19 February 1867, in <a href="/wiki/Menton,_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Menton, France">Menton</a>) was a member of the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Habsburg-Lorraine" class="mw-redirect" title="House of Habsburg-Lorraine">House of Habsburg-Lorraine</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Palatine_of_Hungary" title="Palatine of Hungary">Palatine of Hungary</a> from 1847 to 1848.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archduke_Stephen,_Palatine_of_Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>He was the son of <a href="/wiki/Archduke_Joseph,_Palatine_of_Hungary" title="Archduke Joseph, Palatine of Hungary">Archduke Joseph, Palatine of Hungary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hermine_of_Anhalt-Bernburg-Schaumburg-Hoym" class="mw-redirect" title="Hermine of Anhalt-Bernburg-Schaumburg-Hoym">Hermine of Anhalt-Bernburg-Schaumburg-Hoym</a>. His mother died shortly after giving birth to him and his twin sister, <a href="/wiki/Archduchess_Hermine_of_Austria" title="Archduchess Hermine of Austria">Archduchess Hermine of Austria</a>. He was brought up by his stepmother, <a href="/wiki/Maria_Dorothea_of_W%C3%BCrttemberg" class="mw-redirect" title="Maria Dorothea of Württemberg">Maria Dorothea of Württemberg</a>.</p>
<p>He spent much of his childhood in <a href="/wiki/Buda" title="Buda">Buda</a> and at the family estate in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Alcs%C3%BAt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Alcsút (page does not exist)">Alcsút</a> and received an excellent education. He was mainly interested in <a href="/wiki/Political_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Political Science">Political Science</a>, which he also studied later in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Military_career">Military career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Archduke_Stephen,_Palatine_of_Hungary&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Military career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>He gained the rank of <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_Field_Marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Lieutenant Field Marshal">Lieutenant Field Marshal</a> in the service of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Empire" title="Austrian Empire">Austrian</a> Army. From 1839 until 1841 he was a courteor at Vienna. In 1841 he travelled through the different countries of the monarchy, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Bohemia" title="Kingdom of Bohemia">Kingdom of Bohemia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Lombardy%E2%80%93Venetia" title="Kingdom of Lombardy–Venetia">Kingdom of Lombardy–Venetia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/County_of_Tyrol" title="County of Tyrol">Tyrol</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Piedmont-Sardinia" class="mw-redirect" title="Kingdom of Piedmont-Sardinia">Kingdom of Piedmont-Sardinia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Papal_States" title="Papal States">Papal States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Modena" class="mw-redirect" title="Duchy of Modena">Modena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany" title="Grand Duchy of Tuscany">Tuscany</a>. In 1843 Emperor <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_Austria" title="Ferdinand I of Austria">Ferdinand I of Austria</a> appointed him governor of Bohemia. He stayed in that capacity until, in January 1847, his father died and he inherited the title of Count <a href="/wiki/Palatine_of_Hungary" title="Palatine of Hungary">Palatine of Hungary</a>, from 12 November 1847 to 26 September 1848.</p>
<p>He was the last of the Counts Palatine, as he died unmarried and without heirs.</p>
