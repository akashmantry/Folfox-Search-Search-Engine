<p><b>Armenian Genocide Remembrance Day</b> (<a href="/wiki/Armenian_language" title="Armenian language">Armenian</a>: <span lang="hy" xml:lang="hy">Մեծ Եղեռնի զոհերի հիշատակի օր</span> <i>Mets Yegherrni zoheri hishataki or</i>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Геноцид армян День памяти</span>, <i>Genotsid armyan Den' pamyati</i>; <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>: <span lang="tr" xml:lang="tr"><i>Ermeni Soykırımı Anma Günü</i></span>) or <b>Armenian Genocide Memorial Day</b><sup id="cite_ref-adam_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adam-1">[1]</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Armenia" title="Public holidays in Armenia">national holiday</a> in <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_Republic" title="Nagorno-Karabakh Republic">Nagorno-Karabakh Republic</a> and is observed by the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_diaspora" title="Armenian diaspora">Armenian diaspora</a> on April 24.<sup id="cite_ref-adam_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adam-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> It is held annually to commemorate the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a> of 1915. In <a href="/wiki/Yerevan" title="Yerevan">Yerevan</a>, the capital of <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, hundreds of thousands of people walk to the <a href="/wiki/Tsitsernakaberd" title="Tsitsernakaberd">Tsitsernakaberd Genocide Memorial</a> to lay flowers at the <a href="/wiki/Eternal_flame" title="Eternal flame">eternal flame</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Armenian_Genocide_Remembrance_Day&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">

<p>The date 24 April commemorates the <a href="/wiki/Deportation_of_Armenian_intellectuals_on_24_April_1915" title="Deportation of Armenian intellectuals on 24 April 1915">deportation of Armenian intellectuals on 24 April 1915</a> from Constantinople (current Istanbul, Turkey). The first commemoration, organized by a group of Armenian Genocide survivors, was held in Istanbul in 1919 at the local St. Trinity Armenian church.<sup id="cite_ref-museum_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museum-3">[3]</a></sup> Many prominent figures in the Armenian community participated in the commemoration. Following its initial commemoration in 1919, the date became the annual day of remembrance for the Armenian Genocide.<sup id="cite_ref-museum_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-museum-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>On 9 April 1975, the <a href="/wiki/US_House_of_Representatives" class="mw-redirect" title="US House of Representatives">US House of Representatives</a> passed Joint Resolution 148 designating 24 April as a <i>National Day of Remembrance of Man’s Inhumanity to Man</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The Resolution commemorated the victims of genocide, <i>especially those of Armenian ancestry who succumbed to the genocide perpetrated in 1915,</i> The resolution however failed to pass in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Senate_Judiciary_Committee" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee">U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee</a> due to President <a href="/wiki/Gerald_R._Ford" class="mw-redirect" title="Gerald R. Ford">Gerald R. Ford</a>’s strong opposition to what he saw as a threat to the country's strategic alliance with Turkey.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 1988, <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Armenia" class="mw-redirect" title="Soviet Armenia">Soviet Armenia</a> formally adopted 24 April as a public day of commemoration.<sup id="cite_ref-Bloxham_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloxham-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:215</sup></p>
<p>In 1997 the <a href="/wiki/California_State_Assembly" title="California State Assembly">California State Assembly</a> declared 24 April as a <i>Day of Remembrance for the Armenian Genocide of 1915–1923, and for the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Sumgait_Pogroms" class="mw-redirect" title="Sumgait Pogroms">Sumgait Pogroms</a> of 1988 and <a href="/wiki/Pogrom_of_Armenians_in_Baku" class="mw-redirect" title="Pogrom of Armenians in Baku">Baku Riots</a> of 1990</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bloxham_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloxham-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">:232</sup></p>
