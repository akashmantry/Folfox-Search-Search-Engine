<p><b>Arnold Arre</b> (born September 2, 1971 in <a href="/wiki/Metro_Manila" title="Metro Manila">Metro Manila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>) is a <a href="/wiki/Filipino_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino people">Filipino</a> comic book writer, <a href="/wiki/Comic_book_artist" class="mw-redirect" title="Comic book artist">artist</a> and self-taught <a href="/wiki/Animator" title="Animator">animator</a> best known for his graphic novels <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mythology_Class" title="The Mythology Class">The Mythology Class</a></i> (1999) and <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ang_Mundo_ni_Andong_Agimat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat (page does not exist)">Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat</a></i> (2006).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Arnold_Arre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Arnold Arre has won <a href="/wiki/Philippine_National_Book_Awards" title="Philippine National Book Awards">National Book Awards</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Manila_Critics_Circle" class="mw-redirect" title="Manila Critics Circle">Manila Critics Circle</a> for his <a href="/wiki/Graphic_novels" class="mw-redirect" title="Graphic novels">graphic novels</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mythology_Class" title="The Mythology Class">The Mythology Class</a></i> (1999), a four-part action-adventure miniseries and <i><a href="/wiki/Trip_to_Tagaytay" title="Trip to Tagaytay">Trip to Tagaytay</a></i> (2000), a one-shot future fiction short story.<sup id="cite_ref-ncca_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncca-1">[1]</a></sup> <i>The Mythology Class</i>, which has been described as "genre-breaking",<sup id="cite_ref-rodski-inquirer_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rodski-inquirer-2">[2]</a></sup> has the distinction of being the first graphic novel to win in the Manila Critics Circle National Book Awards Comic Books category.<sup id="cite_ref-ncca_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ncca-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arre's other titles include the romantic comedy <i>After Eden</i> (2002), <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ang_Mundo_ni_Andong_Agimat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat (page does not exist)">Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat</a></i> (2006), and "Martial Law Babies" (2008).</p>
<p>Aside from his comics work, Arre did numerous design and illustration jobs for various clients such as the <a href="/wiki/San_Miguel_Foundation_for_the_Performing_Arts" title="San Miguel Foundation for the Performing Arts">San Miguel Foundation for the Performing Arts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sony_BMG" title="Sony BMG">Sony BMG</a> Music Entertainment Philippines. He also took part in local and international group exhibits and has had a one-man fantasy-themed show, <i>Mythos</i> in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2007, producer Tony Gloria of Unitel Productions optioned the film rights to Arre's novel <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ang_Mundo_ni_Andong_Agimat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat (page does not exist)">Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-unitel_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unitel-5">[5]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-adobo_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adobo-6">[6]</a></sup> Arre has mentioned in an April 2014 interview that the project is still under development.<sup id="cite_ref-komikon_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-komikon-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, Arre studied the art of <a href="/wiki/Animation" title="Animation">animation</a> and made a 4-minute short film titled <i>Andong Agimat: Kanya ang Kalye</i> based on the main character in his book <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ang_Mundo_ni_Andong_Agimat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat (page does not exist)">Ang Mundo ni Andong Agimat</a></i>. In November 2011 he was commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Gang_Badoy" title="Gang Badoy">Gang Badoy</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rock_Ed_Philippines&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rock Ed Philippines (page does not exist)">Rock Ed Philippines</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Historical_Commission_of_the_Philippines" title="National Historical Commission of the Philippines">National Historical Commission of the Philippines</a> (NHCP) to make an animated music video for <i>Kaninong Anino</i> in celebration of the 150th birth anniversary of Filipino hero <a href="/wiki/Jose_Rizal" class="mw-redirect" title="Jose Rizal">Jose Rizal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> The following year, he was commissioned by Rock Ed Philippines and the NHCP to make an animated music video for <i><a href="/wiki/Lupang_Hinirang" title="Lupang Hinirang">Lupang Hinirang</a></i>, the Philippine National Anthem which was broadcast on national television on June 12, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Philippines)" title="Independence Day (Philippines)">Independence Day (Philippines)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> In September 2012, he did a series of educational animated videos titled <i>Tandaan. Kalayaan. Alagaan.</i> to mark the 40th anniversary of the Philippines' freedom from Martial Law. The series was commissioned by Rock Ed Philippines and the <a href="/wiki/National_Youth_Commission_(Philippines)" title="National Youth Commission (Philippines)">National Youth Commission (Philippines)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>In July 2013, Arre finished his first 20-minute animated short film titled <i>Milkyboy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-manifesto_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-manifesto-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gma-milkyboy_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gma-milkyboy-12">[12]</a></sup> The film went on to win awards at the 25th Gawad CCP Para Sa Alternatibong Pelikula in November 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-gawad_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gawad-13">[13]</a></sup> the 7th <a href="/wiki/Animahenasyon" title="Animahenasyon">Animahenasyon</a> (Philippine Animation Festival) in November 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-animahenasyon_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-animahenasyon-14">[14]</a></sup> and the 30th <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Asian_Pacific_Film_Festival" title="Los Angeles Asian Pacific Film Festival">Los Angeles Asian Pacific Film Festival</a> (LAAPFF) in May 2014 where Arre was awarded the <i>Linda Mabalot New Directors/New Visions Award</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-laapff_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laapff-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hollywoodtimes_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hollywoodtimes-16">[16]</a></sup> presented to a short film that demonstrates innovative and original use of cinematic language and vision.<sup id="cite_ref-mabalot_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mabalot-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>Arre is based in <a href="/wiki/Quezon_City" title="Quezon City">Quezon City</a> and is married to <a href="/wiki/Graphic_designer" title="Graphic designer">graphic designer</a> Cynthia Bauzon.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
