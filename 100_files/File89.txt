<p>The <b>Kansas City Cowboys</b> were a <a href="/wiki/National_League" title="National League">National League</a> baseball team that played one season in <a href="/wiki/1886_in_baseball" title="1886 in baseball">1886</a>. They played at Association Park and finished with a 30-91 record. They finished in seventh place, ahead of another new team, the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Nationals_(1886-89)" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington Nationals (1886-89)">Washington Nationals</a>. They were not connected to the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Cowboys_(Union_Association)" title="Kansas City Cowboys (Union Association)">Union Association Cowboys</a>.</p>
<p>The Cowboys were admitted to the National League on a trial basis for the 1886 season.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The team went out of business in February, 1887, having been forced to sell its players back to the league for $6,000. They were replaced in the league by the <a href="/wiki/Pittsburg_Alleghenys" class="mw-redirect" title="Pittsburg Alleghenys">Pittsburg Alleghenys</a>, which moved to the league from the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">American Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
