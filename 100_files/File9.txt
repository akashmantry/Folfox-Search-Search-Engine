<p>The <b>60th Primetime Emmy Awards</b> were held on Sunday, September 21, 2008, at the newly opened <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Theater" title="Microsoft Theater">Nokia Theatre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> to honor the best in <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> <a href="/wiki/Prime_time" title="Prime time">prime time</a> television.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The ceremony was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Bergeron" title="Tom Bergeron">Tom Bergeron</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heidi_Klum" title="Heidi Klum">Heidi Klum</a>, <a href="/wiki/Howie_Mandel" title="Howie Mandel">Howie Mandel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Probst" title="Jeff Probst">Jeff Probst</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Seacrest" title="Ryan Seacrest">Ryan Seacrest</a> (all were nominated in the debut category—<a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Host_for_a_Reality_or_Reality-Competition_Program" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Host for a Reality or Reality-Competition Program">Outstanding Host for a Reality or Reality-Competition Program</a>) and televised in the United States on <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">ABC</a>.</p>
<p>The nominations were announced on July 17 by <a href="/wiki/Kristin_Chenoweth" title="Kristin Chenoweth">Kristin Chenoweth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Neil_Patrick_Harris" title="Neil Patrick Harris">Neil Patrick Harris</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Creative_Arts_Emmy_Award" title="Creative Arts Emmy Award">Creative Arts Emmy Awards</a> were held eight days earlier (September 13) at the same venue. The ceremony was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Patrick_Harris" title="Neil Patrick Harris">Neil Patrick Harris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Chalke" title="Sarah Chalke">Sarah Chalke</a>.</p>
<p>The telecast was viewed by 12.20 million with a household rating of 8.86/12.79 making it the lowest rated and least viewed ceremony in its televised history.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Many critics cited lackluster performances from the <a href="#Hosting">five hosts</a> as a reason for the huge decline.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Others pointed to the field of nominees which were dominated by low-rated and sparsely viewed programs, thus making the Emmys widely considered as a bust, which was panned by critics as "... the worst ever, laid a big, fat ratings egg as well ..."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, when <a href="/wiki/Pop_(U.S._TV_network)" title="Pop (U.S. TV network)">TV Guide Network</a> re-did their list of "25 Biggest TV Blunders", this ceremony was included.</p>
<p>For the first time in a decade, the <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Comedy_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series">Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Comedy Series</a> was won by the defending champion. <i><a href="/wiki/30_Rock" title="30 Rock">30 Rock</a></i> was the top show of the night receiving five major awards, including the top prize. It also made history when it tied the record for most major nominations by a comedy series of 13 set by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Cosby_Show" title="The Cosby Show">The Cosby Show</a></i> in <a href="/wiki/38th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="38th Primetime Emmy Awards">1986</a>; it would break this record the next year. It set another record when it received seven nominations in the guest acting categories, a mark that has not been matched since.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Drama_Series" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Drama Series">Outstanding Drama Series</a> went to <a href="/wiki/AMC_(TV_channel)" title="AMC (TV channel)">AMC</a> freshmen series <i><a href="/wiki/Mad_Men" title="Mad Men">Mad Men</a></i>. This marked the first series award for a program on a <a href="/wiki/Cable_television_in_the_United_States#Basic_cable" title="Cable television in the United States">basic cable</a> station. <i>Mad Men</i> led all dramas with seven major nominations.</p>
<p>This would be the final ceremony to have five nominees per category, most major categories (acting and programs) were expanded to include at least six slots the following year.</p>
<p><i>Tin Man</i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s win for makeup made <i><a href="/wiki/Adaptations_of_The_Wizard_of_Oz" title="Adaptations of The Wizard of Oz">The Wizard of Oz</a></i> the third franchise to complete <a href="/wiki/List_of_people_who_have_won_Academy,_Emmy,_Grammy,_and_Tony_Awards" title="List of people who have won Academy, Emmy, Grammy, and Tony Awards">EGOT</a> status.</p>
